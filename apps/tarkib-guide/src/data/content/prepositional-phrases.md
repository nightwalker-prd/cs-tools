# Part 7: Prepositional Phrases (شِبْهُ الْجُمْلَةِ)

A prepositional phrase is made up of a **preposition** followed by a **noun**.

## Structure

| English Example | Arabic Term |
|-----------------|-------------|
| *in* the masjid | شِبْهُ الْجُمْلَةِ |
| *before* the prayer | شِبْهُ الْجُمْلَةِ |

---

## Types of Prepositions

There are **two types** of prepositions in Arabic:

### 1. Noun Prepositions (ظَرْفٌ)

These are prepositions that are themselves **اِسْمٌ** (nouns). They are called **ظَرْفٌ**.

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| خِلَالَ | during, in between |
| خَلْفَ | behind |
| عِنْدَ | by, at the time of, in the eyes of, according to |
| فَوْقَ | above |
| قَبْلَ | before, ago |
| مَعَ | with |
| أَثْنَاءَ | during |
| أَمَامَ | in front of |
| بَعْدَ | after |
| بَيْنَ | between, amongst |
| تَحْتَ | under |
| حَوْلَ | around |

### 2. Particle Prepositions (حَرْفٌ جَارٌّ)

These are prepositions that are **حَرْفٌ** (particles). They are called **حَرْفٌ جَارٌّ**.

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| لِ | for, belongs to |
| حَتَّى | until, even |
| عَنْ | regarding, from |
| كَ | like |
| فِي | in |
| عَلَى | on |
| إِلَى | to, towards, until |
| مِنْ | from |
| بِ | with |

---

## Two Types of شِبْهُ الْجُمْلَةِ

### Type 1: شِبْهُ الْجُمْلَةِ Comprising a ظَرْفٌ

When a شِبْهُ الْجُمْلَةِ uses a noun preposition (ظَرْفٌ):
- The ظَرْفٌ becomes **مُضَافٌ** to the noun that follows
- The مُضَافٌ is usually **مَنْصُوْبٌ** (in the accusative case)

**Example:**
```
أَمَامَ الْمَسْجِدِ
in front of the masjid
```

**Tarkib:**

| الْمَسْجِدِ | أَمَامَ |
|-------------|---------|
| مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ | مُضَافٌ |

### Type 2: شِبْهُ الْجُمْلَةِ Comprising a حَرْفٌ جَارٌّ

When a شِبْهُ الْجُمْلَةِ uses a particle preposition (حَرْفٌ جَارٌّ):
- The حُرُوْفُ جَارَّةٌ are labelled as **حَرْفٌ جَارٌّ** (or simply جَارٌّ)
- The following word becomes **مَجْرُوْرٌ** (in the genitive case)
- The حُرُوْفُ جَارَّةٌ are **مَبْنِيٌّ** (indeclinable); they remain unchanged

**Example:**
```
فِي الْمَسْجِدِ
in the masjid
```

**Tarkib:**

| الْمَسْجِدِ | فِي |
|-------------|-----|
| مَجْرُوْرٌ | جَارٌّ |

## Important Notes on Prepositions

### Pronunciation Changes

Some حُرُوْفُ جَارَّةٌ undergo changes to make pronunciation easier:

1. **مِنْ → مِنَ** if the word after it has an أَلْ
   - Example: مِنَ الْمَسْجِدِ (from the masjid)

2. **عَنْ → عَنِ** if the word after it has an أَلْ
   - Example: عَنِ الدَّرْسِ (about the lesson)

3. **لِ before a word with أَلْ**: the ا is dropped
   - Example: الرَّجُلُ → لِلرَّجُلِ (for the man)

4. **لِ before the word اللهُ**: written as **لِلَّهِ**

5. **فِي followed by a word with أَلْ**: the ي is not pronounced
   - Example: فِي الْبَيْتِ (pronounced "fil-bayti")

6. **The ي at the end of إِلَى and حَتَّى**: pronounced as an أَلِفٌ

### Special Meanings

#### 1. عِنْدَ with Abstract Nouns
The word **عِنْدَ** is translated as "at the time of" if followed by a مَصْدَرٌ or abstract noun:

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| عِنْدَ الْأَكْلِ | at the time of eating |
| عِنْدَ الْمُصِيْبَةِ | at the time of difficulty |

#### 2. ذَاتَ with Time/Place Words
The word **ذَاتَ** can become the مُضَافٌ of words denoting time or place:

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| ذَاتَ يَوْمٍ | one day |
| ذَاتَ لَيْلَةٍ | one night |
| ذَاتَ الْيَمِيْنِ | towards the right |
| ذَاتَ الشِّمَالِ | towards the left |

#### 3. بَيْنَ يَدَيْ (In Front Of)
The words **بَيْنَ يَدَيْ** literally mean "between the two hands" but are translated as **in front of** or **before**:

```
يَجْلِسُ الطَّالِبُ بَيْنَ يَدَيِ الْمُعَلِّمِ
The student sits in front of the teacher.
```

```
يُرْسِلُ اللهُ الرِّيَاحَ بَيْنَ يَدَيِ الْمَطَرِ
Allah sends winds before rain.
```
