# Part 1: التَّرْكِيْبُ الْوَصْفِيُّ (Descriptive Phrases)

## Definition

A **Descriptive Phrase** (التَّرْكِيْبُ الْوَصْفِيُّ) consists of:
- **مَنْعُوْتٌ** (Man'ūt): The noun being described
- **نَعْتٌ** (Na't): The adjective/descriptor

The نَعْتٌ always follows the مَنْعُوْتٌ and must agree with it in four aspects.

## The Four Agreements (المُطَابَقَةُ الرُّبَاعِيَّةُ)

| Agreement | Arabic | Must Match |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| 1. Case | الإِعْرَابُ | Nominative (مَرْفُوْعٌ), Accusative (مَنْصُوْبٌ), Genitive (مَجْرُوْرٌ) |
| 2. Definiteness | التَّعْرِيْفُ وَالتَّنْكِيْرُ | Both definite OR both indefinite |
| 3. Number | الْعَدَدُ | Singular, Dual, or Plural |
| 4. Gender | الْجِنْسُ | Masculine or Feminine |

## Basic Examples

### Indefinite Phrases (نَكِرَةٌ)

| Arabic | مَنْعُوْتٌ | نَعْتٌ | Translation |
|--------|----------|-------|-------------|
| قَلَمٌ قَبِيْحٌ | قَلَمٌ | قَبِيْحٌ | An ugly pen |
| شَيْءٌ حَسَنٌ | شَيْءٌ | حَسَنٌ | A good thing |
| لَيْلَةٌ طَوِيْلَةٌ | لَيْلَةٌ | طَوِيْلَةٌ | A long night |
| عَذَابٌ شَدِيْدٌ | عَذَابٌ | شَدِيْدٌ | A severe punishment |

### Definite Phrases (مَعْرِفَةٌ)

| Arabic | مَنْعُوْتٌ | نَعْتٌ | Translation |
|--------|----------|-------|-------------|
| الْمَكْتَبُ الْكَبِيْرُ | الْمَكْتَبُ | الْكَبِيْرُ | The big desk |
| الْوَلَدُ الْكَاذِبُ | الْوَلَدُ | الْكَاذِبُ | The lying boy |
| الْقَمَرُ الصَّغِيْرُ | الْقَمَرُ | الصَّغِيْرُ | The small moon |
| الْمَسْجِدُ الْكَبِيْرُ | الْمَسْجِدُ | الْكَبِيْرُ | The big mosque |

## Dual Forms (المُثَنَّى)

Both مَنْعُوْتٌ and نَعْتٌ take dual endings:

| Arabic | Translation | Analysis |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| بَيْتَانِ صَغِيْرَانِ | Two small houses | Masculine dual |
| مَسْجِدَانِ بَعِيْدَانِ | Two distant mosques | Masculine dual |
| يَوْمَانِ طَوِيْلَانِ | Two long days | Masculine dual |
| الْبِنْتَانِ الْكَاذِبَتَانِ | The two lying girls | Feminine dual definite |
| الْمَرْأَتَانِ الصَّالِحَتَانِ | The two righteous women | Feminine dual definite |

### Dual Endings Chart

| Gender | Indefinite | Definite |
|--------|------------|----------|
| Masculine | ـَانِ (nominative) / ـَيْنِ (acc/gen) | Same |
| Feminine | ـَتَانِ (nominative) / ـَتَيْنِ (acc/gen) | Same |

## Plural Forms (الْجَمْعُ)

### Sound Masculine Plural (جَمْعُ الْمُذَكَّرِ السَّالِمُ)

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| الرِّجَالُ الْأَقْوِيَاءُ | The strong men |
| الْأَوْلَادُ الضُّعَفَاءُ | The weak children |
| الْمُسْلِمُوْنَ الْمُخْلِصُوْنَ | The sincere Muslims |

### Sound Feminine Plural (جَمْعُ الْمُؤَنَّثِ السَّالِمُ)

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| أَخَوَاتٌ صَادِقَاتٌ | Truthful sisters |
| النِّسَاءُ الصَّالِحَاتُ | The righteous women |
| الْحَاضِرَاتُ الْغَائِبَاتُ | The present and absent women |

## Special Rule: Non-Human Plurals (جَمْعُ غَيْرِ الْعَاقِلِ)

**Important Rule**: Plurals of non-human/non-rational beings are treated grammatically as **feminine singular**.

| Arabic | Translation | Note |
|--------|-------------|------|
| أَيَّامٌ مُبَارَكَةٌ | Blessed days | Not مُبَارَكَاتٌ |
| أَمْوَالٌ كَثِيْرَةٌ | Abundant wealth | Not كَثِيْرَاتٌ |
| الثَّمَرَاتُ الْحُلْوَةُ | The sweet fruits | Feminine singular adjective |
| الْمَسَاجِدُ قَدِيْمَةٌ | The mosques are old | Not قَدِيْمَاتٌ |
| الْجَنَّاتُ جَمِيْلَةٌ | The gardens are beautiful | Feminine singular |

### With Verbs

Non-human plurals also take feminine singular verbs:

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| تُغْفَرُ الذُّنُوْبُ | Sins are forgiven |
| ذَهَبَتْ شُهُوْرٌ كَثِيْرَةٌ | Many months have passed |
| جَاءَتِ الْأَيَّامُ الْمُبَارَكَةُ | The blessed days have come |
| كَانَتِ الْأَنْهَارُ كَبِيْرَةً | The rivers were big |
| لَيْسَتِ الْبُيُوْتُ جَدِيْدَةً | The houses are not new |

## Broken Plurals of Humans (جَمْعُ التَّكْسِيْرِ لِلْعَاقِلِ)

Broken plurals of human beings can take either masculine OR feminine verb forms:

| Both Forms Valid | Translation |
|------------------|-------------|
| حَزِنَ الْآبَاءُ / حَزِنَتِ الْآبَاءُ | The fathers grieved |
| جَاءَ رُسُلٌ / جَاءَتْ رُسُلٌ | Messengers came |
| فَرِحَ الْأُمَّهَاتُ / فَرِحَتِ الْأُمَّهَاتُ | The mothers rejoiced |

## Removing the مَنْعُوْتٌ (Substantivized Adjectives)

When the مَنْعُوْتٌ (described noun) is understood from context, it can be omitted. The نَعْتٌ then takes its place and functions as a noun:

| Arabic | Translation | Implied مَنْعُوْتٌ |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| جَاعَ الْفُقَرَاءُ | The poor became hungry | النَّاسُ/الرِّجَالُ |
| جَاءَ الْبُعَدَاءُ | The distant ones came | النَّاسُ |
| خَسِرَ الظَّالِمُ | The oppressor lost | الرَّجُلُ |
| فَرِحَ الْحَزِيْنُ | The sad one rejoiced | الشَّخْصُ |
| يَصْدُقُ الصُّلَحَاءُ | The righteous speak truth | النَّاسُ |
| أَفْلَحَ الْمُخْلِصُوْنَ | The sincere ones succeeded | النَّاسُ |
| يُنْفِقُ الْأَغْنِيَاءُ الْأَمْوَالَ | The wealthy spend wealth | النَّاسُ |

## نَعْتٌ as مَفْعُوْلٌ مُطْلَقٌ (Cognate Accusative)

The نَعْتٌ can function as a مَفْعُوْلٌ مُطْلَقٌ (cognate accusative) to modify verbs, describing the manner or intensity of the action:

| Arabic | Translation | Function |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| يَشْكُرُ النَّاسُ اللهَ قَلِيْلًا | People thank Allah little | Manner |
| يُحِبُّ النَّاسُ الْمَالَ كَثِيْرًا | People love wealth a lot | Intensity |
| لَقَدْ ضَلَّ الْمُشْرِكُوْنَ بَعِيْدًا | The polytheists strayed far | Extent |
| لَيُعَذِّبَنَّ اللهُ الظَّالِمِيْنَ شَدِيْدًا | Allah will punish oppressors severely | Intensity |
| يَقُوْمُ الْعِبَادُ الصَّالِحُوْنَ طَوِيْلًا | The righteous stand (pray) long | Duration |

## Differentiating Phrase vs. Sentence

### Key Distinction

| Structure | Type | Meaning Complete? |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| مَنْعُوْتٌ + نَعْتٌ | Phrase (تَرْكِيْبٌ) | No |
| مُبْتَدَأٌ + خَبَرٌ | Sentence (جُمْلَةٌ) | Yes |

### Pattern Recognition

| Pattern | Example | Type | Translation |
|---------|---------|------|-------------|
| Indefinite + Indefinite | رَجُلٌ قَوِيٌّ | Phrase | A strong man |
| Definite + Indefinite | الرَّجُلُ قَوِيٌّ | Sentence | The man is strong |
| Definite + Definite | الرَّجُلُ الْقَوِيُّ | Phrase | The strong man |

### More Examples

| Arabic | Type | Translation |
|--------|------|-------------|
| رَبٌّ غَفُوْرٌ | Phrase | A forgiving Lord |
| الرَّبُّ غَفُوْرٌ | Sentence | The Lord is Forgiving |
| الرَّبُّ الْغَفُوْرُ | Phrase | The Forgiving Lord |
| يَوْمَانِ طَوِيْلَانِ | Phrase | Two long days |
| الْيَوْمَانِ طَوِيْلَانِ | Sentence | The two days are long |
| الْيَوْمَانِ الطَّوِيْلَانِ | Phrase | The two long days |

## Converting Sentences to Phrases

To convert a مُبْتَدَأ-خَبَر sentence into a مَنْعُوْتٌ-نَعْتٌ phrase, add الـ to the خَبَر:

| Sentence | Phrase |
|----------|--------|
| النَّافِذَةُ مُغْلَقَةٌ (The window is closed) | النَّافِذَةُ الْمُغْلَقَةُ (The closed window) |
| الشَّابُّ فَاسِقٌ (The young man is sinful) | الشَّابُّ الْفَاسِقُ (The sinful young man) |
| الْكُرْسِيُّ قَبِيْحٌ (The chair is ugly) | الْكُرْسِيُّ الْقَبِيْحُ (The ugly chair) |
| الشَّهْرُ مُبَارَكٌ (The month is blessed) | الشَّهْرُ الْمُبَارَكُ (The blessed month) |
| الْفُقَرَاءُ شَاكِرُوْنَ (The poor are grateful) | الْفُقَرَاءُ الشَّاكِرُوْنَ (The grateful poor) |

## Summary Rules

1. **نَعْتٌ always follows مَنْعُوْتٌ**
2. **Both must match in**: Case, Definiteness, Number, Gender
3. **Non-human plurals**: Treated as feminine singular
4. **Broken plurals of humans**: Can take masculine or feminine verbs
5. **Definiteness pattern**:
   - Indefinite + Indefinite = Phrase
   - Definite + Definite = Phrase
   - Definite + Indefinite = Sentence
