# Summary: الجملة الاسمية (Nominal Sentence)

*Based on Unit 2, Section 1, Part 1 Summary*

---

## Overview: الجملة (Sentence)

This guide covers the three main forms of **الجملة الاسمية** (Nominal Sentence):

1. Basic Nominal Sentence (on its own)
2. With فعل ناقص (Incomplete Verb) - كان and its sisters
3. With حرف مشبه بالفعل (Verb-like Particle) - إنّ and its sisters

---

## 1. الجملة الاسمية on its Own (Basic Form)

The simplest form of a nominal sentence has two components:

| Component | Arabic | English | Definiteness | Case |
|-----------|--------|---------|--------------|------|
| **المبتدأ** | مُبْتَدَأ | Subject | **معرفة** (Definite) | **مرفوع** (Nominative) |
| **الخبر** | خَبَر | Information/Predicate | **نكرة** (Indefinite) | **مرفوع** (Nominative) |

### Agreement Rule
The **مبتدأ** and **خبر** must agree in:
- **الإعراب** (I'rab/Grammatical case)
- **العدد** (Number)
- **الجنس** (Gender)

### Structure Diagram

```
الجملة الاسمية (Basic)
┌──────────────┬──────────────┐
│    مُبْتَدَأ    │     خَبَر     │
├──────────────┼──────────────┤
│   subject    │ information  │
│   معرفة      │    نكرة      │
│   مرفوع      │    مرفوع     │
└──────────────┴──────────────┘
```

## 2. الجملة الاسمية with فعل ناقص (Incomplete Verb)

When **كان** (was) or its sisters enter the sentence, the case of the predicate changes.

### The Incomplete Verbs (أفعال ناقصة)

| Affirmative | Meaning | Negative | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|----------|---------|
| **كان** | was | **ما كان** | was not |
| **يكون** | will be | **لا يكون** | will not be |
| **ليس** | is/are not | **ما** | is/are not |

### Structure with كان / ليس / ما

| Component | Arabic | English | Definiteness | Case |
|-----------|--------|---------|--------------|------|
| **اسم كان / ليس / ما** | Subject | Subject | **معرفة** (Definite) | **مرفوع** (Nominative) |
| **خبر كان / ليس / ما** | Predicate | Information | **نكرة** (Indefinite) | **منصوب** (Accusative) |

### Key Change
> ⚠️ **Important:** The **خبر** changes from **مرفوع** to **منصوب** (accusative)!

### Agreement Rule
The **اسم** and **خبر** of كان must agree in:
- **العدد** (Number)
- **الجنس** (Gender)

### Structure Diagram

```
الجملة الاسمية with فعل ناقص
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              كان / ما كان / يكون / لا يكون / ليس / ما    │
├──────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┤
│    اسم كان / ليس / ما     │    خبر كان / ليس / ما     │
├──────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│        subject           │       information         │
│        معرفة             │          نكرة             │
│        مرفوع             │         منصوب            │
└──────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────┘
```

## 3. الجملة الاسمية with حرف مشبه بالفعل (Verb-like Particle)

When **إنّ** (indeed) or its sisters enter the sentence, both case assignments reverse.

### The Verb-like Particle

| Particle | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **إنّ** | indeed, verily, certainly |

### Structure with إنّ

| Component | Arabic | English | Definiteness | Case |
|-----------|--------|---------|--------------|------|
| **اسم إنّ** | Subject | Subject | **معرفة** (Definite) | **منصوب** (Accusative) |
| **خبر إنّ** | Predicate | Information | **نكرة** (Indefinite) | **مرفوع** (Nominative) |

### Key Change
> ⚠️ **Important:** With إنّ, the pattern **reverses**:
> - The **اسم** (subject) becomes **منصوب** (accusative)
> - The **خبر** (predicate) remains **مرفوع** (nominative)

### Agreement Rule
The **اسم** and **خبر** of إنّ must agree in:
- **العدد** (Number)
- **الجنس** (Gender)

### Structure Diagram

```
الجملة الاسمية with حرف مشبه بالفعل
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                         إنّ                          │
├──────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┤
│        اسم إنّ           │         خبر إنّ           │
├──────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│        subject           │       information         │
│        معرفة             │          نكرة             │
│        منصوب            │         مرفوع             │
└──────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────┘
```

## 4. Emphasis with لام (اللام)

Arabic uses emphatic لام particles to add emphasis to sentences.

### Two Types of Emphatic لام

| Arabic Name | English | Position | Function |
|-------------|---------|----------|----------|
| **لام الابتداء** | Lam of Beginning | On the **مبتدأ** (subject) | Emphasis |
| **اللام المزحلقة** | Sliding Lam | On the **خبر إنّ** (predicate of إنّ) | Emphasis |

### Usage Rules

- **لام الابتداء** is placed at the beginning of a basic nominal sentence, attached to the مبتدأ
- When **إنّ** enters the sentence, the لام cannot stay on the اسم (which was the مبتدأ)
- So the لام "slides" to the خبر and becomes **اللام المزحلقة** (the sliding lam)

### Examples

| Type | Arabic | English |
|------|--------|---------|
| لام الابتداء | لَلطالبُ مجتهدٌ | Surely the student is hardworking |
| اللام المزحلقة | إنّ الطالبَ لَمجتهدٌ | Indeed, the student is surely hardworking |

## Definiteness Pattern (Constant Across All Types)

| Component | Definiteness |
|-----------|--------------|
| Subject (مبتدأ / اسم) | **معرفة** (Definite) |
| Predicate (خبر) | **نكرة** (Indefinite) |
