# التَّرْكِيْبُ (Tarkib) - Arabic Phrase Composition Guide

## Introduction

**Tarkib** (التَّرْكِيْبُ) refers to the science of Arabic phrase composition - how words combine to form meaningful phrases and sentences. This guide covers the three main types of tarkib as taught in classical Arabic grammar.

## Overview of Tarkib Types

Arabic phrases (التَّرَاكِيْبُ) are classified into three main categories:

| Type | Arabic Name | Description |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | التَّرْكِيْبُ الْوَصْفِيُّ | Descriptive Phrase (Adjective + Noun) |
| 2 | التَّرْكِيْبُ الْإِشَارِيُّ | Demonstrative Phrase (Demonstrative + Noun) |
| 3 | التَّرْكِيْبُ الْعَطْفِيُّ | Conjunctive Phrase (Noun + Conjunction + Noun) |

## Key Distinction: Phrase vs. Sentence

A fundamental concept in Arabic grammar is distinguishing between:

- **مُرَكَّبٌ / تَرْكِيْبٌ (Phrase)**: A combination of words that does NOT convey a complete meaning
- **جُمْلَةٌ (Sentence)**: A combination of words that DOES convey a complete meaning

### Examples

| Arabic | Type | Translation |
|--------|------|-------------|
| رَجُلٌ قَوِيٌّ | Phrase (تَرْكِيْبٌ) | A strong man |
| الرَّجُلُ قَوِيٌّ | Sentence (جُمْلَةٌ) | The man is strong |

## Grammatical Terminology

| Arabic Term | Transliteration | Meaning |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| مَنْعُوْتٌ | Man'ūt | The noun being described |
| نَعْتٌ | Na't | The adjective/descriptor |
| اِسْمُ الْإِشَارَةِ | Ism al-Ishārah | Demonstrative pronoun |
| مُشَارٌ إِلَيْهِ | Mushār Ilayh | The noun pointed to |
| مَعْطُوْفٌ عَلَيْهِ | Ma'ṭūf 'Alayh | The first noun (before conjunction) |
| حَرْفُ الْعَطْفِ | Ḥarf al-'Aṭf | Conjunction particle |
| مَعْطُوْفٌ | Ma'ṭūf | The second noun (after conjunction) |
| مُبْتَدَأٌ | Mubtada' | Subject (in nominal sentences) |
| خَبَرٌ | Khabar | Predicate (in nominal sentences) |

## The Agreement Principle

A core principle in Arabic tarkib is **agreement** (المُطَابَقَةُ). Components of a phrase must agree in:

1. **الإِعْرَابُ** (Case) - Nominative, Accusative, Genitive
2. **التَّعْرِيْفُ وَالتَّنْكِيْرُ** (Definiteness) - Definite or Indefinite
3. **الْعَدَدُ** (Number) - Singular, Dual, Plural
4. **الْجِنْسُ** (Gender) - Masculine or Feminine

*Exception: Non-human plurals follow special rules (treated as feminine singular)*
