# Overview of Arabic Pronouns (نَظْرَةٌ عَامَّةٌ عَلَى الضَّمَائِر)

## Complete Summary of Pronouns

Arabic pronouns are classified into three main grammatical states based on their function in the sentence.

## Recognizing Pronouns by Position

Understanding where a pronoun appears helps identify its grammatical function:

| Position | Example | Grammatical Function |
|----------|---------|---------------------|
| **On its own** | أَنَا | مُبْتَدَأ (Subject of nominal sentence) |
| **After the verb** | سَمِعْتُ | فَاعِل (Doer/Subject) |
| **After verb/subject pronoun** | سَمِعْتُهُ | مَفْعُولٌ بِهِ (Direct object) |
| **Joined to إِنَّ** | إِنَّهُ | اِسْمُ إِنَّ (Noun of إنّ) |
| **With إِيَّا** | إِيَّاهُ | مَفْعُولٌ بِهِ، اِسْمُ إِنَّ، مَعْطُوف |
| **Joined to a noun** | رَبُّهُ | مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ (Possessive) |
| **Joined to a حَرْفُ جَارٍّ** | إِلَيْهِ | مَجْرُور (Genitive) |

## Examples in Context

### مُبْتَدَأ (Subject)
> **أَنَا** طَالِبٌ
>
> *I am a student.*

### فَاعِل (Doer)
> سَمِعْ**تُ** الدَّرْسَ
>
> *I heard the lesson.*

### مَفْعُولٌ بِهِ (Object)
> سَمِعْتُ**هُ**
>
> *I heard him.*

### اِسْمُ إِنَّ (Noun of إنّ)
> إِنَّ**هُ** عَالِمٌ
>
> *Indeed, he is a scholar.*

### مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ (Possessive)
> رَبُّ**هُ** كَرِيمٌ
>
> *His Lord is generous.*

### مَجْرُور (After preposition)
> ذَهَبَ إِلَيْ**هِ**
>
> *He went to him.*

## Key Terminology

| English | Arabic | Description |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Pronoun | ضَمِيرٌ ج ضَمَائِر | A word that replaces a noun |
| First person | مُتَكَلِّم | The speaker (I, we) |
| Second person | مُخَاطَب | The addressee (you) |
| Third person | غَائِب | The absent one (he, she, they) |
| Hidden pronoun | ضَمِيرٌ مُسْتَتِر | Implied/hidden in the verb |
| Visible pronoun | ضَمِيرٌ بَارِز | Explicitly visible |
| Attached pronoun | ضَمِيرٌ مُتَّصِل | Connected to another word |
| Detached pronoun | ضَمِيرٌ مُنْفَصِل | Stands alone |
