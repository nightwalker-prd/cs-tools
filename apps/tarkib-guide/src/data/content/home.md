# Complete Guide to Tarkib (التَّرْكِيب) - Arabic Phrase Structure

## Introduction

**Tarkib** (التَّرْكِيب) is the grammatical analysis of Arabic phrases and sentences. It involves identifying and labeling the components of a phrase or sentence according to their grammatical function.

In Arabic, nouns can be joined together to form **phrases** (مُرَكَّبات). There are seven types of phrases in Arabic:

1. **Descriptive Phrases** (المَنْعُوت والنَّعْت)
2. **Demonstrative Phrases** (اسْمُ الإشَارَة والمُشَارُ إِلَيْه)
3. **Conjunction Phrases** (المَعْطُوفُ عَلَيْه والمَعْطُوف)
4. **Appositive Phrases** (المُبْدَلُ مِنْه والبَدَل)
5. **Possessive Phrases** (المُضَاف والمُضَافُ إِلَيْه)
6. **Number Phrases** (العَدَد والمَعْدُود)
7. **Prepositional Phrases** (حَرْفُ الجَرّ والمَجْرُور)

This guide covers the first six types in detail.

---

## Part 1: Descriptive Phrases (التَّرْكِيبُ الوَصْفِي)

A descriptive phrase is made up of an **adjective** (نَعْت) and a **noun** (مَنْعُوت).

### Structure

| English | Arabic |
|---------|--------|
| *small house* | بَيْتٌ صَغِيرٌ |

- The noun being described is called **المَنْعُوت** (man'ut)
- The adjective is called **النَّعْت** (na't)

### Key Rules

#### Rule 1: Word Order
In English, the adjective precedes the noun. In Arabic, however, **the noun (مَنْعُوت) precedes the adjective (نَعْت)**.

| English | Direction | Arabic |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| a believing servant | ← | عَبْدٌ مُؤْمِنٌ |
| Noun + Adjective | | مَنْعُوت + نَعْت |

#### Rule 2: Agreement (DING)
The نَعْت must agree with the مَنْعُوت in **four characteristics**:

| Letter | Characteristic | Arabic |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| **D** | Definite – Indefinite | مَعْرِفَة – نَكِرَة |
| **I** | I'rab (grammatical state) | مَرْفُوع – مَنْصُوب – مَجْرُور |
| **N** | Number | مُفْرَد – مُثَنَّى – جَمْع |
| **G** | Gender | مُذَكَّر – مُؤَنَّث |

### Examples

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| الوَلَدُ الكَاذِبُ | the lying boy |
| شَيْءٌ حَسَنٌ | a beautiful thing |
| لَيْلَةٌ طَوِيلَةٌ | a long night |
| المَكْتَبُ الكَبِيرُ | the big desk |

### Tarkib Notation

In tarkib, the parts of a phrase are labelled under the word in **green ink**:

```
    عَبْدٌ     صَالِحٌ
   مَنْعُوت    نَعْت
```

### Non-Human Plurals

In Arabic, the plurals of non-human nouns (جَمْعُ غَيْرِ العُقَلاء) - things or animals - are treated as **singular and feminine**. This affects the نَعْت:

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| الأَنْهَارُ كَبِيرَةٌ | The rivers are big. |
| جَاءَتْ أَيَّامٌ مُبَارَكَةٌ | Blessed days have come. |

### Broken Plurals of Humans

Broken plurals of humans can be treated as either singular feminine or singular masculine:

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| جَاءَ الرِّجَالُ | The men came. |
| جَاءَتِ الرِّجَالُ | The men came. |

### Differentiating نَعْت and خَبَر

| Structure | Agreement | Example | Translation |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| مَنْعُوت – نَعْت (Phrase) | DING | الوَلَدُ الصَّادِقُ / وَلَدٌ صَادِقٌ | the truthful boy / a truthful boy |
| مُبْتَدَأ – خَبَر (Sentence) | ING | الوَلَدُ صَادِقٌ / الأَوْلَادُ صَادِقُونَ | The boy is truthful. / The boys are truthful. |

**Key difference:** مُبْتَدَأ and خَبَر agree in **three** characteristics (ING), whilst مَنْعُوت and نَعْت agree in **four** (DING).

### Summary Table

| Component | Role | Agreement |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| مَنْعُوت | Noun | Agree in DING (Non-human plurals = feminine singular) |
| نَعْت | Adjective | |

## Part 2: Demonstrative Phrases (التَّرْكِيبُ الإشَارِي)

A demonstrative phrase is made up of a **demonstrative pronoun** followed by a **noun**.

### Structure

| English | Arabic |
|---------|--------|
| *this man* | هٰذَا الرَّجُلُ |
| *that man* | ذٰلِكَ الرَّجُلُ |

- The demonstrative pronoun is called **اسْمُ الإشَارَة**
- The thing being pointed at is called **مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه**

### The Demonstrative Pronouns (أَسْمَاءُ الإشَارَة)

There are twelve demonstrative pronouns:

#### For Close Things (لِلْقَرِيب)

| | Masculine (المُذَكَّر) | Feminine (المُؤَنَّث) |
|---|---|---|
| **Singular** (مُفْرَد) | هٰذَا (This) | هٰذِهِ (This) |
| **Dual** (مُثَنَّى) | هٰذَانِ (These) | هَاتَانِ (These) |
| **Plural** (جَمْع) | هٰؤُلاءِ (These) | هٰؤُلاءِ (These) |

#### For Far Things (لِلْبَعِيد)

| | Masculine (المُذَكَّر) | Feminine (المُؤَنَّث) |
|---|---|---|
| **Singular** (مُفْرَد) | ذٰلِكَ (That) | تِلْكَ (That) |
| **Dual** (مُثَنَّى) | ذَانِكَ (Those) | تَانِكَ (Those) |
| **Plural** (جَمْع) | أُولٰئِكَ (Those) | أُولٰئِكَ (Those) |

### Key Rules

#### Rule 1: No أَلْ Required
The أَسْمَاءُ الإشَارَة are مَعْرِفَة by nature and do not need أَلْ.

#### Rule 2: Most are مَبْنِيّ
Most أَسْمَاءُ الإشَارَة are مَبْنِيّ (indeclinable), except the مُثَنَّى forms which change:
- هٰذَانِ ← هٰذَيْنِ
- ذَانِكَ ← ذَيْنِكَ

#### Rule 3: Demonstrative Comes First
The demonstrative pronoun comes **before** the noun:

```
هٰذَا القُرْآنُ
this Quran
```

#### Rule 4: مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه Must Have أَلْ
The مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه must always have an أَلْ. A name cannot be the مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه.

#### Rule 5: Agreement in DING
The اسْمُ الإشَارَة and its مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه must agree in all four characteristics (DING).

### Tarkib Notation

```
    هٰذَا      القُرْآنُ
 اسْمُ الإشَارَة   مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه
```

### Differentiating مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه and خَبَر

| Structure | Agreement | Example | Translation |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| اسْمُ الإشَارَة – مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه (Phrase) | DING | هٰذَا الوَلَدُ | this boy |
| مُبْتَدَأ – خَبَر (Sentence) | ING | هٰذَا وَلَدٌ | This is a boy. |

**Key difference:** If the word after اسْمُ الإشَارَة has أَلْ, it is مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه. If it doesn't have أَلْ, it becomes خَبَر.

### Summary Table

| Component | Role | Agreement |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| اسْمُ الإشَارَة | Demonstrative Pronoun | Agree in DING |
| مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه | Must have an أَلْ | |

## Part 3: Conjunction Phrases (التَّرْكِيبُ العَطْفِي)

A conjunction phrase comprises two or more words joined with a conjunction.

### Structure

| English | Arabic |
|---------|--------|
| *Muhammad ﷺ and Nooh* | مُحَمَّدٌ وَنُوحٌ |

- A conjunction is called **حَرْفُ عَطْف**
- The word before the حَرْفُ العَطْف is called **مَعْطُوفٌ عَلَيْه**
- The word after the حَرْفُ العَطْف is called **مَعْطُوف**

### Key Rule: Agreement in I'rab

The مَعْطُوف will have the **same إِعْرَاب** as the مَعْطُوفٌ عَلَيْه:

```
مُحَمَّدٌ وَأَحْمَدُ
```

### Tarkib Notation

```
    مُحَمَّدٌ        وَ        أَحْمَدُ
  مَعْطُوفٌ عَلَيْه   حَرْفُ عَطْف    مَعْطُوف
```

### Common Arabic Conjunctions (حُرُوفُ العَطْف)

| Arabic | English | Usage |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **وَ** | and | Both affirmative |
| **فَ** | then (immediate) | Sequence, immediate |
| **ثُمَّ** | then (delayed) | Sequence, with delay |
| **أَوْ** | or | Choice, alternatives |
| **إِمَّا...وَإِمَّا** | either...or | Choice between two |
| **لَا** | not | Negating مَعْطُوف in affirmative sentence |
| **وَلَا** | nor | Negating in negative sentence |
| **بَلْ** | rather, instead | Correction after negative |
| **لٰكِنْ** | rather, but | Correction after negative |

### Conjunction Usage Chart

| Conjunction | مَعْطُوفٌ عَلَيْه | مَعْطُوف | Example |
|-------------|-------------------|----------|---------|
| وَ (and) | Affirmative | Affirmative | جَاءَ زَيْدٌ وَخَالِدٌ |
| فَ (then) | Affirmative | Affirmative | جَاءَ زَيْدٌ فَخَالِدٌ |
| ثُمَّ (then) | Affirmative | Affirmative | جَاءَ زَيْدٌ ثُمَّ خَالِدٌ |
| أَوْ (or) | Affirmative | Affirmative | جَاءَ زَيْدٌ أَوْ خَالِدٌ |
| إِمَّا...وَإِمَّا | Affirmative | Affirmative | جَاءَ إِمَّا زَيْدٌ وَإِمَّا خَالِدٌ |
| لَا (not) | Affirmative | Negative | جَاءَ زَيْدٌ لَا خَالِدٌ |
| وَلَا (nor) | Negative | Negative | مَا جَاءَ زَيْدٌ وَلَا خَالِدٌ |
| لَا...وَلَا | Negative | Negative | زَيْدٌ لَا فَقِيرٌ وَلَا غَنِيٌّ |
| بَلْ (rather) | Negative | Affirmative | مَا جَاءَ زَيْدٌ بَلْ خَالِدٌ |
| لٰكِنْ (rather) | Negative | Affirmative | مَا جَاءَ زَيْدٌ لٰكِنْ خَالِدٌ |

### مَفْعُولٌ مَعَهُ (Object of Accompaniment)

The مَفْعُولٌ مَعَهُ is a noun which occurs after a وَ called وَاوُ المَعِيَّة, and shows the person or thing with which the action took place.

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| جَاءَ زَيْدٌ وَخَالِدًا | Zaid came with Khalid. |

**Key differences from مَعْطُوف:**
1. The مَفْعُولٌ مَعَهُ is always in the مَنْصُوب state
2. The مَفْعُولٌ مَعَهُ indicates simultaneous action with the subject

### Special Cases

#### Multiple مَعْطُوف
A single phrase may have multiple مَعْطُوف. Each مَعْطُوف is preceded by the conjunction وَ:

```
جَاءَ زَيْدٌ وَخَالِدٌ وَأَحْمَدُ
Zaid, Khalid, and Ahmad came.
```

#### Conjunction Between Sentences
A حَرْفُ عَطْف can occur between two sentences:

```
جَاءَ زَيْدٌ وَذَهَبَ عَمْرٌو
Zaid came and Amr went.
```

### Summary Table

| Component | Role | Rule |
|-----------|------|------|
| حَرْفُ عَطْف | Conjunction | وَ، فَ، ثُمَّ، أَوْ، إِمَّا، لَا، وَلَا، بَلْ، لٰكِنْ |
| مَعْطُوفٌ عَلَيْه | Word before conjunction | Agree in I'rab |
| مَعْطُوف | Word after conjunction | |
| مَفْعُولٌ مَعَهُ | Simultaneously with subject | مَنْصُوب |

## Part 4: Appositive Phrases (التَّرْكِيبُ البَدَلِي)

An appositive phrase is made up of two nouns, where the second explains or gives more information regarding the first.

### Structure

| English | Arabic |
|---------|--------|
| *Your brother, Ahmad, is very clever.* | أَخُوكَ أَحْمَدُ ذَكِيٌّ جِدًّا |

- The first word (the one being explained) is called **مُبْدَلٌ مِنْه**
- The second word is called **بَدَل**

### Key Rule: Agreement in I'rab

The بَدَل will have the **same إِعْرَاب** as the مُبْدَلٌ مِنْه:

```
أَرْسَلَ اللهُ النَّبِيَّ مُحَمَّدًا ﷺ
Allah sent the Prophet, Muhammad ﷺ.
```

### Tarkib Notation

```
        النَّبِيَّ      مُحَمَّدًا
       مُبْدَلٌ مِنْه      بَدَل
```

### Examples

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| كَانَ النَّبِيُّ إِبْرَاهِيمُ لَصَادِقٌ | Indeed the Prophet Ibrahim was truthful. |
| صَلَّى الأَخُ أَحْمَدُ جَالِسًا | The brother Ahmad prayed sitting. |

### Summary Table

| Component | Role | Agreement |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| مُبْدَلٌ مِنْه | Noun being explained | Agree in I'rab |
| بَدَل | Noun explaining | |

## Part 5: Possessive Phrases (التَّرْكِيبُ الإضَافِي)

A possessive phrase indicates ownership or a relationship between two nouns.

### Structure

| English | Arabic |
|---------|--------|
| *the man's house* | بَيْتُ الرَّجُلِ |
| *the house of the man* | |

- The first word (the possessed) is called **المُضَاف**
- The second word (the possessor) is called **المُضَافُ إِلَيْه**

### Key Rules

#### Rule 1: No أَلْ or تَنْوِين on المُضَاف
The المُضَاف will not have an أَلْ nor a تَنْوِين.

#### Rule 2: المُضَافُ إِلَيْه is Always مَجْرُور
The المُضَافُ إِلَيْه will always be مَجْرُور.

#### Rule 3: Definiteness Follows المُضَافُ إِلَيْه
- If the المُضَافُ إِلَيْه is مَعْرِفَة, the المُضَاف will also be مَعْرِفَة
- If the المُضَافُ إِلَيْه is نَكِرَة, the المُضَاف will also be نَكِرَة

| Arabic | English | Type |
|--------|---------|------|
| بَيْتُ الرَّجُلِ | the man's house | مَعْرِفَة |
| بَيْتُ رَجُلٍ | a man's house | نَكِرَة |

#### Rule 4: No Agreement in DING
The المُضَاف does not have to agree with المُضَافُ إِلَيْه in any of the four characteristics.

### Tarkib Notation

```
      بَيْتُ       الرَّجُلِ
     المُضَاف     المُضَافُ إِلَيْه
```

### Translation

The possessive phrase is translated using **'s** or **of**:

| Arabic | Translation Options |
|--------|---------------------|
| بَيْتُ الرَّجُلِ | the man's house / house of the man |
| كِتَابُ الأُخْتِ | the sister's book / book of the sister |

### Nested Possessive Phrases

One possessive phrase may have multiple possessive phrases nested within it:

```
بَيْتُ أُمِّ صَدِيقِ حَامِدٍ
Hamid's friend's mother's house
The house of the mother of the friend of Hamid
```

### Special Words with Adjective Meanings

#### ذُو and its sisters
The word ذُو and its forms are used as المُضَاف, with their المُضَافُ إِلَيْه being a noun with a general meaning:

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| ذُو مَالٍ | wealthy (possessor of wealth) |
| ذُو ثَمَرٍ | fruitful (full of fruit) |
| رَجُلٌ ذُو مَالٍ | a wealthy man |

**Forms of ذُو:**

| | مَرْفُوع | مَنْصُوب | مَجْرُور |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Masc. Sing.** | ذُو | ذَا | ذِي |
| **Masc. Dual** | ذَوَا | ذَوَيْ | ذَوَيْ |
| **Masc. Plural** | أُولُو | أُولِي | أُولِي |
| **Fem. Sing.** | ذَاتُ | ذَاتَ | ذَاتِ |
| **Fem. Dual** | ذَوَاتَا | ذَوَاتَيْ | ذَوَاتَيْ |
| **Fem. Plural** | أُولَاتُ | أُولَاتِ | أُولَاتِ |

#### مِثْل (like, similar)
| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| زَيْدٌ رَجُلٌ مِثْلُ خَالِدٍ | Zaid is a man like Khalid. |

#### غَيْر and سِوَى (other than)
| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| جَوَابٌ غَيْرُ صَحِيحٍ | an incorrect answer |
| إِلٰهٌ غَيْرُ اللهِ | a deity other than Allah |
| أَحَدٌ سِوَى اللهِ | anyone other than Allah |

### الأَسْمَاءُ الخَمْسَة (The Five Nouns)

Five two-letter nouns express their i'rab differently when they are مُضَاف:

| Word | Meaning | مَرْفُوع | مَنْصُوب | مَجْرُور |
|------|---------|----------|----------|----------|
| أَب | father | أَبُو | أَبَا | أَبِي |
| أَخ | brother | أَخُو | أَخَا | أَخِي |
| حَم | father-in-law | حَمُو | حَمَا | حَمِي |
| فَم | mouth | فُو | فَا | فِي |
| ذُو | possessor | ذُو | ذَا | ذِي |

### Summary Table

| Component | Role | Rule |
|-----------|------|------|
| المُضَاف | Owned | No أَلْ, no تَنْوِين, no ن of جَمْعُ المُذَكَّرِ السَّالِم or المُثَنَّى |
| المُضَافُ إِلَيْه | Owner | مَجْرُور (Don't agree in DING) |

## Part 6: Number Phrases (التَّرْكِيبُ العَدَدِي)

A phrase with a number is called a number phrase.

### Types of Numbers

1. **Cardinal Number** (عَدَد أَصْلِي): Shows how many - one, two, three
2. **Ordinal Number** (عَدَد تَرْتِيبِي): Shows position - first, second, third

### Cardinal Numbers

A cardinal number phrase comprises:
- A number (عَدَد)
- The item being quantified (مَعْدُود)

### Numbers 1-2 (وَاحِد / اِثْنَان)

The meaning of one and two is understood from مُفْرَد and مُثَنَّى forms. When used for emphasis:

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| رَجُلَانِ اثْنَانِ | two men |
| بِنْتَانِ اثْنَتَانِ | two girls |

**Structure:** مَنْعُوت - نَعْت (Descriptive Phrase)
- Number and مَعْدُود: **Agree** in number
- Gender of عَدَد: **Agrees** with مَعْدُود

### Numbers 3-10

| English | Masculine | Feminine |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| three | ثَلَاثٌ | ثَلَاثَةٌ |
| four | أَرْبَعٌ | أَرْبَعَةٌ |
| five | خَمْسٌ | خَمْسَةٌ |
| six | سِتٌّ | سِتَّةٌ |
| seven | سَبْعٌ | سَبْعَةٌ |
| eight | ثَمَانٍ | ثَمَانِيَةٌ |
| nine | تِسْعٌ | تِسْعَةٌ |
| ten | عَشْرٌ | عَشَرَةٌ |

**Structure:** مُضَاف - مُضَافُ إِلَيْه (Possessive Phrase)

**Key Rules:**
1. The عَدَد becomes مُضَاف to the مَعْدُود
2. The مَعْدُود will be in **plural** form
3. **Chiastic Concord** (تَخَالُف): The numbers have the **opposite gender** to their مَعْدُود

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| ثَلَاثَةُ رِجَالٍ | three men |
| ثَلَاثُ نِسَاءٍ | three women |
| سَبْعَةُ أَبْوَابٍ | seven doors |

### Numbers 11-12

| English | Masculine | Feminine |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| eleven | أَحَدَ عَشَرَ | إِحْدَى عَشْرَةَ |
| twelve | اِثْنَا عَشَرَ / اِثْنَيْ عَشَرَ | اِثْنَتَا عَشْرَةَ / اِثْنَتَيْ عَشْرَةَ |

**Structure:** تَمْيِيز (Specification)

**Rules:**
- Both parts agree in gender with the مَعْدُود
- The مَعْدُود is singular and مَنْصُوب

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| أَحَدَ عَشَرَ كَوْكَبًا | eleven stars |
| إِحْدَى عَشْرَةَ بِنْتًا | eleven girls |

### Numbers 13-19

| English | Masculine | Feminine |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| thirteen | ثَلَاثَةَ عَشَرَ | ثَلَاثَ عَشْرَةَ |
| fourteen | أَرْبَعَةَ عَشَرَ | أَرْبَعَ عَشْرَةَ |
| fifteen | خَمْسَةَ عَشَرَ | خَمْسَ عَشْرَةَ |

**Structure:** تَمْيِيز (Specification)

**Rules:**
- The ten agrees in gender with the مَعْدُود
- The unit has **chiastic concord** with the مَعْدُود
- The مَعْدُود is singular and مَنْصُوب

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| ثَلَاثَةَ عَشَرَ رَجُلًا | thirteen men |
| ثَلَاثَ عَشْرَةَ امْرَأَةً | thirteen women |

### Tens (20, 30, 40, 50, 60, 70, 80, 90)

| English | Arabic |
|---------|--------|
| twenty | عِشْرُونَ |
| thirty | ثَلَاثُونَ |
| forty | أَرْبَعُونَ |
| fifty | خَمْسُونَ |
| sixty | سِتُّونَ |
| seventy | سَبْعُونَ |
| eighty | ثَمَانُونَ |
| ninety | تِسْعُونَ |

**Structure:** تَمْيِيز (Specification)

**Rules:**
- The عَدَد has the same i'rab as جَمْعُ المُذَكَّرِ السَّالِم (عِشْرِينَ in مَجْرُور/مَنْصُوب)
- The مَعْدُود is singular and مَنْصُوب
- Gender is **unchanged**

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| عِشْرُونَ وَلَدًا | twenty boys |
| عِشْرُونَ بِنْتًا | twenty girls |

### Tens and Units (21-99)

In Arabic, the units precede the tens in written form. The حَرْفُ عَطْف وَ joins them.

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| خَمْسَةٌ وَثَلَاثُونَ يَوْمًا | thirty-five days |

**Rules for units:**
- 21, 31, 41... : Unit agrees in gender with مَعْدُود
- 23-29, 33-39... : Unit has chiastic concord with مَعْدُود

### Numbers 100 and 1,000

| English | Arabic |
|---------|--------|
| one hundred | مِائَةٌ (ج مِئَات) |
| one thousand | أَلْفٌ (ج آلَافٌ، أُلُوفٌ) |

**Structure:** مُضَاف - مُضَافُ إِلَيْه (Possessive Phrase)

**Rules:**
- The مَعْدُود is in its **singular** form
- Gender is **unchanged**

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| مِائَةُ عَامٍ | one hundred years |
| أَلْفُ شَهْرٍ | one thousand months |

### Joining Large Numbers

Thousands, hundreds, tens, and units are joined with وَ:

```
3,525
ثَلَاثَةُ آلَافٍ وَخَمْسُ مِائَةٍ وَخَمْسٌ وَعِشْرُونَ
Thousands + Hundreds + Units + Tens
```

## Supplement: Nested Phrases

One phrase may be nested within another.

### Example

```
هٰذَا الوَلَدُ الصَّالِحُ صَادِقٌ
This pious boy is truthful.
```

In this sentence, the demonstrative phrase "this pious boy" contains a descriptive phrase "pious boy" nested within it.

### Tarkib Notation

```
        هٰذَا       الوَلَدُ      الصَّالِحُ      صَادِقٌ
     اسْمُ الإشَارَة                              خَبَر
                    مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه
                    مَنْعُوت       نَعْت
```

### Possessive Phrases with Other Phrases

The مُضَاف and مُضَافُ إِلَيْه cannot be separated. When combined with other phrases, adjustments are made accordingly.

#### Possessive + Descriptive Phrase

The نَعْت of the مُضَافُ إِلَيْه comes after it:

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| كِتَابُ الرَّجُلِ الفَقِيرِ | the poor man's book |
| كِتَابُ الرَّجُلِ الكَبِيرِ | the man's big book |

#### Possessive + Demonstrative Phrase

When an اسْمُ الإشَارَة comes with a possessive phrase, the مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه can be either the مُضَاف or the مُضَافُ إِلَيْه:

| Arabic | English |
|--------|---------|
| كِتَابُ هٰذَا الرَّجُلِ | this man's book |
| كِتَابُ الرَّجُلِ هٰذَا | this book of the man |

### Summary: How Phrases Join Together

| | نَعْت | إِشَارَة | عَطْف | بَدَل | إِضَافَة |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **نَعْت** | | هٰذَا الوَلَدُ الصَّالِحُ | وَلَدٌ صَالِحٌ وَبِنْتٌ صَالِحَةٌ | الوَلَدُ الصَّالِحُ أَحْمَدُ | أُمُّ الوَلَدِ الصَّالِحِ |
| **إِشَارَة** | | | هٰذَا الوَلَدُ وَتِلْكَ البِنْتُ | هٰذَا الوَلَدُ زَيْدٌ | كِتَابُ الوَلَدِ هٰذَا |
| **عَطْف** | | | | الوَلَدَانِ حَامِدٌ وَخَالِدٌ | كِتَابُ الوَلَدِ وَالبِنْتِ |
| **بَدَل** | | | | | مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللهِ |

## Quick Reference: Agreement Rules

| Phrase Type | Agreement Rule |
|-------------|----------------|
| Descriptive (نَعْت) | DING (Definite, I'rab, Number, Gender) |
| Demonstrative (إِشَارَة) | DING |
| Conjunction (عَطْف) | I'rab only |
| Appositive (بَدَل) | I'rab only |
| Possessive (إِضَافَة) | No agreement; مُضَافُ إِلَيْه always مَجْرُور |
| Number (عَدَد) | Varies by number range (see table above) |

## Glossary of Tarkib Terms

| Arabic | English | Definition |
|--------|---------|------------|
| مَنْعُوت | Described noun | The noun being described in a descriptive phrase |
| نَعْت | Adjective | The word describing the مَنْعُوت |
| اسْمُ الإشَارَة | Demonstrative pronoun | This, that, these, those |
| مُشَارٌ إِلَيْه | Pointed-to noun | The noun being pointed at |
| حَرْفُ عَطْف | Conjunction | و، ف، ثم، أو، etc. |
| مَعْطُوفٌ عَلَيْه | Conjoined-upon | Word before the conjunction |
| مَعْطُوف | Conjoined | Word after the conjunction |
| مُبْدَلٌ مِنْه | Substituted-from | The noun being explained |
| بَدَل | Substitute | The explanatory noun |
| مُضَاف | Possessed | The owned item in a possessive phrase |
| مُضَافُ إِلَيْه | Possessor | The owner in a possessive phrase |
| عَدَد | Number | Cardinal or ordinal number |
| مَعْدُود | Counted | The item being counted |
| تَمْيِيز | Specification | The counted noun with numbers 11+ |
| مَفْعُولٌ مَعَهُ | Object of accompaniment | Noun indicating simultaneous action |
