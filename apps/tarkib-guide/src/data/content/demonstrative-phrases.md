# Part 2: التَّرْكِيْبُ الْإِشَارِيُّ (Demonstrative Phrases)

## Definition

A **Demonstrative Phrase** (التَّرْكِيْبُ الْإِشَارِيُّ) consists of:
- **اِسْمُ الْإِشَارَةِ** (Ism al-Ishārah): The demonstrative pronoun
- **مُشَارٌ إِلَيْهِ** (Mushār Ilayh): The noun being pointed to

## Demonstrative Pronouns Chart (أَسْمَاءُ الْإِشَارَةِ)

### Near (القَرِيْبُ) - "This/These"

| Gender | Singular | Dual | Plural |
|--------|----------|------|--------|
| Masculine | هٰذَا | هٰذَانِ / هٰذَيْنِ | هٰؤُلَاءِ |
| Feminine | هٰذِهِ | هَاتَانِ / هَاتَيْنِ | هٰؤُلَاءِ |

### Far (البَعِيْدُ) - "That/Those"

| Gender | Singular | Dual | Plural |
|--------|----------|------|--------|
| Masculine | ذٰلِكَ | ذَانِكَ / ذَيْنِكَ | أُولٰئِكَ |
| Feminine | تِلْكَ | تَانِكَ / تَيْنِكَ | أُولٰئِكَ |

**Note**: The dual forms with ـَانِ are for nominative case; ـَيْنِ are for accusative/genitive.

## Basic Examples

### Near Demonstratives (الْقَرِيْبُ)

| Arabic | اِسْمُ الْإِشَارَةِ | مُشَارٌ إِلَيْهِ | Translation |
|--------|------------------|----------------|-------------|
| هٰذَا الْأُسْتَاذُ | هٰذَا | الْأُسْتَاذُ | This teacher |
| هٰذِهِ الثَّمَرَةُ | هٰذِهِ | الثَّمَرَةُ | This fruit |
| هٰذَا الْكُرْسِيُّ | هٰذَا | الْكُرْسِيُّ | This chair |
| هٰذَا الْأُسْبُوْعُ | هٰذَا | الْأُسْبُوْعُ | This week |
| هٰذِهِ الْأَنْهَارُ | هٰذِهِ | الْأَنْهَارُ | These rivers |

### Far Demonstratives (البَعِيْدُ)

| Arabic | اِسْمُ الْإِشَارَةِ | مُشَارٌ إِلَيْهِ | Translation |
|--------|------------------|----------------|-------------|
| ذٰلِكَ الدِّيْنُ | ذٰلِكَ | الدِّيْنُ | That religion |
| ذٰلِكَ الْخَلِيْلُ | ذٰلِكَ | الْخَلِيْلُ | That close friend |
| ذٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ | ذٰلِكَ | الْكِتَابُ | That book |
| تِلْكَ الرُّسُلُ | تِلْكَ | الرُّسُلُ | Those messengers |
| تِلْكَ السَّاعَةُ | تِلْكَ | السَّاعَةُ | That hour/watch |
| تِلْكَ الْأَبْوَابُ | تِلْكَ | الْأَبْوَابُ | Those doors |

## Key Rule: مُشَارٌ إِلَيْهِ Must Be Definite

The noun following the demonstrative (مُشَارٌ إِلَيْهِ) must ALWAYS have الـ (the definite article):

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| هٰذَا الْكِتَابُ (This book - phrase) | ~~هٰذَا كِتَابٌ~~ (phrase) |
| ذٰلِكَ الرَّجُلُ (That man - phrase) | ~~ذٰلِكَ رَجُلٌ~~ (phrase) |

**Important**: When the noun is indefinite, it becomes a sentence, not a phrase (see below).

## Differentiating Phrase vs. Sentence

### The Pattern

| Structure | Example | Type | Translation |
|-----------|---------|------|-------------|
| Demonstrative + Definite Noun | هٰذِهِ اللَّيْلَةُ | Phrase | This night |
| Demonstrative + Indefinite Noun | هٰذَا بَابٌ | Sentence | This is a door |

### Detailed Examples

| Arabic | Type | Translation | Why? |
|--------|------|-------------|------|
| هٰذَا بَابٌ | Sentence | This is a door | Indefinite noun = خَبَر |
| هٰذِهِ اللَّيْلَةُ | Phrase | This night | Definite noun = مُشَارٌ إِلَيْهِ |
| ذٰلِكَ الْمَالُ | Phrase | That wealth | Definite noun |
| ذٰلِكَ نَهْرٌ | Sentence | That is a river | Indefinite noun |
| تِلْكَ الْمَرْأَةُ | Phrase | That woman | Definite noun |
| هٰؤُلَاءِ أَوْلَادٌ | Sentence | These are children | Indefinite noun |
| ذَانِكَ كِتَابَانِ | Sentence | Those are two books | Indefinite dual |
| هَاتَانِ امْرَأَتَانِ | Sentence | These are two women | Indefinite dual |

### Quranic Examples

| Arabic | Type | Translation |
|--------|------|-------------|
| ﴿مَا هٰذَا إِلَّا بَشَرٌ﴾ | Sentence | This is nothing but a human |
| ﴿إِنْ هٰذَا إِلَّا مَلَكٌ كَرِيْمٌ﴾ | Sentence | This is nothing but a noble angel |

## Complex Sentences with Demonstratives

Demonstrative phrases can appear in various positions within sentences:

### As Subject (فَاعِلٌ)

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| صَلَّى هٰذَا الرَّجُلُ تِلْكَ اللَّيْلَةَ | This man prayed that night |
| سَافَرَتْ هٰذِهِ الْبِنْتُ ذٰلِكَ الْيَوْمَ | This girl traveled that day |
| لَقَدْ صَامَ هٰؤُلَاءِ الْأَوْلَادُ احْتِسَابًا | Indeed, these children fasted seeking reward |

### As Object (مَفْعُوْلٌ بِهِ)

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| شَفَى اللهُ هٰذَا الْمُعَلِّمَ شِفَاءً | Allah cured this teacher completely |
| إِنَّمَا يَنْصُرُ هٰذَا الْفَقِيْرَ ذٰلِكَ الرَّجُلُ | Only that man helps this poor person |
| شَرِبَ هٰذَا الْوَلَدُ ذٰلِكَ اللَّبَنَ جَالِسًا | This boy drank that milk while sitting |

### Multiple Demonstratives in One Sentence

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| اتَّخَذَ أُولٰئِكَ النَّاسُ تِلْكَ الْأَصْنَامَ آلِهَةً | Those people took those idols as gods |
| قَرَأَتْ تِلْكَ الْمُعَلِّمَةُ ذٰلِكَ الْكِتَابَ قَائِمَةً | That teacher (f) read that book while standing |
| نَصَرَتْ هٰذِهِ الْأُمُّ هٰؤُلَاءِ الْبَنَاتِ إِلَّا خَدِيْجَةَ | This mother helped these girls except Khadijah |

### With Time Expressions (ظَرْفُ الزَّمَانِ)

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| مَا غَابَ مُحَمَّدٌ إِلَّا ذَيْنِكَ الْيَوْمَيْنِ | Muhammad was not absent except those two days |

## Case Changes in Demonstratives

The dual demonstratives change based on grammatical case:

### Nominative (مَرْفُوْعٌ)

| Near | Far | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| هٰذَانِ | ذَانِكَ | هٰذَانِ الرَّجُلَانِ جَاءَا (These two men came) |
| هَاتَانِ | تَانِكَ | هَاتَانِ الْبِنْتَانِ جَاءَتَا (These two girls came) |

### Accusative/Genitive (مَنْصُوْبٌ/مَجْرُوْرٌ)

| Near | Far | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| هٰذَيْنِ | ذَيْنِكَ | رَأَيْتُ هٰذَيْنِ الرَّجُلَيْنِ (I saw these two men) |
| هَاتَيْنِ | تَيْنِكَ | مَرَرْتُ بِهَاتَيْنِ الْبِنْتَيْنِ (I passed by these two girls) |

## Summary Rules

1. **اِسْمُ الْإِشَارَةِ precedes مُشَارٌ إِلَيْهِ**
2. **مُشَارٌ إِلَيْهِ must be definite** (with الـ) for it to be a phrase
3. **Demonstrative + Indefinite = Sentence** (not a phrase)
4. **Gender and number agreement** required between demonstrative and noun
5. **Dual demonstratives** change form based on grammatical case
6. **Plural forms** (هٰؤُلَاءِ / أُولٰئِكَ) are the same for both genders
