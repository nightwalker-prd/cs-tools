# Supplementary Topics: Plurals, Cases, and Special Rules

## Plural Types in Arabic

Arabic has multiple plural forms that affect how tarkib (phrase composition) works.

### Overview of Plural Types

| Type | Arabic Name | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Sound Masculine Plural | جَمْعُ الْمُذَكَّرِ السَّالِمُ | Regular masculine plural | مُسْلِمُوْنَ (Muslims) |
| Sound Feminine Plural | جَمْعُ الْمُؤَنَّثِ السَّالِمُ | Regular feminine plural | مُسْلِمَاتٌ (Muslim women) |
| Broken Plural | جَمْعُ التَّكْسِيْرِ | Irregular plural | رِجَالٌ (men), كُتُبٌ (books) |

## Sound Masculine Plural (جَمْعُ الْمُذَكَّرِ السَّالِمُ)

### Formation

| Case | Ending | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Nominative (مَرْفُوْعٌ) | ـُوْنَ | مُسْلِمُوْنَ |
| Accusative (مَنْصُوْبٌ) | ـِيْنَ | مُسْلِمِيْنَ |
| Genitive (مَجْرُوْرٌ) | ـِيْنَ | مُسْلِمِيْنَ |

### Examples in Phrases

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| الْمُسْلِمُوْنَ الْمُخْلِصُوْنَ | The sincere Muslims |
| الْمُجْتَهِدُوْنَ الصَّادِقُوْنَ | The truthful hardworking ones |
| يَتْرُكُ الصَّائِمُوْنَ الطَّعَامَ | The fasting ones leave food |

## Sound Feminine Plural (جَمْعُ الْمُؤَنَّثِ السَّالِمُ)

### Formation

| Case | Ending | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Nominative (مَرْفُوْعٌ) | ـَاتٌ | مُسْلِمَاتٌ |
| Accusative (مَنْصُوْبٌ) | ـَاتٍ | مُسْلِمَاتٍ |
| Genitive (مَجْرُوْرٌ) | ـَاتٍ | مُسْلِمَاتٍ |

### Examples in Phrases

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| أَخَوَاتٌ صَادِقَاتٌ | Truthful sisters |
| النِّسَاءُ الصَّالِحَاتُ | The righteous women |
| اهْتَدَتِ الْمُؤْمِنَاتُ | The believing women were guided |

## Broken Plural (جَمْعُ التَّكْسِيْرِ)

Broken plurals do not follow a regular pattern. The internal structure of the word changes.

### Common Patterns

| Singular | Plural | Pattern |
|----------|--------|---------|
| كِتَابٌ (book) | كُتُبٌ | فُعُلٌ |
| رَجُلٌ (man) | رِجَالٌ | فِعَالٌ |
| بَيْتٌ (house) | بُيُوْتٌ | فُعُوْلٌ |
| يَوْمٌ (day) | أَيَّامٌ | أَفْعَالٌ |
| شَهْرٌ (month) | شُهُوْرٌ | فُعُوْلٌ |
| نَهْرٌ (river) | أَنْهَارٌ | أَفْعَالٌ |

## The Non-Human Plural Rule (جَمْعُ غَيْرِ الْعَاقِلِ)

**Critical Rule**: Plurals of non-human (non-rational) beings are treated grammatically as **feminine singular**.

### Effect on Adjectives

| Plural Noun | Adjective Form | NOT | Translation |
|-------------|----------------|-----|-------------|
| أَيَّامٌ | مُبَارَكَةٌ | ~~مُبَارَكَاتٌ~~ | Blessed days |
| أَمْوَالٌ | كَثِيْرَةٌ | ~~كَثِيْرَاتٌ~~ | Abundant wealth |
| الْمَسَاجِدُ | قَدِيْمَةٌ | ~~قَدِيْمَاتٌ~~ | Old mosques |
| الثَّمَرَاتُ | الْحُلْوَةُ | ~~الْحُلْوَاتُ~~ | The sweet fruits |
| الْجَنَّاتُ | جَمِيْلَةٌ | ~~جَمِيْلَاتٌ~~ | Beautiful gardens |
| السَّنَوَاتُ | طَوِيْلَةٌ | ~~طَوِيْلَاتٌ~~ | Long years |

### Effect on Verbs

Non-human plurals take **feminine singular** verbs:

| Arabic | Translation | Verb Form |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| تُغْفَرُ الذُّنُوْبُ | Sins are forgiven | Feminine singular |
| ذَهَبَتْ شُهُوْرٌ كَثِيْرَةٌ | Many months passed | Feminine singular |
| جَاءَتِ الْأَيَّامُ | The days came | Feminine singular |
| كَانَتِ الْأَنْهَارُ كَبِيْرَةً | The rivers were big | Feminine singular |
| لَيْسَتِ الْبُيُوْتُ جَدِيْدَةً | The houses are not new | Feminine singular |

## Broken Plurals of Humans

**Special Case**: Broken plurals of human beings can take EITHER masculine OR feminine verb forms. Both are grammatically correct.

| Both Valid | Translation |
|------------|-------------|
| حَزِنَ الْآبَاءُ / حَزِنَتِ الْآبَاءُ | The fathers grieved |
| جَاءَ رُسُلٌ / جَاءَتْ رُسُلٌ | Messengers came |
| فَرِحَ الْأُمَّهَاتُ / فَرِحَتِ الْأُمَّهَاتُ | The mothers rejoiced |
| آمَنَ الْمُهَاجِرُوْنَ | The emigrants believed |

### Quranic Evidence

| Quranic Example | Translation |
|-----------------|-------------|
| ﴿قَالَ نِسْوَةٌ﴾ | Women said (masculine verb) |
| ﴿لَقَدْ كُذِّبَتْ رُسُلٌ﴾ | Messengers were denied (feminine verb) |

## Grammatical Cases Summary (الإِعْرَابُ)

### The Three Cases

| Case | Arabic | Markers | Usage |
|------|--------|---------|-------|
| Nominative | مَرْفُوْعٌ | ـُ / ـٌ / ـَانِ / ـُوْنَ | Subject, Predicate |
| Accusative | مَنْصُوْبٌ | ـَ / ـًا / ـَيْنِ / ـِيْنَ | Direct Object |
| Genitive | مَجْرُوْرٌ | ـِ / ـٍ / ـَيْنِ / ـِيْنَ | After Prepositions |

### Case Markers by Number

| Type | Nominative | Accusative | Genitive |
|------|------------|------------|----------|
| Singular | ـُ / ـٌ | ـَ / ـًا | ـِ / ـٍ |
| Dual | ـَانِ | ـَيْنِ | ـَيْنِ |
| Sound Masc. Plural | ـُوْنَ | ـِيْنَ | ـِيْنَ |
| Sound Fem. Plural | ـَاتٌ | ـَاتٍ | ـَاتٍ |

## The Dual Form (المُثَنَّى)

### Dual Endings

| Gender | Nominative | Accusative/Genitive |
|--------|------------|---------------------|
| Masculine | ـَانِ | ـَيْنِ |
| Feminine | ـَتَانِ | ـَتَيْنِ |

### Dual Examples in Descriptive Phrases

| Arabic | Translation | Case |
|--------|-------------|------|
| بَيْتَانِ صَغِيْرَانِ | Two small houses | Nominative |
| بَيْتَيْنِ صَغِيْرَيْنِ | Two small houses | Acc/Gen |
| الْبِنْتَانِ الْكَاذِبَتَانِ | The two lying girls | Nominative |
| الْبِنْتَيْنِ الْكَاذِبَتَيْنِ | The two lying girls | Acc/Gen |

## Definiteness Rules (التَّعْرِيْفُ وَالتَّنْكِيْرُ)

### What Makes a Word Definite?

1. **الـ** (The definite article): الرَّجُلُ (the man)
2. **Proper nouns**: مُحَمَّدٌ (Muhammad)
3. **Pronouns**: هُوَ (he)
4. **Demonstratives**: هٰذَا (this)
5. **Possessive constructions**: كِتَابُ زَيْدٍ (Zayd's book)

### Definiteness in Tarkib

| Phrase Type | Rule | Example |
|-------------|------|---------|
| Descriptive | Both must match | الرَّجُلُ الْقَوِيُّ OR رَجُلٌ قَوِيٌّ |
| Demonstrative | Second must be definite | هٰذَا الْكِتَابُ |
| Conjunctive | Each keeps own definiteness | الْأَبُ وَأُمٌّ (valid but unusual) |

## Quick Reference: Phrase vs. Sentence

### Descriptive

| Pattern | Type | Example | Translation |
|---------|------|---------|-------------|
| Indefinite + Indefinite | Phrase | رَجُلٌ قَوِيٌّ | A strong man |
| Definite + Indefinite | Sentence | الرَّجُلُ قَوِيٌّ | The man is strong |
| Definite + Definite | Phrase | الرَّجُلُ الْقَوِيُّ | The strong man |

### Demonstrative

| Pattern | Type | Example | Translation |
|---------|------|---------|-------------|
| Demonstrative + Definite | Phrase | هٰذَا الْكِتَابُ | This book |
| Demonstrative + Indefinite | Sentence | هٰذَا كِتَابٌ | This is a book |
