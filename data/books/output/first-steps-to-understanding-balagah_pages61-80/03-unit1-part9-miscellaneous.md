# Miscellaneous Rules

## تَجَاهُلُ الْعَارِفِ (Tajaahul al-'Aarif / Feigning Ignorance)

<div dir="rtl">

**تَجَاهُلُ الْعَارِفِ**

</div>

تَجَاهُلُ الْعَارِفِ is a literary device which is used to express a statement as though the speaker is unaware of it, even though he is aware.

> <div dir="rtl">
>
> ﴿وَإِنَّا أَوْ إِيَّاكُمْ لَعَلَى هُدًى أَوْ فِي ضَلَالٍ مُبِينٍ﴾
>
> </div>
>
> *And We or you are either on the right path or in open error.*

---

## التَّغْلِيبُ (At-Taghleeb / Predominance)

<div dir="rtl">

**التَّغْلِيبُ**

</div>

التَّغْلِيبُ is a literary device in which one word is used to represent two meanings.

There are various ways in which تَغْلِيبٌ can occur:

### 1. Using a dual or plural form to represent different singular forms

For example, using الْقَمَرَانِ to represent both الْقَمَرُ and الشَّمْسُ.

### 2. Using one gender to represent both genders

For example, using وَالِدَانِ and أَبَوَانِ to refer to mother and father.

### 3. Using a term typically associated with one group to encompass both

Using عُقَلَاءِ to encompass both عُقَلَاءِ and غَيْرُ عَقَلَاءِ.

> <div dir="rtl">
>
> ﴿الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ﴾
>
> </div>
>
> *Praise belongs to Allah, lord of the worlds.*

The word عَالَمِينَ is a plural usually used to refer to عُقَلَاءُ. However, in this verse it refers to both عُقَلَاءِ and غَيْرُ عَقَلَاءِ.

## أُسْلُوبُ الْحَكِيمِ (Usloob al-Hakeem / The Wise Style)

<div dir="rtl">

**أُسْلُوبُ الْحَكِيمِ**

</div>

أُسْلُوبُ الْحَكِيمِ is a literary device in which a question is not answered directly. Instead, an answer to a more suitable question is given.

> <div dir="rtl">
>
> ﴿يَسْأَلُونَكَ عَنِ الْأَهِلَّةِ قُلْ هِيَ مَوَاقِيتُ لِلنَّاسِ وَالْحَجِّ﴾
>
> </div>
>
> *They ask you regarding the crescent. Say: It they are signs to mark fixed periods of time.*

When the companions inquired about cause of the change in the moon, instead of directly addressing their initial query, Allah responded by providing information that was more pertinent to their concerns.

## تَنْزِيلٌ (Tanzeel / Treating One Category as Another)

<div dir="rtl">

**تَنْزِيلٌ**

</div>

تَنْزِيلٌ is a literary device in which one thing is brought under the ruling of another. For example, speaking to a غَيْرُ عَاقِلٍ as if it were عَاقِلٌ.

> <div dir="rtl">
>
> ﴿فَقَالَ أَلَا تَأْكُلُونَ﴾
>
> </div>
>
> *Ibrahim (peace be upon him) said (to the idols) Do you not eat.*

In this verse, the verb تَأْكُلُونَ is used to address idols. However, without تَنْزِيلٌ, the singular feminine form would normally have been used.
