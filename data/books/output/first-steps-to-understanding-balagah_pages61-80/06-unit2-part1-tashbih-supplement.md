## تَشْبِيهٌ مَقْلُوبٌ (Tashbih Maqloob / Inverted Simile)

<div dir="rtl">

**تَشْبِيهٌ مَقْلُوبٌ**

</div>

The وَجْهُ الشَّبَهِ is typically a quality that is greater, more familiar, or more well-known within the مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ.

However, the مُشَبَّهٌ and مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ can be swapped to suggest that the original مُشَبَّهٌ possesses the quality to a greater extent than the original مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ. This is known as an inverted simile or تَشْبِيهٌ مَقْلُوبٌ.

> <div dir="rtl">
>
> ﴿قَالُوا إِنَّمَا الْبَيْعُ مِثْلُ الرِّبَوا﴾
>
> </div>
>
> *Trade is just like usury.*

The disbelievers, to justify their practice of usury, claimed that usury is just like a normal transaction. The verse would have read: "Usury is just like trade". However, the مُشَبَّهٌ and مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ are swapped giving the impression that trade should be compared to usury, not the other way around.

#### Analysis of the Standard vs. Inverted Simile:

| | مُشَبَّهٌ | أَدَاةُ التَّشْبِيهِ | مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ | وَجْهُ الشَّبَهِ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **التَّشْبِيهُ الْأَصْلِيُّ** (Standard) | الرِّبَوا | مِثْلُ | الْبَيْعُ | فِي الْحِلِّ |
| **التَّشْبِيهُ الْمَقْلُوبُ** (Inverted) | الْبَيْعُ | مِثْلُ | الرِّبَوا | -- |

## تَشْبِيهٌ ضِمْنِيٌّ (Tashbih Dimni / Implicit Simile)

<div dir="rtl">

**تَشْبِيهٌ ضِمْنِيٌّ**

</div>

The elements of a تَشْبِيهٌ may be mentioned over multiple sentences instead of a single structure.

> <div dir="rtl">
>
> فَــإِنْ تَفُــقِ الْأَنَـــامَ وَأَنْـــتَ بِـــنْهُمْ &emsp;&emsp;&emsp; فَـــإِنَّ الْمِسْـــكَ بَعْـــضُ دَمِ الْغَـــزَالِ
>
> </div>
>
> *If you excel all the creation whilst you remain one of them,*
> *Then musk is also blood of a deer.*

This can be broken down as follows:

| مُشَبَّهٌ | أَدَاةُ التَّشْبِيهِ | مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ | وَجْهُ الشَّبَهِ |
|---|---|---|---|
| أَنْتَ | مِثْلُ | الْمِسْكِ | *(implicit -- excelling despite humble origin)* |
