# Unit 1: علم المعاني (Ilm al-Ma'ani) -- Part 8 (Continued)
# إِيجَازٌ (Ijaaz / Conciseness) -- حَذْفُ اقْتِصَارٍ (Conciseness by Omission)

> Pages 61-62

---

This section continues the discussion of إِيجَازٌ (conciseness) from earlier pages, covering the specific subtypes of حَذْفٌ (omission).

---

## حَذْفُ اقْتِصَارٍ (Hadhf Iqtisaar)

<div dir="rtl">

**حَذْفُ اقْتِصَارٍ**

</div>

حَذْفُ اقْتِصَارٍ is to construct a sentence or paragraph in such a way that the addressee can deduce hidden meaning even though no word, words, phrases or sentences have been removed or hidden.

Below are a few examples.

---

### تَضْمِينٌ (Tadmeen)

<div dir="rtl">

**تَضْمِينٌ**

</div>

تَضْمِينٌ is mentioning a verb with a مَفْعُولٌ بِهِ غَيْرُ صَرِيحٍ of another verb to indicate towards its meaning.

> <div dir="rtl">
>
> دَعْ مَا يَرِيبُكَ إِلَى مَا لَا يَرِيبُكَ
>
> </div>
>
> *Leave what causes you doubt and turn to what does not cause you doubt.*

This sentence encompasses the meaning of the verb اِذْهَبْ. This is understood from the phrase إِلَى مَا لَا يَرِيبُكَ.

| | Verb | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **فِعْلٌ ١** | دَعْ | مَا يَرِيبُكَ |
| **فِعْلٌ ٢** | اِذْهَبْ | إِلَى مَا لَا يَرِيبُكَ |

---

### إِكْتِفَاءٌ (Iktifaa')

<div dir="rtl">

**إِكْتِفَاءٌ**

</div>

إِكْتِفَاءٌ is to omit one of two things commonly associated relying on the listener to understand the second.

> <div dir="rtl">
>
> ﴿بِيَدِكَ الْخَيْرُ إِنَّكَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ﴾
>
> </div>
>
> *In your hand is all goodness. Indeed, you are capable of everything.*

The word خَيْرٌ is commonly mentioned with شَرٌّ. However, in this verse, out of respect, the word شَرٌّ is not mentioned.

| **Implied Word** | **Mentioned Word** |
|---|---|
| الشَّرُّ | الْخَيْرُ |

---

### اِحْتِبَاكٌ (Ihtibaak)

<div dir="rtl">

**اِحْتِبَاكٌ**

</div>

اِحْتِبَاكٌ is mentioning two words explicitly, whilst relying on the context to understand their opposite or related.

> <div dir="rtl">
>
> ﴿قُلْ إِنِّي لَا أَمْلِكُ لَكُمْ ضَرًّا وَلَا رَشَدًا﴾
>
> </div>
>
> *Say: I do not have control of harm or guidance.*

In this verse, the word ضَرًّا (harm) is mentioned along with رَشَدًا (guidance). The opposite of each can be understood: نَفْعًا (benefit) and ضَلَالًا (misguidance).

| **Implied Word** | **Mentioned Word** |
|---|---|
| نَفْعًا | ضَرًّا |
| ضَلَالًا | رَشَدًا |

## Summary (p. 62)

The following diagram summarizes the complete structure of إِيجَازٌ:

```
                        إِيجَازٌ
                       /         \
                   حَذْفٌ        قَصْرٌ
                  /       \
           اِخْتِصَارٌ    اقْتِصَارٌ
            /    \           /    |     \
    حَذْفُ   حَذْفُ    تَضْمِينٌ  إِكْتِفَاءٌ  اِحْتِبَاكٌ
    كَلِمَةٍ  جُمْلَةٍ
```

### إِيجَازٌ (Ijaaz) Complete Breakdown:

| Category | Subcategory | Types |
|---|---|---|
| **إِيجَازٌ** | | |
| | **قَصْرٌ** | *(conciseness in expression)* |
| | **حَذْفٌ** | |
| | | **اِخْتِصَارٌ** (Abbreviation): حَذْفُ كَلِمَةٍ (omission of a word), حَذْفُ جُمْلَةٍ (omission of a sentence) |
| | | **اقْتِصَارٌ** (Sufficiency): تَضْمِينٌ, إِكْتِفَاءٌ, اِحْتِبَاكٌ |
