# Part 3 (cont.) -- Type 6: نِدَاءٌ (Nida' - Calling)

**Unit 1: علم المعاني (Ilm al-Ma'ani)**
**Pages: 30-32**

---

## Primary Usage of نِدَاءٌ

نِدَاءٌ is to call someone. There are two particles of نِدَاءٌ:

### 1) أَيْ or أَ

These are used to call someone who is close.

### 2) يَا

This is used to call someone who is far.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿يَٓا آدَمُ﴾

</div>

*O Adam!*

---

## Inverted Uses of the Particles of نِدَاءٌ

Someone who is close may be called via يَا to show abstract distance.

Similarly, someone far may be called via أَ to show abstract proximity.

For example, when Musa (peace be upon him) held the beard of his brother Harun (peace be upon him) and reprimanded him for letting the Children of Israel worship the cow, Harun replied by saying:

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿قَالَ يٰبْنَؤُمَّ مَا مَنَعَكَ اِذْ رَأَيْتَهُمْ ضَلُّوْا ۞ أَلَّا تَتَّبِعَنِ أَفَعَصَيْتَ أَمْرِيْ ۞ قَالَ يَبْنَؤُمَّ لَا تَأْخُذْ بِلِحْيَتِيْ وَلَا
> بِرَأْسِيْ ۚ إِنِّيْ خَشِيْتُ أَنْ تَقُوْلَ فَرَّقْتَ بَيْنَ بَنِيْٓ إِسْرَائِيْلَ وَلَمْ تَرْقُبْ قَوْلِيْ﴾

</div>

*Moses said, "O Aaron! What prevented you, when you saw them going astray, from following me? How could you disobey my orders?" He replied: O son of my mother, do not hold me by my beard, nor by my head.*

Even though they were physically close, the word يَا is used to show the abstract distance between them.

---

## Secondary Uses of نِدَاءٌ

Sometimes نِدَاءٌ is used for other than calling. Some of these are explained below.

### إِغْرَاءٌ (Ighra' - Urging/Inciting)

A نِدَاءٌ can be used for إِغْرَاءٌ, urging or inciting someone.

After the miraculous birth of Isa (peace be upon him), Maryam (peace be upon her) brought him to the people. Assuming he was an illegitimate child, their initial reaction was of shock and amazement.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿يٰٓاُخْتَ هٰرُوْنَ مَا كَانَ اَبُوْكِ امْرَأَ سَوْءٍ وَّمَا كَانَتْ اُمُّكِ بَغِيًّا﴾

</div>

*O sister of Harun, neither your father was a man of evil nor was your mother unchaste.*

They addressed her as sister of Harun, to reprimand her that being from such a noble family it was not befitting of her to carry out such an act (according to their assumption).

### مِزَاحٌ and تَلَطُّفٌ (Mizah and Talattuf - Jesting and Playing)

A نِدَاءٌ can be used for مِزَاحٌ and تَلَطُّفٌ, jesting and playing.

Once Ali (may Allah be pleased with him) stormed out of his house after he had an argument with his wife, Fatima (may Allah be pleased with her). When the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) came to know of their argument, he set out looking for Ali (may Allah be pleased with him) and found him sleeping on the ground of the masjid covered in dust. To lighten the mood, the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) addressed him saying:

<div dir="rtl">

> يَا أَبَا تُرَابٍ

</div>

*O Abu Turab (one covered in dust)*

### اِسْتِهْزَاءٌ (Istihza' - Mocking)

A نِدَاءٌ can be used for اِسْتِهْزَاءٌ, mocking.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿وَقَالُوْا يَٓاَيُّهَا الَّذِيْ نُزِّلَ عَلَيْهِ الذِّكْرُ إِنَّكَ لَمَجْنُوْنٌ﴾

</div>

*They say, "O you to whom the Dhikr [the Qur'an] has been revealed, you are surely insane.*

The people of Makkah did not believe that revelation came to the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him). Hence, this statement of theirs was sarcastic.
