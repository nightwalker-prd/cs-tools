# Part 3 (cont.) -- Secondary Usages of الأَمْرُ and النَّهْيُ

**Unit 1: علم المعاني (Ilm al-Ma'ani)**
**Pages: 22-24**

---

Sometimes الأَمْرُ and النَّهْيُ are used for other than issuing a command or prohibition. Some of these are explained below.

---

## إِرْشَادٌ (Irshad - Advice)

An أَمْرٌ can be used for إِرْشَادٌ, giving advice or a recommendation.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿يَٓاَيُّهَا الَّذِيْنَ اٰمَنُوْٓا اِذَا تَدَايَنْتُمْ بِدَيْنٍ اِلٰٓى اَجَلٍ مُسَمًّى فَاكْتُبُوْهُ﴾

</div>

*O you who believe, when you transact a debt payable at a specified time, put it in writing.*

---

## إِبَاحَةٌ (Ibahah - Permission)

An أَمْرٌ can be used for إِبَاحَةٌ, showing that something is permissible.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿يَٓاَيُّهَا النَّاسُ كُلُوْا مِمَّا فِي الْأَرْضِ حَلٰلًا طَيِّبًا﴾

</div>

*O people, eat permissible good things out of what lies in the earth.*

---

## اِلْتِمَاسٌ (Iltimas - Request from a Peer or Superior)

An أَمْرٌ can be used for اِلْتِمَاسٌ, making a request from a peer or someone superior.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿قَالَ ابْنَ أُمَّ إِنَّ الْقَوْمَ اسْتَضْعَفُونِي وَكَادُوا يَقْتُلُونَنِي
> فَلَا تُشْمِتْ بِيَ الْأَعْدَاءَ وَلَا تَجْعَلْنِي مَعَ الْقَوْمِ الظّٰلِمِينَ﴾

</div>

*He [Harun] said [to Musa], "My mother's son, the people took me as weak and were about to kill me. So do not let the enemies laugh at me, and do not count me with the wrong-doers."*

---

## دُعَاءٌ (Du'a' - Prayer)

An أَمْرٌ can be used for دُعَاءٌ, making a prayer to Allah.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿رَبَّنَا تَقَبَّلْ مِنَّا﴾

</div>

*"Our Lord, accept from us!"*

---

## تَمَنٍّ (Tamanni - Wishing for the Impossible)

An أَمْرٌ can be used for تَمَنٍّ, wishing for something impossible or improbable.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿وَنَادَوْا يٰمٰلِكُ لِيَقْضِ عَلَيْنَا رَبُّكَ﴾

</div>

*"O Malik [warden of the Hell], let your Lord make an end of us."*

---

## تَسْوِيَةٌ (Taswiyah - Equalization)

An أَمْرٌ and نَهْيٌ can be used for تَسْوِيَةٌ, showing that two actions are equal.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿اِصْلَوْهَا فَاصْبِرُوْٓا اَوْ لَا تَصْبِرُوْا سَوَآءٌ عَلَيْكُمْ﴾

</div>

*Whether you endure [it] patiently or impatiently, it is all the same for you.*

---
