## Order within A Sentence

A جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ and جُمْلَةٌ اِسْمِيَّةٌ both have their regular grammatical structures which can be adjusted to create emphasis or focus.

---

## Alteration of Word Order in a جُمْلَةٌ اِسْمِيَّةٌ

In a جُمْلَةٌ اِسْمِيَّةٌ the regular word order is as follows:

<div dir="rtl">

> مُبْتَدَأٌ - خَبَرٌ

</div>

Sometimes, this order is not observed due to a grammatical necessity. This will not affect the Balagah of the sentence. For example, if the نَكِرَةٌ is مُبْتَدَأٌ and the شِبْهُ الْجُمْلَةِ is خَبَرٌ, it is grammatically necessary to forward the خَبَرٌ.

<div dir="rtl">

> فِي الْبَيْتِ رَجُلٌ

</div>

If there is no grammatical obligation to bring one word first, either can be fronted. However, the point being emphasised will be brought first.

### Example: مَعْرِفَة as both مُبْتَدَأٌ and خَبَرٌ

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿اللّٰهُ رَبُّنَا وَرَبُّكُمْ﴾

</div>

*Allah is our Lord and your Lord.*

In this verse, because both words are مَعْرِفَة, grammatically either can be brought first. However, the topic of discussion has been chosen to be the مُبْتَدَأٌ.

### Example: خَبَرٌ brought before مُبْتَدَأٌ for emphasis

Sometimes the خَبَرٌ can be brought before the مُبْتَدَأٌ to give emphasis.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿لَكُمْ دِيْنُكُمْ وَلِيَ دِيْنِ﴾

</div>

*For you is your faith, and for me, my faith.*

---

## Alteration of Word Order in a جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ

In a جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ the regular word order is as follows:

<div dir="rtl">

> فِعْلٌ - فَاعِلٌ - مَفْعُوْلٌ بِهِ

</div>

Sometimes the regular word order can be altered for various reasons. Some of these are explained below.

### تَأْكِيدٌ (Ta'kid - Emphasis)

A word can be fronted for تَأْكِيدٌ, emphasis.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ﴾

</div>

*You alone do we worship*

In this verse, the مَفْعُوْلٌ بِهِ is fronted to create emphasis. This is reflected in the word **alone**.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿وَهُمْ يَعْلَمُوْنَ﴾

</div>

*whilst they know*

In this verse, the فَاعِلٌ, now the مُبْتَدَأٌ, is fronted to create emphasis.

### سَجَعٌ (Saj' - Rhyming Prose)

A word can be fronted for سَجَعٌ, keeping the rhythm of a sentence.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿خُذُوْهُ فَغُلُّوْهُ ۞ ثُمَّ الْجَحِيمَ صَلُّوْهُ ۞ ثُمَّ فِيْ سِلْسِلَةٍ ذَرْعُهَا سَبْعُونَ ذِرَاعًا فَاسْلُكُوْهُ﴾

</div>

*Seize him, then put a collar around his neck, and then, let him burn in the blazing fire. Thereafter, fasten him with a chain the measure of which is seventy hands.*

In the last two verses, the مَفْعُوْلٌ has been fronted to keep the pattern of verses ending in ه.

## Alteration of Word Order Within Parts of a Sentence

If a part of a sentence is made up of two or more words joined via a حَرْفُ عَطْفٍ, one word must be brought before the other. Below are some reasons for fronting one over the other.

### تَرْتِيبٌ حَقِيقِيٌّ (Tartib Haqiqi - Natural Order)

A word can be fronted because of تَرْتِيبٌ حَقِيقِيٌّ, the natural order.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ﴾

</div>

*Neither dozing overtakes Him nor sleep.*

In this verse, سِنَةٌ, dozing, is mentioned before نَوْمٌ, sleep. The written order is in line with the natural order.

### تَرْتِيبٌ رُتَبِيٌّ (Tartib Rutabi - Hierarchy)

A word can be fronted because of تَرْتِيبٌ رُتَبِيٌّ, hierarchy.

<div dir="rtl">

> وَاِذْ يَرْفَعُ اِبْرٰهِمُ الْقَوَاعِدَ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ وَاِسْمٰعِيلُ

</div>

*When Ibrahim was raising up the foundations of the House, along with Isma'il.*

In this verse the name of Ismail (peace be upon him) is mentioned after Ibrahim (peace be upon him) and delayed to even after the مَفْعُوْلٌ بِهِ. This is to depict the hierarchy between father and son and pointing out Ibrahim (peace be upon him) as the main builder and Ismail (peace be upon him) as an assistant.

### تَعْجِيلُ الْمَسَرَّةِ (Ta'jil al-Masarrah - Bringing Good News First)

A word can be fronted because of تَعْجِيلُ الْمَسَرَّةِ, bringing good news first.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿إِذْ قَالَ اللّٰهُ يٰعِيسٰٓى إِنِّيْ مُتَوَفِّيكَ وَرَافِعُكَ إِلَيَّ وَمُطَهِّرُكَ مِنَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا﴾

</div>

*O 'Isa! I am to take you in full and to raise you towards Myself.*

When Isa (peace be upon him) was surrounded by those who wanted to kill him, Allah reassured him that he would not be killed. The sentence "I am going to take you in full" i.e. elevate you with body and soul, is mentioned first as this was the most prevailing issue.

## Order within Sentences

In a paragraph comprised of multiple sentences, one sentence must be brought before the other. Below is one reason for fronting one sentence over another.

### تَعْجِيلُ الْمَسَرَّةِ (Ta'jil al-Masarrah - Bringing Good News First)

A word can be fronted because of تَعْجِيلُ الْمَسَرَّةِ, bringing good news first.

<div dir="rtl">

> ﴿عَفَا اللّٰهُ عَنْكَ لِمَ أَذِنْتَ لَهُمْ﴾

</div>

*(O Prophet,) Allah has forgiven you; why did you permit them (to stay in Madinah)*

The sentence mentioning the Prophet's pardon is mentioned even before mentioning the error. This was done to avoid him worrying about Allah's displeasure.
