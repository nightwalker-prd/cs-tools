## Categorisation 3: Extension (Page 82)

In an اِسْتِعَارَةٌ, something may be mentioned which matches either the مُشَبَّهٌ or مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ. This is called a مُلَائِمٌ.

> *The lion entered the debate hall, and with one roar, he caused the opponents to accept defeat.*

In this sentence, the word **lion** is an اِسْتِعَارَةٌ used to refer to a brave man. The word **roar** is a مُلَائِمٌ of the مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ.

### Diagram: Elements in the Example

| مُلَائِمُ الْمُشَبَّهِ بِهِ | وَجْهُ الشَّبَهِ | مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ | أَدَاةُ التَّشْبِيهِ | مُشَبَّهٌ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| roar | -- | lion | -- | brave man |

According to the مُلَائِمٌ mentioned in an اِسْتِعَارَةٌ, there are two types of اِسْتِعَارَةٌ:

1. اِسْتِعَارَةٌ مُطْلَقَةٌ
2. اِسْتِعَارَةٌ مُرَشَّحَةٌ

---

### اِسْتِعَارَةٌ مُطْلَقَةٌ (Isti'arah Mutlaqah)

This is an اِسْتِعَارَةٌ in which no مُلَائِمٌ is mentioned.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿الٓر كِتٰبٌ اَنْزَلْنٰهُ اِلَيْكَ لِتُخْرِجَ النَّاسَ مِنَ الظُّلُمٰتِ اِلَى النُّوْرِ﴾

</div>

---

### اِسْتِعَارَةٌ مُرَشَّحَةٌ (Isti'arah Murashshahah)

An اِسْتِعَارَةٌ مُرَشَّحَةٌ is an اِسْتِعَارَةٌ in which a مُلَائِمٌ of the مُشَبَّهٌ بِهِ is mentioned.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿اُولٰئِكَ الَّذِيْنَ اشْتَرَوُا الضَّلَالَةَ بِالْهُدٰى فَمَا رَبِحَتْ تِجَارَتُهُمْ﴾

</div>

> *Those are the people who **purchased** misguidance in exchange for guidance. And **their transaction was not profitable**.*

The word اشْتَرَوْا is an اِسْتِعَارَةٌ used to refer to **exchange**. Later in the verse, the word رَبِحَتْ is mentioned, which is a مُلَائِمٌ of the مَشَبَّهٌ بِهِ.

---

## الاِسْتِعَارَةُ التَّمْثِيلِيَّةُ (Al-Isti'arah al-Tamthiliyyah) (Page 83)

An اِسْتِعَارَةٌ تَمْثِيلِيَّةٌ is a type of figurative language that uses a comparison to convey a message. For instance, the phrase "You are opening a can of worms" is used metaphorically to imply irreversible consequences.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿مَا مِنْ دَآبَّةٍ اِلَّا هُوَ اٰخِذٌ بِنَاصِيَتِهَا﴾

</div>

> *There is no creature, but **He holds it by the forelock**.*

This verse explains that Allah has complete control over every creature.

---

### Note (Page 83)

An اِسْتِعَارَةٌ مُفْرَدَةٌ is the use of a single word in its metaphorical meaning, whilst the remaining remain in their literal meaning.

An اِسْتِعَارَةٌ تَمْثِيلِيَّةٌ is the use of an entire sentence in its metaphorical meaning.
