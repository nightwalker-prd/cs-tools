## Part 3: الكِنَايَة (Al-Kinayah)

<div dir="rtl">

الكِنَايَة

</div>

A الكِنَايَةٌ, allegory, is a figure of speech in which the literal meaning of a sentence is not intended, even though it could be interpreted literally.

For example, the sentence "I don't have blood on my hands" is an allegory used to convey innocence, and it is not intended to be taken literally.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿قَالَتْ اَنّٰى يَكُوْنُ لِيْ غُلٰمٌ وَّلَمْ يَمْسَسْنِيْ بَشَرٌ وَّلَمْ اَكُ بَغِيًّا ۞﴾

</div>

> *Maryam said, "How shall I have a son while no human has ever **touched me**?"*

In this verse the word "touched" is being used as an allegory to convey the meaning of cohabitation, rather than a literal touch.
