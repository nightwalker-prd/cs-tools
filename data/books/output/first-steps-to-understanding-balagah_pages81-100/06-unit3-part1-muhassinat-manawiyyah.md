# Unit 3: عِلْمُ الْبَدِيْعِ (Ilm al-Badi') — Part 1: مُحَسَّنَاتٌ مَعْنَوِيَّة (Muhassinat Ma'nawiyyah)

*Pages 91-93*

---

## Part 1: مُحَسَّنَاتٌ مَعْنَوِيَّة (Muhassinat Ma'nawiyyah — Embellishments Related to Meaning)

---

## 1. الطِّبَاقُ (Al-Tibaq — Antithesis) (Page 91)

<div dir="rtl">

الطِّبَاقُ

</div>

الطِّبَاقُ is a literary device which involves bringing together opposites. This occurs in various forms.

### الطِّبَاقُ with Nouns

طِبَاقٌ can take place with nouns.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿هُوَ الْأَوَّلُ وَالْأٰخِرُ وَالظَّاهِرُ وَالْبَاطِنُ وَهُوَ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيْمٌ ۞﴾

</div>

> *He is the **First** and the **Last**, and the **Manifest** and the **Hidden**.*

### الطِّبَاقُ with Verbs

طِبَاقٌ can take place with verbs.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿وَاَنَّهُ هُوَ اَضْحَكَ وَاَبْكٰى ۞ وَاَنَّهُ هُوَ اَمَاتَ وَاَحْيَا ۞﴾

</div>

> *... He is the One who makes **laugh** and makes **weep**, and that He is the One who gives **death** and gives **life**.*

### الطِّبَاقُ with a Mixture of Nouns and Verbs

طِبَاقٌ can take place with a mixture of nouns and verbs.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿وَمَنْ يُّضْلِلِ اللّٰهُ فَمَا لَهُ مِنْ هَادٍ ۞﴾

</div>

> *The one whom Allah **deprives of guidance**, there is no one to **guide**.*

---

## 2. مُرَاعَاةُ النَّظِيْرِ (Mura'at al-Nadhir — Grouping of Similar Items) (Page 91)

<div dir="rtl">

مُرَاعَاةُ النَّظِيْرِ

</div>

مُرَاعَاةُ النَّظِيْرِ is a literary device which involves bringing similar items or elements together.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿اَلشَّمْسُ وَالْقَمَرُ بِحُسْبَانٍ ۞ وَالنَّجْمُ وَالشَّجَرُ يَسْجُدَانِ ۞﴾

</div>

> *The **sun** and the **moon** are [bound] by a [fixed] calculation, and **shrubs** and **trees** prostrate.*

---

## 3. الْمُشَاكَلَةُ (Al-Mushakalah — Conformity) (Page 92)

<div dir="rtl">

الْمُشَاكَلَةُ

</div>

الْمُشَاكَلَةُ is a literary device which involves expressing a meaning using a word for which it has not been coined. This occurs when two words come in proximity.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿فَاعْتَدُوْا عَلَيْهِ بِمِثْلِ مَا اعْتَدٰى عَلَيْكُمْ ﴾

</div>

> *You may **transgress** against another like they have **transgressed** against you,*

The term اِعْتَدٰى means to transgress, exceed, or go beyond limits. This verse suggests that if someone were to transgress and harm another, the victim has the right to seek justice. The word اعتدى is used twice, first in its original meaning and secondly in the meaning of taking retribution.

---

## 4. التَّوْرِيَةُ (Al-Tawriyah — Double Entendre) (Page 92)

<div dir="rtl">

التَّوْرِيَةُ

</div>

التَّوْرِيَةُ is a literary device which involves using a sentence that can have two different meanings, where the listener understands one meaning while the speaker intends another.

**Historical Example:**

When Abu Bakr رضي الله عنه was asked about the person accompanying him during the migration from Makkah to Madinah, instead of saying Prophet Muhammad صلى الله عليه وسلم, he would reply:

<div dir="rtl">

هٰذَا الرَّجُلُ يَهْدِيْنِي السَّبِيْلَ

</div>

> *This man is **showing me the path**.*

The listener would assume he meant a guide who was showing him the path to Madinah whilst he meant the one who was showing him the path to good. By doing this not only did he conceal the identity of the Prophet Muhammad صلى الله عليه وسلم, but he also avoided lying.

## 5. تَأْكِيْدُ الذَّمِّ بِمَا يُشْبِهُ الْمَدْحَ (Ta'kid al-Dhamm bi ma Yushbih al-Madh — Emphasizing Criticism with What Resembles a Compliment) (Page 93)

<div dir="rtl">

تَأْكِيْدُ الذَّمِّ بِمَا يُشْبِهُ الْمَدْحَ

</div>

تَأْكِيْدُ الذَّمِّ بِمَا يُشْبِهُ الْمَدْحَ is a literary device which involves emphasizing a criticism in a way that makes it sound like a compliment.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿فَلَا صَدَّقَ وَلَا صَلّٰى ۞ وَلٰكِنْ كَذَّبَ وَتَوَلّٰى ۞﴾

</div>

> *So [the denier of the Hereafter] neither believed, nor prayed, **but rejected the truth and turned away [from it]**.*

In this verse, the word **but** and the following sentence is used to create the expectation that the following sentence will mention something positive after the initial criticism, but instead another negative trait is mentioned, implying that everything about the person being described is inherently evil.

---

## 6. تَأْكِيْدُ الْمَدْحِ بِمَا يُشْبِهُ الذَّمَ (Ta'kid al-Madh bi ma Yushbih al-Dhamm — Emphasizing Praise with What Resembles Criticism) (Page 93)

<div dir="rtl">

تَأْكِيْدُ الْمَدْحِ بِمَا يُشْبِهُ الذَّمَ

</div>

تَأْكِيْدُ الْمَدْحِ بِمَا يُشْبِهُ الذَّمَ is a literary device which involves emphasizing a praise in a way that makes it sound like a criticism.

**Quranic Example:**

<div dir="rtl">

﴿لَا يَسْمَعُوْنَ فِيْهَا لَغْوًا وَّلَا تَأْثِيْمًا ۞ اِلَّا قِيْلًا سَلٰمًا سَلٰمًا ۞﴾

</div>

> *They will hear neither an absurd talk in heaven, nor something leading to sin, **but [they will hear] the words of Salam, Salam [as greetings]**.*

In this verse, the word **but** and the following sentence is used to create the expectation that the following sentence will mention something negative after the initial praise, but instead another positive trait is mentioned, implying that everything about Heaven is good.
