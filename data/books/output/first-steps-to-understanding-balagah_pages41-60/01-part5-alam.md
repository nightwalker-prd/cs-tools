# Part 5 (cont.) -- عَلَمٌ ('Alam - Proper Name)

**Unit 1: علم المعاني (Ilm al-Ma'ani)**
**Page: 41**

---

## Definition

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

عَلَمٌ

</div>

An عَلَمٌ, a name, is used to refer to someone or something.

### Primary Example

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿مُحَمَّدٌ رَسُوْلُ اللهِ﴾

</div>

> ***Muhammad** is the messenger of Allah.*

---

## Secondary Usage of عَلَمٌ

However, names can be used to give additional implications if that name carries a special or unique meaning which fits the context.

For example, the name Abu Lahab literally means father of the flame. He was called this because he was very fair skinned with redness in his skin colour. His name is mentioned in the Quran where it fits the punishment he will receive.

### Quranic Example

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿تَبَّتْ يَدَآ أَبِي لَهَبٍ وَتَبَّ ۞ مَآ أَغْنَىٰ عَنْهُ مَالُهُ وَمَا كَسَبَ ۞ سَيَصْلَىٰ نَارًا ذَاتَ لَهَبٍ ۞﴾

</div>

> *May the two hands of Abu Lahab (the father of the **flame**) perish, and may he perish! ... He will soon enter a Fire, full of **flames**.*
