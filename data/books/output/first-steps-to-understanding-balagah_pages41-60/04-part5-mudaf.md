# Part 5 (cont.) -- مُضَافٌ (Mudaf - Possessive Construction)

**Unit 1: علم المعاني (Ilm al-Ma'ani)**
**Page: 45**

---

## Definition

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

مُضَافٌ

</div>

A مُضَافٌ is used to express ownership or attribution.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

رَسُوْلُ اللهِ

</div>

> *the prophet of Allah*

---

## Secondary Uses of مُضَافٌ

Sometimes a مُضَافٌ is used for a reason besides showing ownership. Some of these are explained below.

---

### تَعْظِيْمٌ (Ta'dhim - Showing Greatness)

A مُضَافٌ can be brought to show تَعْظِيْمٌ, greatness.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَاذْكُرْ عَبْدَنَا دَاوُدَ ذَا الْأَيْدِ إِنَّهُ أَوَّابٌ ۞﴾

</div>

> *And remember **Our servant** Dawud (peace be upon him).*

By attributing the word servant to Allah, it shows the lofty status of Dawud (peace be upon him).

---

### تَحْقِيْرٌ (Tahqir - Showing Insignificance)

A مُضَافٌ can be brought to show تَحْقِيْرٌ, insignificance.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

لُعِنَ عَبْدُ الدِّيْنَارِ وَلُعِنَ عَبْدُ الدِّرْهَمِ

</div>

> ***The slave of the dinar** is cursed, and **the slave of the dirham** is cursed.*

By attributing the word slave to the dinar and dirham, it shows the lowly status of the servant.
