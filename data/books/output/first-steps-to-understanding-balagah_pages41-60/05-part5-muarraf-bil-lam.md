# Part 5 (cont.) -- مُعَرَّفٌ بِاللَّامِ (Mu'arraf bil-Lam - Definite with Al)

**Unit 1: علم المعاني (Ilm al-Ma'ani)**
**Pages: 46-48**

---

## Definition

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

مُعَرَّفٌ بِاللَّامِ

</div>

A noun can be made مَعْرِفَةٌ by prefixing an ال. However, there are three types of ال, each conveying a unique meaning.

---

## 1. اَلْ الْعَهْدِيَّةُ (Al al-'Ahdiyyah)

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

اَلْ الْعَهْدِيَّةُ

</div>

عَهْدٌ means acquaintance, familiarity or knowledge.

A noun with an اَلْ الْعَهْدِيَّةُ implies that the addressee is familiar with that noun. There are three types of اَلْ الْعَهْدِيَّةُ.

---

### عَهْدٌ ذِكْرِيٌّ ('Ahd Dhikri)

If a word is used as نَكِرَةٌ and then repeated as مَعْرِفَةٌ, the عَهْدٌ will be known as عَهْدٌ ذِكْرِيٌّ.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿إِنَّآ أَرْسَلْنَا إِلَيْكُمْ رَسُوْلًا شَاهِدًا عَلَيْكُمْ كَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَآ إِلَىٰ فِرْعَوْنَ رَسُوْلًا ۞ فَعَصَىٰ فِرْعَوْنُ الرَّسُوْلَ فَأَخَذْنٰهُ أَخْذًا وَبِيْلًا ۞﴾

</div>

> *We sent **a messenger** to Fir'awn, Then, Fir'awn disobeyed **the messenger**.*

---

### عَهْدٌ حُضُوْرِيٌّ ('Ahd Huduri)

If the thing being spoken about is present, the عَهْدٌ will be known as عَهْدٌ حُضُوْرِيٌّ.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿الْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِيْنَكُمْ﴾

</div>

> ***Today**, I have completed your religion for you.*

---

### عَهْدٌ ذِهْنِيٌّ ('Ahd Dhihni)

If the addressee is familiar with the word even it has not been mentioned previously, the عَهْدٌ will be known as عَهْدٌ ذِهْنِيٌّ.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿فَمَنْ حَجَّ الْبَيْتَ أَوِ اعْتَمَرَ فَلَا جُنَاحَ عَلَيْهِ أَنْ يَطَّوَّفَ بِهِمَا﴾

</div>

> *So whoever comes to **the House** for Hajj or performs 'Umrah.*

Prior to this verse, the word بَيْت is not mentioned. However, it is known that الْبَيْتِ refers to the Ka'bah.

---

## 2. اَلْ الْجِنْسِيَّةُ (Al al-Jinsiyyah)

*(Page 47)*

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

اَلْ الْجِنْسِيَّةُ

</div>

جِنْسٌ means species or type.

A noun with an اَلْ الْجِنْسِيَّةُ implies the speaker is referring to the type, not a single specified member of the type.

A word with an اَلْ الْجِنْسِيَّةُ can be used in two ways.

### 1. General Meaning or Concept

The word can refer to a general meaning or concept.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَجَعَلْنَا مِنَ الْمَآءِ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ حَيٍّ﴾

</div>

> *We created from **water** every living thing.*

In this verse, water is being referred to in the general sense, not any individual type of water.

### 2. Unspecified Member of the Type

The word can refer an unspecified member of the type.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَأَخَافُ أَنْ يَأْكُلَهُ الذِّئْبُ وَأَنْتُمْ عَنْهُ غٰفِلُوْنَ ۞﴾

</div>

> *I fear that **a wolf** may eat him up*

The word الذِّئْبُ has an اَلْ الْجِنْسِيَّةُ and it is not referring to a specific wolf, rather any wolf. Therefore, it is translated as **a wolf**.

This type of word will be مَعْرِفَةٌ grammatically, but نَكِرَةٌ in meaning.

---

## 3. اَلْ الْاِسْتِغْرَاقِيَّةُ (Al al-Istighraqiyyah)

*(Page 48)*

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

اَلْ الْاِسْتِغْرَاقِيَّةُ

</div>

اِسْتِغْرَاقٌ means to encompass.

A noun with an اَلْ الْاِسْتِغْرَاقِيَّةُ implies the speaker is referring to every member of the type, not a single member.

A word with an اَلْ الْاِسْتِغْرَاقِيَّةُ can be used in two ways.

---

### اِسْتِغْرَاقٌ حَقِيْقِيٌّ (Istighraaq Haqiqi - True Encompassing)

اِسْتِغْرَاقٌ حَقِيْقِيٌّ means the word is referring to every single member.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَالْعَصْرِ ۞ إِنَّ الْإِنْسَانَ لَفِيْ خُسْرٍ ۞﴾

</div>

> ***Man** is in a state of loss indeed, except those who believed and did righteous deeds*

The word الْإِنْسَانُ refers to every member of mankind.

---

### اِسْتِغْرَاقٌ عُرْفِيٌّ (Istighraaq 'Urfi - Customary Encompassing)

اِسْتِغْرَاقٌ عُرْفِيٌّ means the word is not referring to every single member absolutely, but rather only those members which are relative to the topic of conversation.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَجَآءَ السَّحَرَةُ فِرْعَوْنَ﴾

</div>

> ***The sorcerers** came to Pharaoh.*

السَّحَرَةُ does not refer to every single member of the type, rather, it is limited to the sorcerers of Firawn's kingdom.
