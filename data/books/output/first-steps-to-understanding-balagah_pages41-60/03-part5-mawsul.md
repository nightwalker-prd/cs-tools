# Part 5 (cont.) -- مَوْصُوْلٌ (Mawsul - Relative Pronoun)

**Unit 1: علم المعاني (Ilm al-Ma'ani)**
**Pages: 43-44**

---

## Definition

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

مَوْصُوْلٌ

</div>

An اسْمُ مَوْصُوْلٍ is primarily used when the name of the person or thing being discussed is not known, nor can it be pointed to.

For example, if someone does not know a person's name nor is he close enough to point at, the speaker would have to describe him as follows: "I saw that man in the shop **who we met at the masjid**."

Sometimes it can used for other reasons, a few of which are mentioned below.

---

## Secondary Usages of مَوْصُوْلٌ

### تَعْلِيْلٌ (Ta'lil - Giving the Reason)

A مَوْصُوْلٌ can be used for تَعْلِيْلٌ, pointing out the reason for the ruling.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَقَالَ رَبُّكُمُ ادْعُوْنِيْ أَسْتَجِبْ لَكُمْ إِنَّ الَّذِيْنَ يَسْتَكْبِرُوْنَ عَنْ عِبَادَتِيْ سَيَدْخُلُوْنَ جَهَنَّمَ دَاخِرِيْنَ ۞﴾

</div>

> ***Those who show arrogance against worshipping Me** shall enter Hell in disgrace*

In this verse, a مَوْصُوْلٌ is used instead of a noun. This is to show the reason for them entering the Hellfire.

---

### تَنْبِيْهٌ عَلَى الْخَطَإِ (Tanbih 'alal-Khata' - Pointing Out an Error)

A مَوْصُوْلٌ can be used for تَنْبِيْهٌ عَلَى الْخَطَإِ, pointing out an error.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿إِنَّ الَّذِيْنَ تَدْعُوْنَ مِنْ دُوْنِ اللهِ عِبَادٌ أَمْثَالُكُمْ فَادْعُوْهُمْ فَلْيَسْتَجِيْبُوْا لَكُمْ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صٰدِقِيْنَ ۞﴾

</div>

> ***Those whom you invoke beside Allah** are certainly slaves (of Allah) like you.*

In this verse, a مَوْصُوْلٌ is used instead of a noun. This is to show the error in considering the idols to be gods.

---

### تَعْظِيْمٌ (Ta'dhim - Showing Grandeur)

*(Page 44)*

A مَوْصُوْلٌ can be used for تَعْظِيْمٌ, showing grandeur.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿فَأَتْبَعَهُمْ فِرْعَوْنُ بِجُنُوْدِهِ فَغَشِيَهُمْ مِنَ الْيَمِّ مَا غَشِيَهُمْ ۞﴾

</div>

> *So, they were encircled by **that** [huge wave] from the sea **which overwhelmed them**.*

---

### تَحْقِيْرٌ (Tahqir - Showing Insignificance)

A مَوْصُوْلٌ can be used for تَحْقِيْرٌ, showing insignificance.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿فَاقْضِ مَآ أَنْتَ قَاضٍ﴾

</div>

> *So, decide **whatever** you must decide.*

---

### تَقْرِيْرُ الْغَرَضِ (Taqrir al-Gharad - Emphasising the Point)

A مَوْصُوْلٌ can be used for تَقْرِيْرُ الْغَرَضِ, emphasising the point being discussed.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَرَاوَدَتْهُ الَّتِيْ هُوَ فِيْ بَيْتِهَا عَنْ نَفْسِهِ﴾

</div>

> *And **she, in whose house he was**, seduced him towards herself.*

In this verse, a مَوْصُوْلٌ is used instead of a noun. This is to highlight how Yusuf (peace be upon him) kept himself away from sin and avoided the temptation to do evil despite residing in her house and being well acquainted with her.
