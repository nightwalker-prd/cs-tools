# Part 5 (cont.) -- نَكِرَةٌ (Nakirah - Indefinite Noun)

**Unit 1: علم المعاني (Ilm al-Ma'ani)**
**Pages: 49-50**

---

## Definition

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

نَكِرَةٌ

</div>

A noun is brought as نَكِرَةٌ when the speaker or addressee is not familiar or acquainted with the person or thing being discussed, or when there is no benefit in mentioning it as معرفة.

### Primary Example

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَجَآءَ مِنْ أَقْصَا الْمَدِيْنَةِ رَجُلٌ يَسْعَىٰ﴾

</div>

> *And there came **a man** running from the farthest part of the city.*

In this verse, the main purpose is to show that one person came to them and warned them regarding mistreating the messengers. Hence, there was no need to mention the معرفة as رجل.

---

## Secondary Usage of نَكِرَةٌ Words

Sometimes a word may be brought as نَكِرَةٌ for secondary reasons. Some of these are explained below.

---

### إِخْفَاءُ الْأَمْرِ (Ikhfa' al-Amr - Concealing Identity)

To conceal the identity of someone.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿فَقَالَتْ هَلْ أَدُلُّكُمْ عَلَىٰ أَهْلِ بَيْتٍ يَكْفُلُوْنَهُ لَكُمْ وَهُمْ لَهُ نٰصِحُوْنَ ۞﴾

</div>

> *Shall I point out to you **a family** who will nurse him for you?*

This sister of Musa (peace be upon him) did not wish to reveal the identity of her mother as that would have jeopardised Musa's (peace be upon him) safety; hence she kept it as نكرة.

---

### تَنْوِيْعٌ (Tanwi' - Keeping Unspecific)

The speaker may wish to keep something unspecific so the addressees may choose as they wish.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿اقْتُلُوْا يُوْسُفَ أَوِ اطْرَحُوْهُ أَرْضًا يَخْلُ لَكُمْ وَجْهُ أَبِيْكُمْ﴾

</div>

> *Throw him at some place on **earth**.*

---

### تَكْثِيْرٌ (Takthir - Showing Variety/Multitude)

*(Page 50)*

To show variety, i.e. a plural نكرة word in a sentence can show the meaning of كثير without actually having to add the صفة of كثير.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَإِنْ يُكَذِّبُوْكَ فَقَدْ كُذِّبَتْ رُسُلٌ مِنْ قَبْلِكَ﴾

</div>

> *Indeed, **many** messengers have been rejected before you.*

In this verse and the verses before, Allah is consoling the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him). There would be greater solace if many Prophets (peace be upon them) had been through the same ordeal as the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him). Therefore, the word many has been added into the translation.

---

### تَعْظِيْمٌ (Ta'dhim - Showing Greatness)

To convey the meaning of عظيم (great).

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿يَآأَيُّهَا الَّذِيْنَ اٰمَنُوا اتَّقُوا اللهَ وَذَرُوْا مَا بَقِيَ مِنَ الرِّبَوَاْ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ مُؤْمِنِيْنَ ۞ فَإِنْ لَّمْ تَفْعَلُوْا فَأْذَنُوْا بِحَرْبٍ مِنَ اللهِ وَرَسُوْلِهِ﴾

</div>

> *But if you do not [give it up], then listen to the declaration of **war** from Allah and His Messenger.*

In this verse, a warning is being issued. The context shows that war here is in the meaning of حرب عظيمة.

---

### تَحْقِيْرٌ (Tahqir - Showing Insignificance)

To convey the meaning of حقير (insignificant).

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَمَا الْحَيٰوةُ الدُّنْيَا فِي الْاٰخِرَةِ إِلَّا مَتَاعٌ ۞﴾

</div>

> *This life of the world in comparison to the hereafter is only a [**momentary**] **benefit**.*

In this verse, the world is being showed as paltry. Hence متاع here in its نكرة form means متاع حقير.
