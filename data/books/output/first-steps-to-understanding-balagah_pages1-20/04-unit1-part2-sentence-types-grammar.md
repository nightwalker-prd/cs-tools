# Unit 1, Part 2: Types of Sentences According to Grammar

*(Pages 11–18)*

A sentence can be analysed in two ways:

1. According to its grammar (Part 2)
2. According to its meaning (Part 3)

## Types of Sentences According to Grammar

According to its grammar, there are two types of sentences:

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

1) جُمْلَةٌ اسْمِيَّةٌ
2) جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ

</div>

In the study of Nahw, when a sentence starts with a noun, it is classified as a جُمْلَةٌ اسْمِيَّةٌ regardless of the content of its خَبَرٌ.

However, in Balagah, if a sentence begins with a noun and contains a فِعْلٌ in its خَبَرٌ, it will still be categorized as a جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ. This is because the focus of Balagah is on the meaning of the sentence, and the presence of a فِعْلٌ anywhere in the sentence gives the meaning of the verb.

### Comparison Table: Nahw vs. Balagah Classification

| Sentence | زَيْدٌ صَائِمٌ | زَيْنَبُ تَصُوْمُ | تَصُوْمُ زَيْنَبُ |
|----------|:--------------:|:------------------:|:------------------:|
| عِلْمُ النَّحْوِ | اسْمِيَّةٌ | اسْمِيَّةٌ | فِعْلِيَّةٌ |
| عِلْمُ الْبَلَاغَةِ | اسْمِيَّةٌ | فِعْلِيَّةٌ | فِعْلِيَّةٌ |

## جُمْلَةٌ اسْمِيَّةٌ (Nominal Sentence)

*(Page 12)*

A جُمْلَةٌ اسْمِيَّةٌ represents a **connection** between the مُبْتَدَأً and خَبَرٌ, indicating that the مُبْتَدَأً possesses the characteristic described in the خَبَرٌ. For example, in the sentence الدَّرْسُ سَهْلٌ, we observe a relationship between the مُبْتَدَأً and خَبَرٌ i.e., the lesson being easy.

### Tense of the Connections

The connections between the مُبْتَدَأً and خَبَرٌ can occur in any of the three tenses: past, present, or future.

#### Present Tense

The connection between the مُبْتَدَأً and خَبَرٌ can indicate a present tense link. This is the default tense of the جُمْلَةٌ اسْمِيَّةٌ.

This type of connection is called ثُبُوْتٌ.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

الرَّجُلُ قَائِمٌ

</div>

> *The man is standing.*

> **Note:** Sometimes, the present tense can depict a long-term meaning. This is called دَوَامٌ.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ رَبِّ الْعَلَمِيْنَ ۞﴾

</div>

> *All praise is for Allah, Lord of the worlds.*

#### Past Tense

*(Page 12)*

The connection between the مُبْتَدَأً and خَبَرٌ can also indicate a past tense link.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿إِنَّ إِبْرٰهِيْمَ كَانَ أُمَّةً قَانِتًا لِلّٰهِ حَنِيْفًا﴾

</div>

> *Surely, Ibrahim **was** an Ummah.*

This type of connection is called ثُبُوْتٌ فِي الْمَاضِيْ.

#### Future Tense

*(Page 13)*

The connection between the مُبْتَدَأً and خَبَرٌ can also indicate a future tense link.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿إِنَّ الْمُتَّقِيْنَ فِيْ جَنّٰتٍ وَعُيُوْنٍ ۞﴾

</div>

> *Surely, the God-Fearing will be in gardens and springs.*

This type of connection is called ثُبُوْتٌ فِي الْمُسْتَقْبِلِ.

---

### Note on كَانَ with جُمْلَةٌ اسْمِيَّةٌ

*(Page 13)*

The meaning of a جُمْلَةٌ اسْمِيَّةٌ with كَانَ as its verb defaults to a link that existed in the past and has since ended. However, كَانَ can also indicate a link that is ongoing, as demonstrated in the following scenarios:

#### a) When the subject is the word Allah.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَكَانَ اللهُ عَلِيْمًا حَكِيْمًا﴾

</div>

> *Allah is **ever** Knowing, Wise.*

Here, the verb كَانَ implies that Allah has always possessed these qualities and will continue to possess them for eternity.

#### b) When the subject possesses a quality inherently.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَكَانَ الْإِنْسَانُ عَجُوْلًا﴾

</div>

> *Man is **prone** to be hasty.*

In this case, the verb كَانَ indicates that man is predisposed to this particular trait.

#### c) When the sentence shows a certain future event. This is known as the Prophetic perfect tense.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿إِنَّ الْبَاطِلَ كَانَ زَهُوْقًا﴾

</div>

> *Surely, falsehood is **ever bound** to vanish (by its very nature).*

Here, the verb كَانَ implies that the vanishing of falsehood is inevitable and bound to happen in the future.

### Types of Connections

*(Page 14)*

The connections between the مُبْتَدَأً and خَبَرٌ can be of two types:

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

1. ثُبُوْتٌ (stative)
2. دَوَامٌ (continuous)

</div>

#### ثُبُوْتٌ (stative)

This refers to a connection without any reference to its duration.

#### دَوَامٌ (continuous)

This refers to the establishment of a long-lasting connection.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ رَبِّ الْعَلَمِيْنَ ۞﴾

</div>

> *All praise is for Allah, Lord of the worlds.*

In this verse, the connection between مُبْتَدَأً and خَبَرٌ extends over a long period. This means that the quality of Allah being the Lord of the worlds is eternal.

## جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ (Verbal Sentence)

*(Page 15)*

A جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ shows the occurrence of an action.

There are two types of occurrences:

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

1. حُدُوْثٌ (Single Occurrence)
2. تَجَدُّدٌ (Repeated Occurrence)

</div>

### Single Occurrence (حُدُوْثٌ)

حُدُوْثٌ is a single occurrence either in the past or future.

#### Simple Past Tense

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَقَتَلَ دَاوُدُ جَالُوْتَ﴾

</div>

> *And Dawud (David) **killed** Golliath.*

#### Simple Future Tense

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿ثُمَّ يَأْتِيْ مِنْ بَعْدِ ذٰلِكَ سَبْعٌ شِدَادٌ﴾

</div>

> *Then after that **will** come seven hard years.*

### Repeated Occurrence (تَجَدُّدٌ)

تَجَدُّدٌ is a repeated occurrence either in the past, present, or future.

#### Past Habitual Tense

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿كَانَا يَأْكُلَانِ الطَّعَامَ﴾

</div>

> *They **used to eat** food.*

#### Present Habitual

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَاللهُ يَرْزُقُ مَنْ يَشَآءُ بِغَيْرِ حِسَابٍ ۞﴾

</div>

> *Allah **gives** whom He wills without counting.*

#### Future Habitual

*(Page 16)*

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَلَكُمْ فِيْهَا مَا تَشْتَهِيْٓ أَنْفُسُكُمْ وَلَكُمْ فِيْهَا مَا تَدَّعُوْنَ ۞﴾

</div>

> *And for you therein are whatever your souls **desire**, and for you therein is whatever you **request**.*

---

### Example: Hypocrites in the Quran

*(Page 16)*

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَإِذَا لَقُوا الَّذِيْنَ اٰمَنُوْا قَالُوْا اٰمَنَّا وَإِذَا خَلَوْا إِلَىٰ شَيَاطِيْنِهِمْ قَالُوْا إِنَّا مَعَكُمْ إِنَّمَا نَحْنُ مُسْتَهْزِءُوْنَ﴾

</div>

> *When they [the hypocrites] meet those who believe, they say, "**We have entered Faith**;" but when they are alone with their satans, they say, "**Indeed, we are with you**."*

This verse contains two quotes from the hypocrites.

The first sentence is a جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ (اٰمَنَّا). When the hypocrites proclaimed their faith to the believers, it is hypocritical i.e. there is no ثُبُوْتٌ. Hence, their words are expressed using a جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ which depicts حُدُوْثٌ.

The second sentence is جُمْلَةٌ اسْمِيَّةٌ (إِنَّا مَعَكُمْ). When the hypocrites express their affiliation with their evil companions, it accurately reflects their strong relationship, as it is based on evidence of their actions i.e. there is ثُبُوْتٌ. Hence, their words are expressed using a جُمْلَةٌ اسْمِيَّةٌ indicating the permanence and continuity of their association i.e., depicts ثُبُوْتٌ and دَوَامٌ.

---

### حُدُوْثٌ and تَجَدُّدٌ in Nouns

*(Page 17)*

The concept of تَجَدُّدٌ and حُدُوْثٌ, ثُبُوْتٌ also applies to a شِبْهُ الْفِعْلِ (i.e. اِسْمُ الْفَاعِلِ, اَلصِّفَةُ الْمُشَبَّهَةُ بِاسْمِ الْفَاعِلِ and اِسْمُ الْمَفْعُوْلِ).

#### Example

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَإِذَا قَامُوْٓا إِلَى الصَّلوٰةِ قَامُوْا كُسَالَىٰ يُرَآءُوْنَ النَّاسَ﴾

</div>

> *And when they stand for Salah, they stand up lazily, only to show people.*

This verse portrays the salah of hypocrites in two ways: they are lazy, and they display their worship for show.

The first characteristic, laziness, is conveyed through a صِفَةٌ مُشَبَّهَةٌ بِاسْمِ الْفَاعِلِ which indicates a state of permanence, ثُبُوْتٌ. This shows that laziness is an enduring trait that persists throughout the entire prayer.

The second characteristic, showing off, is expressed through a verb that suggests recurrence or renewal, تَجَدُّدٌ. This shows that showing off is a behaviour that is repeated whenever there is an audience present.

## Supplement: Interchanging فِعْلٌ مُضَارِعٌ and فِعْلٌ مَاضٍ

*(Page 18)*

### Using a فِعْلٌ مَاضٍ to Describe a Future Event

Sometimes, a فِعْلٌ مَاضٍ is used to describe a future event to indicate the certainty of that event.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿أَتَىٰٓ أَمْرُ اللهِ فَلَا تَسْتَعْجِلُوْهُ﴾

</div>

> *The Event ordained by Allah **will surely come** to pass.*

In this verse, the past tense verb أَتَىٰ has been used in place of a مُضَارِعٌ to depict a future event. This emphasizes the inevitability of the future occurrence.

This style is commonly used in supplications:

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

جَزَاكَ اللهُ

</div>

The past tense of the verb جَزَى has been used in place of a مُضَارِعٌ to depict a future event. This emphasizes the hope of the speaker in the future occurrence.

> *May Allah reward you.*

### Using a فِعْلٌ مُضَارِعٌ to Describe a Past Event

Sometimes, a فِعْلٌ مُضَارِعٌ is used to describe a past event.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿فَفَرِيْقًا كَذَّبْتُمْ وَفَرِيْقًا تَقْتُلُوْنَ﴾

</div>

> *So, a faction (of Prophets) you denied, and a faction **you kill**.*

In this verse, the past event of killing the messengers of Allah is described using the فِعْلٌ مُضَارِعٌ. This highlights the abomination of this action.
