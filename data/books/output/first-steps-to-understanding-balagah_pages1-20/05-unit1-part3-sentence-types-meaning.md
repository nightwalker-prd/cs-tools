# Unit 1, Part 3: Types of Sentences According to Meaning

According to its meaning, there are two types of sentences:

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

1) جُمْلَةٌ خَبَرِيَّةٌ
2) جُمْلَةٌ إِنْشَائِيَّةٌ

---

## جُمْلَةٌ خَبَرِيَّةٌ (Informative Sentence)

*(Page 19)*

A جُمْلَةٌ خَبَرِيَّةٌ is a sentence which consists of information with a true or false value in accordance with reality.

For example, the sentence "The pen is broken" has a true or false value depending on whether the pen is broken or not.

### Quranic Examples

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿لَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا نُوْحًا إِلَىٰ قَوْمِهِ﴾

</div>

> *Indeed, we sent Nooh to his people.*

The sentence in this verse is a جُمْلَةٌ خَبَرِيَّةٌ; it comprises of information which can be checked with reality i.e. it has a true value.

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿...لَيَقُوْلُوْنَ ۞ وَلَدَ اللهُ وَإِنَّهُمْ لَكَاذِبُوْنَ ۞﴾

</div>

> *Indeed, they say, "**Allah has given birth**," and surely, they are liars.*

The middle sentence in this verse is a جُمْلَةٌ خَبَرِيَّةٌ which has a false value; it is not in accordance with reality.

---

## جُمْلَةٌ إِنْشَائِيَّةٌ (Non-Informative / Performative Sentence)

*(Pages 19–20)*

A جُمْلَةٌ إِنْشَائِيَّةٌ is a sentence which does not consist of information with a true or false value. Instead, it consists of commands, requests, etc.

For example, the sentence "Do not break the pen" is a command. It does not have a true or false value; it has no reality to be checked against.

### Quranic Example

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

﴿وَأَقِمِ الصَّلوٰةَ﴾

</div>

> *And establish salah.*

### Types of جُمْلَةٌ إِنْشَائِيَّةٌ

We will discuss the following types of جُمْلَةٌ إِنْشَائِيَّةٌ:

<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

1) أَمْرٌ
2) نَهْيٌ
3) اِسْتِفْهَامٌ
4) تَمَنٍّ
5) تَرَجٍّ
6) نِدَاءٌ

</div>

| # | Arabic Term | English |
|---|------------|---------|
| 1 | أَمْرٌ | Command |
| 2 | نَهْيٌ | Prohibition |
| 3 | اِسْتِفْهَامٌ | Interrogation |
| 4 | تَمَنٍّ | Wishing |
| 5 | تَرَجٍّ | Hoping |
| 6 | نِدَاءٌ | Calling |
