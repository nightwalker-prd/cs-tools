The study of the Arabic language can be divided into three layers, namely: Grammar, Balagah, and 'Ijaz.

## Grammar

Grammar encompasses Nahw (Syntax) and Sarf (Morphology). In Nahw, the initial discussion revolves around the إِعْرَابٌ of words and their position in a sentence. Sarf, on the other hand, focuses on the structure of words.

## Balagah (اَلْبَلَاغَةُ)

Balagah (اَلْبَلَاغَةُ) delves into the intricacies of word usage, sentence structure, emphasis placement, speech length and brevity, and other related topics.

It explores, for instance, the appropriate word choice or sentence structure e.g. the positioning of a مَفْعُوْلٌ بِهِ in relation to the verb.

## 'Ijaz (اَلْإِعْجَازُ)

'Ijaz (اَلْإِعْجَازُ) is the remarkable utilization of Balagah within the Quran, resulting in a text that is truly miraculous and unparalleled in its form.
