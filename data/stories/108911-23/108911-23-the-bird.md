## Full Verse-by-Verse Rendering

### Verse 1
> الحَبْسُ لَيْسَ مَذْهَبِيْ &emsp; وَلَيْسَ فِيهِ طَرَبِيْ

**Captivity is not my way, and there is no joy in it for me.**

### Verse 2
> فَلَسْتُ أَرْضَى قَفَصاً &emsp; وَإِنْ يَكُنْ مِنْ ذَهَبِ

**So I would never accept a cage, even if it were made of gold.**

### Verse 3
> غَابَاتُ رَبِّيْ غَايَتِيْ &emsp; وَالعَيْشُ فِيهَا مَطْلَبِيْ

**The forests of my Lord are my goal, and living in them is my heart's desire.**

### Verse 4
> قَدْ طَابَ فِيهَا مَطْعَمِيْ &emsp; وَرَاقَ فِيهَا مَشْرَبِيْ

**Indeed, my food has been delightful there, and my drink has been pure and pleasing.**

### Verse 5
> أَذْهَبُ فِيهَا أَسْتَقِيْ &emsp; مِنْ مَاءٍ نَبْعٍ أَعْذَبِ

**I go about in them to draw water from the sweetest of springs.**

### Verse 6
> أَصْدَحُ فِيهَا مُطْلَقاً &emsp; فَالحَبْسُ لَيْسَ مَذْهَبِيْ

**I sing freely there — for captivity is not my way.**

## Grammatical Analysis (تَحْلِيل نَحْوِيّ)

### 1. لَيْسَ — The Verb of Negation

لَيْسَ is one of the أَفْعَال نَاقِصَة (deficient/incomplete verbs, also called كَانَ وَأَخَوَاتُهَا). It negates a nominal sentence:

- **الحَبْسُ لَيْسَ مَذْهَبِيْ** — الحَبْسُ is the اسم (subject, marfū'), and مَذْهَبِيْ is the خَبَر (predicate, originally manṣūb but the yā' of possession absorbs the case marker)
- **لَيْسَ فِيهِ طَرَبِيْ** — Here the خَبَر (فِيهِ) is a prepositional phrase fronted, and طَرَبِيْ is the delayed اسم لَيْسَ

### 2. فَلَسْتُ أَرْضَى — Negated Imperfect

- لَسْتُ is لَيْسَ conjugated for 1st person singular (أنا)
- أَرْضَى is a مُضَارِع (imperfect) verb — the whole phrase means "I do not accept / I am not pleased with"
- قَفَصاً is the مفعول به (direct object) of أَرْضَى, in the accusative case (منصوب)

### 3. وَإِنْ يَكُنْ مِنْ ذَهَبِ — Conditional Clause

- إِنْ is a conditional particle (أداة شرط جازمة) — it governs the مجزوم (jussive) mood
- يَكُنْ is the مضارع مجزوم of كَانَ (the nūn is dropped from يَكُونُ due to jazm)
- مِنْ ذَهَبٍ is the خَبَر كَانَ (predicate of kāna), a prepositional phrase
- The اسم كَانَ (subject) is hidden, referring back to قَفَصاً — "even if it (the cage) be of gold"
- The جواب الشرط (answer to the condition) is understood from the previous clause (فَلَسْتُ أَرْضَى)

### 4. Nominal Sentences (جُمَل اِسْمِيَّة)

Several lines are pure nominal sentences (jumal ismiyya):

| Sentence | مُبْتَدَأ (Subject) | خَبَر (Predicate) |
|----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| غَابَاتُ رَبِّيْ غَايَتِيْ | غَابَاتُ رَبِّيْ | غَايَتِيْ |
| العَيْشُ فِيهَا مَطْلَبِيْ | العَيْشُ | مَطْلَبِيْ (with فِيهَا as متعلق) |

- غَابَاتُ رَبِّيْ is an إضافة (possessive construction): "forests of my Lord"
- The muḍāf (غابات) takes its case from its position; the muḍāf ilayh (ربّي) is in the genitive (مجرور)

### 5. قَدْ + Past Tense (تَحْقِيق)

- **قَدْ طَابَ** — قَدْ before a past tense verb indicates تَحْقِيق (certainty/emphasis): "indeed it was pleasant"
- طَابَ is a hollow verb (أَجْوَف يَائِي) from root ط-ي-ب

### 6. حَال (Adverb of State)

- **أَصْدَحُ فِيهَا مُطْلَقاً** — مُطْلَقاً is a حَال (adverb of state), describing the condition of the subject while singing
- It is an اسم مفعول (passive participle) from أَطْلَقَ (Form IV: to release), used adverbially in the accusative

### 7. المَصْدَر المِيمِيّ (Mīmī Verbal Noun) Pattern

A notable feature of this poem is the heavy use of the مَفْعَل pattern for mīmī verbal nouns, all serving as the rhyme words:

| Word | Root | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| مَذْهَب | ذ-ه-ب | way, doctrine |
| مَطْلَب | ط-ل-ب | desire, aspiration |
| مَطْعَم | ط-ع-م | food, nourishment |
| مَشْرَب | ش-ر-ب | drink |

This pattern (مَفْعَل) can indicate **place**, **time**, or **manner** of the action. Here they all function as **manner/abstract nouns** with the 1st person possessive suffix ـِي.

### 8. Rhyme Scheme (القَافِيَة)

The entire poem rhymes on **بِيْ / بِ** (the letter بَاء with kasra):
- مَذْهَبِيْ / طَرَبِيْ / ذَهَبِ / مَطْلَبِيْ / مَشْرَبِيْ / أَعْذَبِ / مَذْهَبِيْ

This is called قَافِيَة مُطْلَقَة (open rhyme). The poem uses the بَحْر المُتَقَارِب or a similar short meter, appropriate for children's literature and songs.

### 9. Circular Structure (البِنْيَة الدَّائِرِيَّة)

The poem begins and ends with the same line: **الحَبْسُ لَيْسَ مَذْهَبِيْ** — "Captivity is not my way." This circular/ring structure (رَدّ العَجُز عَلَى الصَّدْر) reinforces the bird's unwavering commitment to freedom.

---

## Thematic Summary

This short poem is spoken from the perspective of a bird who declares its love of freedom and rejection of captivity. Even a cage of gold holds no appeal — the bird's joy lies in the God-given forests where it eats, drinks from fresh springs, and sings freely. The poem carries a broader moral message: **true happiness comes from freedom, not from gilded prisons**. The attribution of the forests to "my Lord" (رَبِّي) adds a spiritual dimension — freedom is a divine gift, and the natural world is where the bird fulfills its God-given purpose.
