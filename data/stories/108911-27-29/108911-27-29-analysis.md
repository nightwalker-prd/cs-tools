# Story 7: مَن يَمنَعُكَ مِنِّي؟

## "Who Will Protect You from Me?"

*Source: Extracted from the two Sahihs (Bukhari & Muslim) and Sahih of Abu Bakr al-Isma'ili*

---

## Arabic Text & English Translation

### Introduction (p. 27)

<div dir="rtl">

> خَرَجَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ فِي غَزوَةٍ! هَل تَعرِفُونَ مَا هِيَ الغَزوَةُ؟

</div>

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ went out on a military expedition (ghazwa)! Do you know what a ghazwa is?

---

<div dir="rtl">

> لَعَلَّكُم تَعلَمُونَ أَنَّ المُسلِمِينَ كَانُوا يَخرُجُونَ لِلجِهَادِ فِي سَبِيلِ اللهِ وَكَانُوا يُقَاتِلُونَ المُشرِكِينَ وَالكُفَّارَ لِوَجهِ اللهِ تَعَالَى، وَلَعَلَّكُم تَعلَمُونَ فَضِيلَةَ الجِهَادِ فِي سَبِيلِ اللهِ؟

</div>

Perhaps you know that the Muslims used to go out for jihad in the way of Allah, and they used to fight the polytheists and the disbelievers for the sake of Allah the Most High. And perhaps you know the virtue of jihad in the way of Allah?

---

<div dir="rtl">

> وَكَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَخرُجُ أَحيَانًا مَعَ المُسلِمِينَ وَأَحيَانًا يَمكُثُ فِي المَدِينَةِ لِشُغلٍ أَو مَصلَحَةٍ وَيَبعَثُ جُندًا مِنَ المُسلِمِينَ.

</div>

The Prophet ﷺ would sometimes go out with the Muslims, and sometimes he would stay in Madinah due to some matter or interest, and would send out a troop of Muslims.

---

<div dir="rtl">

> فَالغَزوَةُ مَا خَرَجَ فِيهَا رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ فِي جُندٍ مِنَ المُسلِمِينَ لِلجِهَادِ فِي سَبِيلِ اللهِ.

</div>

So a **ghazwa** is an expedition in which the Messenger of Allah ﷺ himself went out with a troop of Muslims for jihad in the way of Allah.

---

### The Story Begins (p. 27)

<div dir="rtl">

> نَعَم فَخَرَجَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ فِي غَزوَةٍ وَرَجَعَ عَنهَا فِي الظَّهِيرَةِ وَكَانَت أَيَّامُ الصَّيفِ فَأَرَادَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ أَن يَستَرِيحَ.

</div>

Yes, so the Messenger of Allah ﷺ went out on an expedition and returned from it at midday. It was the days of summer, so the Messenger of Allah ﷺ wanted to rest.

---

<div dir="rtl">

> وَلَيسَ فِي البَرِّيَّةِ مَكَانٌ يَستَرِيحُ فِيهِ الإِنسَانُ إِلَّا الشَّجَرُ.

</div>

And there is no place in the wilderness where a person can rest except under trees.

---

### Setting the Scene (p. 28)

<div dir="rtl">

> وَلَيسَ فِي البَرِّيَّةِ فِي بِلَادِ العَرَبِ شَجَرٌ كَثِيرٌ وَلَيسَ فِيهَا إِلَّا السَّمُرُ.

</div>

And there are not many trees in the wilderness of the lands of the Arabs — there is nothing in it except the samur (acacia) trees.

---

<div dir="rtl">

> فَنَزَلَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ تَحتَ سَمُرَةٍ وَعَلَّقَ بِهَا سَيفَهُ، وَتَفَرَّقَ النَّاسُ وَنَامُوا، وَنَامَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ تَحتَ السَّمُرَةِ.

</div>

So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ stopped under an acacia tree and hung his sword on it. The people dispersed and slept, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ slept under the acacia tree.

---

<div dir="rtl">

> وَجَاءَ رَجُلٌ مِنَ المُشرِكِينَ وَسَيفُ رَسُولِ اللهِ ﷺ مُعَلَّقٌ بِالسَّمُرَةِ وَهُوَ فِي غِمدِهِ.

</div>

And a man from the polytheists came, while the sword of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was hanging on the acacia tree, still in its sheath.

---

<div dir="rtl">

> فَأَخَذَ المُشرِكُ السَّيفَ وَسَلَّهُ مِن غِمدِهِ وَاستَيقَظَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ.

</div>

The polytheist took the sword and drew it from its sheath, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ woke up.

---

### The Confrontation (p. 28)

<div dir="rtl">

> فَقَالَ المُشرِكُ... وَالسَّيفُ مَسلُولٌ فِي يَدِهِ...
> لِرَسُولِ اللهِ ﷺ: تَخَافُنِي؟

</div>

The polytheist said — with the sword drawn in his hand — to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ: **"Do you fear me?"**

---

<div dir="rtl">

> قَالَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ: لَا!

</div>

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: **"No!"**

---

<div dir="rtl">

> قَالَ المُشرِكُ: مَن يَمنَعُكَ مِنِّي؟

</div>

The polytheist said: **"Who will protect you from me?"**

---

<div dir="rtl">

> قَالَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ: اللهُ!

</div>

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: **"Allah!"**

---

### The Tables Turn (p. 28–29)

<div dir="rtl">

> فَسَقَطَ السَّيفُ مِن يَدِ المُشرِكِ فَأَخَذَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ السَّيفَ فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ لِلمُشرِكِ: مَن يَمنَعُكَ مِنِّي؟

</div>

The sword fell from the hand of the polytheist, so the Messenger of Allah ﷺ picked up the sword and said to the polytheist: **"Who will protect you from me?"**

---

<div dir="rtl">

> فَقَالَ المُشرِكُ: كُن خَيرَ آخِذٍ!

</div>

The polytheist said: **"Be the best of those who seize (i.e., be merciful)!"**

---

<div dir="rtl">

> فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ: أَتَشهَدُ أَن لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَأَنِّي رَسُولُ اللهِ؟

</div>

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: **"Do you testify that there is no god but Allah and that I am the Messenger of Allah?"**

---

<div dir="rtl">

> قَالَ المُشرِكُ: لَا! وَلَكِنِّي أُعَاهِدُكَ عَلَى أَن لَا أُقَاتِلَكَ وَلَا أَكُونَ مَعَ قَومٍ يُقَاتِلُونَكَ!

</div>

The polytheist said: **"No! But I pledge to you that I will not fight you, and I will not be with a people who fight you!"**

---

<div dir="rtl">

> فَخَلَّى رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ سَبِيلَهُ.

</div>

So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ let him go (lit. freed his way).

---

<div dir="rtl">

> فَأَتَى المُشرِكُ أَصحَابَهُ فَقَالَ: جِئتُكُم مِن عِندِ خَيرِ النَّاسِ.

</div>

The polytheist went to his companions and said: **"I have come to you from the best of people."**

---

<div dir="rtl">

> [مُلتَقَطٌ مِنَ الصَّحِيحَينِ وَصَحِيحِ أَبِي بَكرٍ الإِسمَاعِيلِيّ]

</div>

*[Extracted from the two Sahihs (Bukhari & Muslim) and the Sahih of Abu Bakr al-Isma'ili]*

## Grammatical Analysis (التحليل النحوي)

### 1. Verb Forms (الأوزان)

The text showcases multiple verb forms beyond Form I:

| Verb | Form | Pattern | Meaning Shift |
|------|------|---------|---------------|
| عَلَّقَ | II (فَعَّلَ) | ta'diya (transitive intensification) | "to hang" (causative of عَلِقَ "to cling") |
| تَفَرَّقَ | V (تَفَعَّلَ) | muṭāwa'a (reflexive of II) | "to disperse oneself" (from فَرَّقَ "to separate") |
| يُقَاتِلُونَ | III (فَاعَلَ) | mushāraka (mutual action) | "to fight one another" (from قَتَلَ "to kill") |
| أُعَاهِدُكَ | III (فَاعَلَ) | mushāraka | "to make a mutual pledge" (from عَهِدَ "to know/pledge") |
| يَستَرِيحُ | X (اِستَفعَلَ) | ṭalab (seeking) | "to seek rest" (from رَاحَ "to rest") |
| استَيقَظَ | X (اِستَفعَلَ) | ṭalab | "to wake up" (seeking wakefulness, from يَقِظَ) |

### 2. Nominal Sentence Patterns (الجمل الاسمية)

**a) لَيسَ (negation of nominal sentences):**

> وَلَيسَ فِي البَرِّيَّةِ مَكَانٌ يَستَرِيحُ فِيهِ الإِنسَانُ إِلَّا الشَّجَرُ

- **لَيسَ**: verb of negation (from the أخوات كان group)
- **مَكَانٌ**: اسم ليس (marfu' — nominative)
- **إِلَّا الشَّجَرُ**: exception after negation (badal from مكان, hence marfu')
- This is a **حصر** (restriction) construction: لَيسَ ... إِلَّا = "nothing ... except"

**b) Jumal ḥāliyya (حال sentences):**

> وَالسَّيفُ مَسلُولٌ فِي يَدِهِ

- This is a **جملة حالية** (circumstantial clause) — a nominal sentence describing the state of the subject while the main action happens
- **الواو**: واو الحال (the waw of state/circumstance)
- **السَّيفُ**: مبتدأ (subject)
- **مَسلُولٌ**: خبر (predicate) — ism maf'ul acting as an adjective: "drawn/unsheathed"

> وَسَيفُ رَسُولِ اللهِ ﷺ مُعَلَّقٌ بِالسَّمُرَةِ وَهُوَ فِي غِمدِهِ

- Another **جملة حالية** nested within a larger one
- **مُعَلَّقٌ**: ism maf'ul of Form II (عَلَّقَ), meaning "hung/suspended"

### 3. كَانَ and Its Sisters (كان وأخواتها)

Multiple uses of كانَ for past continuous/habitual action:

> كَانُوا يَخرُجُونَ — "they used to go out"
> كَانُوا يُقَاتِلُونَ — "they used to fight"
> وَكَانَ النَّبِيُّ يَخرُجُ — "the Prophet used to go out"

- **كَانَ** + **مضارع** (imperfect verb) = past habitual action ("used to...")
- اسم كان is marfu' (النَّبِيُّ), while the following verbal sentence serves as the خبر

### 4. لَعَلَّ (إنّ وأخواتها)

> لَعَلَّكُم تَعلَمُونَ

- **لَعَلَّ**: particle of hope/expectation (from the إنّ group)
- **ـكُم**: attached pronoun = اسم لعلّ (manṣūb position, but pronouns are mabni)
- **تَعلَمُونَ**: خبر لعلّ (a verbal sentence)

### 5. أَنَّ (المصدر المؤوّل)

> لَعَلَّكُم تَعلَمُونَ **أَنَّ** المُسلِمِينَ كَانُوا يَخرُجُونَ

- **أَنَّ المُسلِمِينَ**: أنّ + اسمها (manṣūb: المسلمينَ)
- **كَانُوا يَخرُجُونَ**: خبر أنّ (a verbal sentence)
- The entire أنّ clause functions as **مفعول به** (object) of تَعلَمُونَ

### 6. The لام of Purpose (لام التعليل)

> لِلجِهَادِ — "for jihad" (purpose)
> لِوَجهِ اللهِ — "for the face/sake of Allah" (purpose)
> لِشُغلٍ أَو مَصلَحَةٍ — "due to some work or benefit" (reason)

- All use **لِـ** + مجرور to express purpose or reason

### 7. أَنْ + المضارع المنصوب (المصدر المؤوّل)

> أَرَادَ... **أَن يَستَرِيحَ** — "he wanted to rest"
> أُعَاهِدُكَ عَلَى **أَن لَا أُقَاتِلَكَ** — "I pledge to you that I will not fight you"

- **أَن** is a naṣb particle; the following mudāri' verb takes fatḥa
- The combination أَن + verb forms a **مصدر مؤوّل** (interpreted masdar) functioning as maf'ul bihi

### 8. مَا الموصولة (Relative مَا)

> فَالغَزوَةُ **مَا** خَرَجَ فِيهَا رَسُولُ اللهِ

- **مَا** here is an **اسم موصول** (relative pronoun) = "that which"
- **خَرَجَ فِيهَا**: the صلة الموصول (relative clause), with **فِيهَا** as the عائد (returning pronoun)

### 9. Iḍāfa Chains (الإضافة)

Several multi-level iḍāfa constructions:

| Chain | Analysis |
|-------|----------|
| رَسُولُ اللهِ | muḍāf + muḍāf ilayhi (2 levels) |
| سَيفُ رَسُولِ اللهِ | 3-level chain: sword → messenger → Allah |
| يَدِ المُشرِكِ | hand of the polytheist |
| فَضِيلَةَ الجِهَادِ | virtue of jihad |
| أَيَّامُ الصَّيفِ | days of summer |
| بِلَادِ العَرَبِ | lands of the Arabs |
| خَيرِ النَّاسِ | best of people |

### 10. Dialogue Markers & Rhetorical Devices

- **هَل تَعرِفُونَ**: rhetorical question using هل to engage the reader
- **أَتَشهَدُ**: interrogative using the hamza of istifhām (أَ)
- **كُن خَيرَ آخِذٍ**: imperative (أمر) of كَانَ — a rare and eloquent construction meaning "be the best captor" (i.e., show mercy)
- The reversal of the question **مَن يَمنَعُكَ مِنِّي؟** — first asked by the mushrik, then by the Prophet ﷺ — is a powerful rhetorical device showing the complete reversal of power

### 11. Ism Maf'ul (اسم المفعول)

Three key instances of the passive participle:

| Word | Form | From | Meaning |
|------|------|------|---------|
| مُعَلَّق | II (مُفَعَّل) | عَلَّقَ | hung, suspended |
| مَسلُول | I (مَفعُول) | سَلَّ | drawn (unsheathed) |
| مُلتَقَط | VIII (مُفتَعَل) | اِلتَقَطَ | extracted, selected |

### 12. Exception with إِلَّا (الاستثناء)

> وَلَيسَ فِيهَا إِلَّا السَّمُرُ

- **Negative context** (ليس) + **إِلَّا** + **exception** = حصر (restriction)
- After negation, the مُستَثنَى follows the i'rab of the sentence (here it is اسم ليس, so مرفوع)
- This is **استثناء مُفرَّغ** (emptied exception) — the exception fills the grammatical role directly

## Key Themes & Lessons

1. **Trust in Allah (التوكل على الله)**: The Prophet's ﷺ calm response — "Allah!" — when threatened with a sword demonstrates absolute reliance on divine protection.

2. **Prophetic character (الأخلاق النبوية)**: Despite having the upper hand, the Prophet ﷺ offered the man Islam rather than revenge, and accepted a lesser pledge of non-aggression.

3. **The power of mercy**: The mushrik's final testimony — "I have come to you from the best of people" — shows how prophetic mercy transformed an enemy into an admirer.

4. **Definition of ghazwa vs. sariyya**: The text explicitly teaches that a غَزوَة is an expedition the Prophet ﷺ personally participated in, as opposed to a سَرِيَّة (implied) where he sent troops without going himself.
