# Story 10: مُسابَقَة — A Race

Pages 37–38 from *al-Qira'a al-Rashida* (Book 1)

---

## Arabic Text

### Page 37

كانَتْ أمسِ مُسابَقَةٌ فِي الجَرْيِ فِي مَدْرَسَتِي ، أوَّلاً اخْتارَ مُعَلِّمُ الرِّياضَةِ أرْبَعَةً وَعِشْرِينَ طالِباً مِنْ جَمِيعِ الصُّفُوفِ هُمْ أقْرانٌ وَأكْفاءُ ، وَأوْقَفَهُمْ فِي صُفُوفٍ صَفّاً خَلْفَ صَفٍّ ، وَفِي كُلِّ صَفٍّ ثَلاثَةٌ.

وَوَقَفَ الأُسْتاذُ بِجانِبٍ مِنْ هذِهِ الصُّفُوفِ ، وَقَدَّمَ صَفّاً فِيهِ مُحَمَّدٌ وَإبْراهِيمُ وَسَعِيدٌ وَهُمْ أقْرانٌ وَأكْفاءُ وَقالَ: قُومُوا فِي صَفٍّ واحِدٍ وَعَلى خَطٍّ واحِدٍ ، وَلا يَتَقَدَّمْ مِنْكُمْ أحَدٌ ، وَكانَ إبْراهِيمُ مُتَقَدِّماً قَلِيلاً فَأخَّرَهُ وَجَعَلَهُ فِي الصَّفِّ وَقالَ: أنا أعُدُّ لَكُمْ فَإذا قُلْتُ: واحِد فَسَوُّوا الصَّفَّ ، وَإذا قُلْتُ: اثْنانِ ، فَاسْتَعِدُّوا واجْمَعُوا ثِيابَكُمْ ، وَإذا قُلْتُ: ثَلاثَة ، فَطِيرُوا. وَذَهَبَ أحَدُ

### Page 38

المُعَلِّمِينَ إلى آخِرِ المَيْدانِ وَوَضَعَ هُنالِكَ قَصَبَةً وَقالَ: هذِهِ هِيَ الغايَةُ.

وَقالَ الأُسْتاذُ: واحِدٌ ، وَوَقَفَ قَلِيلاً ثُمَّ قالَ: اثْنانِ ، فَتَقَدَّمَ سَعِيدٌ ، فَقالَ الأُسْتاذُ: تَأخَّرْ يا سَعِيدُ وَأنا أعُدُّ مَرَّةً ثانِيَةً فَقالَ: واحِدٌ ، اثْنانِ ، ثَلاثَةٌ ، فَطارَ الأوْلادُ لا يَدْرِي أحَدٌ مَنِ السّابِقُ ، حَتّى بَرَزَ مُحَمَّدٌ وَهَتَفَ الأوْلادُ بِاسْمِهِ وَقالُوا: مُحَمَّد مُحَمَّد وَصاحُوا: مَرْحَى مَرْحَى ، وَكانَ هُوَ المُجَلِّيَ، وَلَحِقَهُ إبْراهِيمُ فَكانَ هُوَ المُصَلِّيَ ، وَجاءَ دَوْرِي وَقُمْتُ فِي أقْرانِي وَأكْفائِي وَعَدَّ الأُسْتاذُ: واحِدٌ ، اثْنانِ ، ثَلاثَةٌ ، وَأبْطَأتُ قَلِيلاً عِنْدَ الجَرْيِ فَما قَدَرْتُ أنْ أسْبِقَ وَأكُونَ المُجَلِّيَ ، وَوَصَلْنا إلى الغايَةِ وَكُنْتُ المُصَلِّيَ.

وَكانَ خالِدٌ المُجَلِّيَ ، فَهَتَفَ الأوْلادُ بِاسْمِهِ وَقالُوا: مَرْحَى مَرْحَى ، وَكانَ إخْوانِي يَظُنُّونَ أنِّي أنا المُجَلِّيَ ، لأنِّي خَفِيفٌ وَسَرِيعٌ وَأجْرِي كُلَّ يَوْمٍ ، وَتَأسَّفْتُ أيْضاً وَلكِنِّي قُلْتُ فِي نَفْسِي: سَأسْبِقُ فِي المَرَّةِ الثّانِيَةِ عِنْدَ انْتِهاءِ السَّنَةِ إنْ شاءَ اللهُ.

وَخَطَبَ الأُسْتاذُ فِي الأخِيرِ وَقالَ: إنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ كانَ يُسابِقُ وَكانَ أصْحابُهُ يَتَسابَقُونَ ، وَيَنْبَغِي لِلْمُسْلِمِ أنْ يَكُونَ نَشِيطاً خَفِيفاً قَوِيّاً حَتّى لا يَعْجِزَ فِي الجِهادِ.

## English Translation

Yesterday there was a running race at my school. First, the PE teacher selected twenty-four students from all the classes — they were peers and equals — and stood them in rows, one row behind another, with three in each row.

The teacher stood to one side of these rows and brought forward a row containing Muhammad, Ibrahim, and Sa'id — they were peers and equals — and said: "Stand in one row on one line, and let none of you step forward." Ibrahim was slightly ahead, so he moved him back and placed him in the row. Then he said: "I will count for you. When I say 'one,' straighten the row. When I say 'two,' get ready and gather up your clothes. And when I say 'three,' fly!" One of the teachers went to the end of the field and placed a reed there, and said: "This is the finish line."

The teacher said: "One!" — and paused briefly, then said: "Two!" Sa'id moved forward, so the teacher said: "Get back, Sa'id!" And he counted again: "One, two, three!" The boys flew off — no one knew who was in the lead — until Muhammad emerged ahead. The boys cheered his name and said: "Muhammad! Muhammad!" and shouted: "Bravo! Bravo!" He was the first-place finisher (*al-mujalli*), and Ibrahim caught up behind him, so he was the runner-up (*al-musalli*).

Then my turn came, and I stood among my peers and equals. The teacher counted: "One, two, three!" I was slightly slow at the start of the run, so I could not overtake anyone and be the winner. We reached the finish line, and I was the runner-up.

Khalid was the winner, and the boys cheered his name and said: "Bravo! Bravo!" My brothers had thought that I would be the winner, because I am light and fast and I run every day. I was disappointed too, but I said to myself: "I will win next time, at the end of the year, God willing."

At the end, the teacher gave a speech and said: "The Prophet ﷺ used to race, and his companions used to race one another. A Muslim should be active, light, and strong, so that he does not become incapable in striving (jihad)."

## Grammatical Analysis

### 1. Existential كانَ (كانَتْ أمسِ مُسابَقَةٌ)

The verb كانَ is used existentially: "There was a race yesterday." The subject مُسابَقَةٌ is indefinite (marfū' with tanwīn). The تْ on كانَتْ agrees with the feminine noun. The adverb أمسِ (yesterday) is mabni (indeclinable) on kasra.

### 2. Compound Number: أرْبَعَةً وَعِشْرِينَ طالِباً

- أرْبَعَةً — units (manṣūb as object of اخْتارَ)
- وَعِشْرِينَ — tens (manṣūb with yā' because it's a sound masculine plural pattern in the accusative)
- طالِباً — tamyīz (specification), singular accusative after numbers 11–99

### 3. Ḥāl (Adverbial State): صَفّاً خَلْفَ صَفٍّ

صَفّاً is a ḥāl (accusative state description) modifying how they were stood up: "row behind row." The word خَلْفَ is a ẓarf (adverb of place).

### 4. Prohibition: وَلا يَتَقَدَّمْ مِنْكُمْ أحَدٌ

- لا — lā an-nāhiya (prohibitive لا)
- يَتَقَدَّمْ — Form V muḍāri', majzūm (jussive) after لا الناهية — the final vowel is dropped (sukūn)
- This is a 3rd-person prohibition (indirect command), equivalent to: "Let no one among you step forward"

### 5. Conditional with إذا

Three parallel conditional clauses with إذا الشرطية:

| Condition | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| إذا قُلْتُ: واحِد | فَسَوُّوا الصَّفَّ |
| إذا قُلْتُ: اثْنانِ | فَاسْتَعِدُّوا واجْمَعُوا ثِيابَكُمْ |
| إذا قُلْتُ: ثَلاثَة | فَطِيرُوا |

The فَ in each response clause is called فاء الجزاء (correlative فَ). The verbs after it are imperative (أمر).

### 6. Indirect Question: لا يَدْرِي أحَدٌ مَنِ السّابِقُ

- لا يَدْرِي — "no one knows"
- مَنِ السّابِقُ — indirect question: "who the leader is"
- السّابِقُ remains marfū' (nominative) as the predicate of a nominal sentence within the question (مَن is the mubtada', السّابِقُ is the khabar)

### 7. حَتّى + Past Tense: حَتّى بَرَزَ مُحَمَّدٌ

حَتّى here is ابتدائية (introduces a new clause rather than governing the verb). It indicates the climax: "until Muhammad emerged." The past tense verb بَرَزَ is not affected grammatically by حتى.

### 8. أنْ + Subjunctive: قَدَرْتُ أنْ أسْبِقَ وَأكُونَ

- أنْ المصدرية — followed by the manṣūb (subjunctive) verb أسْبِقَ
- وَأكُونَ — also manṣūb, conjoined to أسْبِقَ
- The entire أنْ clause functions as the object of قَدَرْتُ

### 9. Negation with ما: فَما قَدَرْتُ

ما النافية negates the past-tense verb. Equivalent to: "I was not able to..."

### 10. Future + Conditional: سَأسْبِقُ ... إنْ شاءَ اللهُ

- سَـ — prefix for near future (will)
- إنْ شاءَ اللهُ — conditional with إنْ الشرطية + past tense verb شاءَ. The response clause is implied (= فَسَأسْبِقُ). This is a formulaic expression of hope.

### 11. Purpose Clause: حَتّى لا يَعْجِزَ فِي الجِهادِ

- حَتّى — purpose particle ("so that")
- لا يَعْجِزَ — negated subjunctive (manṣūb after حتى)
- Meaning: "so that he does not become incapable"

### 12. يَنْبَغِي + لِـ + أنْ Clause

> يَنْبَغِي لِلْمُسْلِمِ أنْ يَكُونَ نَشِيطاً خَفِيفاً قَوِيّاً

- يَنْبَغِي — impersonal verb ("it is proper/fitting")
- لِلْمُسْلِمِ — the one for whom it is fitting (introduced by لِـ)
- أنْ يَكُونَ — the subject of يَنْبَغِي (an أنْ clause functioning as فاعل)
- نَشِيطاً خَفِيفاً قَوِيّاً — three predicates (khabar) of يَكُونَ, all manṣūb

### 13. Verb Form Derivation from Root س-ب-ق

This story beautifully demonstrates three different verb forms from a single root:

| Form | Verb | Meaning | Pattern |
|------|------|---------|---------|
| I | سَبَقَ / أسْبِقَ | to outrun, be first | فَعَلَ |
| III | يُسابِقُ | to race (someone) | يُفاعِلُ — implies doing the action *with/against* someone |
| VI | يَتَسابَقُونَ | to race one another | يَتَفاعَلُونَ — mutual/reciprocal action |

## Cultural Note: Classical Horse-Racing Terminology

The story uses المُجَلِّي and المُصَلِّي — two terms from the classical Arabic system for ranking the top 10 finishers in a horse race (السِّباق). This system has its own names for each position:

| Rank | Arabic | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1st | المُجَلِّي | "The one who shines" — reveals himself ahead of all |
| 2nd | المُصَلِّي | "The one at the flanks" — his head is at the first horse's صَلا (hip) |
| 3rd | المُسَلِّي | "The consoler" — consoles himself for not being first or second |
| 4th | التّالِي | "The follower" |
| 5th | المُرْتاح | "The comfortable one" |

The use of these terms in a children's story shows how deeply embedded classical literary vocabulary is in traditional Arabic textbooks.
