# Story 6: نُزْهَةٌ وَطَبْخ — An Outing and Cooking

*From pages 24–26 of the reader (108911)*

---

## Arabic Text and English Translation

### Paragraph 1

> كَانَ يَوْمُ الثُّلَاثَاءِ الْمَاضِيْ يَوْمَ عُطْلَةٍ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ. جَاءَ إِلَيَّ دَاوُدُ صَبَاحاً وَقَالَ: الْيَوْمُ يَوْمُ الْعُطْلَةِ ، أَلَا نَخْرُجُ إِلَىٰ بُسْتَانٍ ، أَوْ مَكَانٍ فِيْ ضَوَاحِي الْمَدِيْنَةِ نَرْتَعُ وَنَلْعَبُ ، وَنَطْبُخُ مِنَ الطَّعَامِ مَا نَشْتَهِيْ وَنَأْكُلُ ، وَنَرْجِعُ فِي الْمَسَاءِ ، قُلْتُ: هُوَ كَذٰلِكَ! وَأَنَا كُنْتُ أُفَكِّرُ أَيْضاً كَيْفَ أَقْضِيْ هٰذَا الْيَوْمَ ، وَلٰكِنْ كَلِّمْ أَخَاكَ سُلَيْمَانَ وَالأَخَ هَاشِماً وَالسَّيِّدَ عُمَرَ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَخْرُجُوْنَ مَعَنَا.

Last Tuesday was a holiday at the school. Dawud came to me in the morning and said: "Today is the holiday — shall we not go out to a garden, or a place in the suburbs of the city, where we can enjoy ourselves and play, and cook whatever food we desire and eat, and return in the evening?" I said: "Indeed so! I too was thinking about how to spend this day. But speak to your brother Sulayman, and brother Hashim, and Mr. Umar — perhaps they will come out with us."

### Paragraph 2

> وَافَقَ دَاوُدُ عَلَىٰ ذٰلِكَ وَكَلَّمَهُمْ ، وَفَرِحُوْا جِدّاً وَجَاؤُوا إِلَىٰ بَيْتِيْ مِنْ سَاعَتِهِمْ وَصَدِيْقُنَا خَالِدٌ فَفَرِحْنَا بِهِ وَقُلْنَا: مَرْحَباً.

Dawud agreed to that and spoke to them. They were very happy and came to my house immediately, and our friend Khalid was with them, so we were pleased with him and said: "Welcome!"

### Paragraph 3

> اجْتَمَعْنَا وَقُلْنَا: هَلْ نَقْصِدُ بُسْتَاناً مِنْ بَسَاتِيْنِ الْمَدِيْنَةِ أَوْ نَتَوَجَّهُ إِلَىٰ ضَاحِيَةٍ مِنْ ضَوَاحِي الْمَدِيْنَةِ.

We gathered and said: "Shall we head to a garden from the gardens of the city, or head to a suburb from the suburbs of the city?"

### Paragraph 4

> قَالَ دَاوُدُ وَعُمَرُ: بَلْ نَقْصِدُ الْبُسْتَانَ الْكَبِيْرَ فِيْ وَسَطِ الْمَدِيْنَةِ ، فَإِنَّ الْبُسْتَانَ قَرِيْبٌ فَلَا يَضِيْعُ وَقْتُنَا فِي الذَّهَابِ إِلَىٰ ضَاحِيَةٍ مِنْ ضَوَاحِي الْبَلَدِ.

Dawud and Umar said: "Rather, let us head to the large garden in the center of the city, for the garden is nearby so our time will not be wasted going to a suburb on the outskirts of the town."

### Paragraph 5

> وَقَالَ سُلَيْمَانُ وَهَاشِمٌ وَأَنَا مَعَهُمَا: بَلْ نَتَوَجَّهُ إِلَىٰ بَعْضِ الضَّوَاحِي لِأَنَّا نُرِيْدُ أَنْ نَطْبُخَ الطَّعَامَ وَنَقْضِيَ النَّهَارَ فِي النُّزْهَةِ وَالطَّبْخِ.

Sulayman and Hashim — and I with them — said: "Rather, let us head to one of the suburbs, because we want to cook food and spend the day on the outing and cooking."

### Paragraph 6

> فَاسْتَقَرَّ رَأْيُنَا عَلَى الذَّهَابِ إِلَى الضَّاحِيَةِ ، فَاكْتَرَيْنَا مَرْكَبَةً وَوَصَلْنَا مِنْ سَاعَتِنَا إِلَى الضَّاحِيَةِ.

So our opinion settled on going to the suburb. We rented a vehicle and arrived at the suburb right away.

### Paragraph 7

> وَكُنَّا أَخَذْنَا مَعَنَا الرُّزَّ وَاللَّحْمَ وَالتَّوَابِلَ وَالسَّمْنَ وَالْخُضَرَ وَأَخَذْنَا قِدْرَيْنِ وَأَوَانِيَ ، وَكُنَّا عَلِمْنَا أَنَّ فِي الْمَحَلِّ خَبَّازاً فَقُلْنَا نَشْتَرِي الرَّغِيْفَ فَإِنَّ الرَّغِيْفَ فِيْهِ تَعَبٌ.

We had taken with us rice, meat, spices, ghee, and vegetables. We also took two pots and utensils. We had learned that there was a baker in the area, so we said we would buy the bread, since making bread is hard work.

### Paragraph 8

> اخْتَرْنَا مَكَاناً ظَلِيْلاً وَكَانَ السَّيِّدُ عُمَرُ وَالسَّيِّدُ هَاشِمٌ يُحْسِنَانِ الطَّبْخَ فَتَوَلَّيَا أَمْرَ الطَّبْخِ وَسَاعَدَهُمَا دَاوُدُ وَسُلَيْمَانُ.

We chose a shady spot. Mr. Umar and Mr. Hashim were skilled at cooking, so they took charge of the cooking, and Dawud and Sulayman assisted them.

### Paragraph 9

> وَتَوَلَّيْتُ أَمْرَ الْحَطَبِ فَذَهَبْتُ إِلَى الْغَابَةِ الْقَرِيْبَةِ وَجِئْتُ بِالْحَطَبِ مِنْ سَاعَتِيْ ، وَدَقَّ خَالِدٌ التَّوَابِلَ وَذَهَبْتُ أَنَا إِلَى الْخَبَّازِ فَاشْتَرَيْتُ الأَرْغِفَةَ.

I took charge of the firewood, so I went to the nearby forest and came back with firewood quickly. Khalid ground the spices, and I went to the baker and bought the loaves.

### Paragraph 10

> وَأَدْرَكَ الطَّعَامُ فِي السَّاعَةِ الْحَادِيَةَ عَشْرَةَ ، وَقَدْ غَلَبَنَا الْجُوْعُ وَاشْتَهَيْنَا الطَّعَامَ فَأَكَلْنَا بِرَغْبَةٍ ، وَكَانَ الطَّعَامُ شَهِيّاً لَذِيْذاً.

The food was ready at eleven o'clock. Hunger had overcome us and we craved the food, so we ate eagerly. The food was appetizing and delicious.

### Paragraph 11

> وَجَلَسْنَا نَتَحَدَّثُ حَتَّىٰ كَانَ وَقْتُ الظُّهْرِ فَأَذَّنْتُ وَصَلَّيْنَا جَمَاعَةً.

We sat chatting until it was time for Dhuhr (noon prayer), so I gave the adhan (call to prayer) and we prayed in congregation.

### Paragraph 12

> وَخَرَجْنَا بَعْدَ الصَّلَاةِ نَزُوْرُ بَعْضَ الآثَارِ ، وَفِيْ الْعَصْرِ رَجَعْنَا إِلَى الْبَلَدِ مَسْرُوْرِيْنَ.

We went out after the prayer to visit some historical sites, and at Asr (afternoon) time we returned to the town, happy and content.

## Grammatical Analysis

### 1. كَانَ and its Sisters (النَّواسِخ)

The text makes extensive use of **كَانَ** as both a full verb and a defective (nāqiṣ) verb:

- **كَانَ يَوْمُ الثُّلَاثَاءِ... يَوْمَ عُطْلَةٍ** — كَانَ as a nāsikh: يَوْمُ is the ism (nominative), يَوْمَ عُطْلَةٍ is the khabar (accusative).
- **كَانَ السَّيِّدُ عُمَرُ... يُحْسِنَانِ** — ism of كَانَ is السَّيِّدُ عُمَرُ وَالسَّيِّدُ هَاشِمٌ; the khabar is the verbal sentence يُحْسِنَانِ الطَّبْخَ.
- **كَانَ الطَّعَامُ شَهِيّاً لَذِيْذاً** — ism: الطَّعَامُ (nom.); khabar: شَهِيّاً لَذِيْذاً (acc.).
- **كُنَّا أَخَذْنَا** — كَانَ used to express pluperfect ("we had taken"); the khabar is the verbal sentence أَخَذْنَا.
- **كُنْتُ أُفَكِّرُ** — past continuous ("I was thinking"); khabar is a muḍāriʿ sentence.

### 2. إِنَّ and its Sisters

- **فَإِنَّ الْبُسْتَانَ قَرِيْبٌ** — إِنَّ puts الْبُسْتَانَ in accusative (ism of inna); قَرِيْبٌ is the khabar (nom.).
- **فَإِنَّ الرَّغِيْفَ فِيْهِ تَعَبٌ** — ism: الرَّغِيْفَ (acc.); khabar: the prepositional phrase فِيْهِ تَعَبٌ (a jumlah).
- **لِأَنَّا نُرِيْدُ** — لِأَنَّنَا contracted to لِأَنَّا; ism is the pronoun نَا; khabar is the verb نُرِيْدُ.
- **أَنَّ فِي الْمَحَلِّ خَبَّازاً** — ism: خَبَّازاً (acc., delayed ism); khabar: the prepositional phrase فِي الْمَحَلِّ (fronted).

### 3. لَعَلَّ (Particle of Hope)

- **لَعَلَّهُمْ يَخْرُجُوْنَ مَعَنَا** — "perhaps they will go out with us." لَعَلَّ is from إِنَّ وَأَخَوَاتُهَا. The attached pronoun هُمْ is the ism (acc.); يَخْرُجُوْنَ مَعَنَا is the khabar (verbal sentence).

### 4. The Dual (المُثَنَّى)

- **قِدْرَيْنِ** — dual of قِدْر (two pots), in the accusative/genitive case (يَاء + نُون).
- **يُحْسِنَانِ** — dual muḍāriʿ verb ("they two are skilled"), referring to Umar and Hashim.
- **فَتَوَلَّيَا** — dual māḍī ("they two took charge"), the alif ending marks the dual subject.
- **سَاعَدَهُمَا** — the attached pronoun هُمَا is the dual object pronoun ("assisted them both").
- **مَعَهُمَا** — "with them both" (dual attached pronoun).

### 7. الحَال (Circumstantial Accusative)

- **جَاءَ إِلَيَّ دَاوُدُ صَبَاحاً** — صَبَاحاً functions as a ẓarf zamān (adverb of time, manṣūb).
- **فَرِحُوا جِدّاً** — جِدّاً is a ḥāl / adverb of manner ("very much").
- **رَجَعْنَا إِلَى الْبَلَدِ مَسْرُورِيْنَ** — مَسْرُورِيْنَ is a ḥāl (accusative, sound masculine plural in naṣb form), describing the state of the subject: "we returned... being happy."

### 8. Fronted Khabar (تَقْدِيم الخَبَر)

- **أَنَّ فِي الْمَحَلِّ خَبَّازاً** — The khabar (فِي الْمَحَلِّ) is fronted before the ism (خَبَّازاً). This is common when the ism is indefinite (nakirah) and the khabar is a prepositional phrase.
- **فِيْهِ تَعَبٌ** — The khabar (فِيْهِ) is fronted before the mubtadaʾ (تَعَبٌ).

### 9. أَلَا (Particle of Invitation)

- **أَلَا نَخْرُجُ إِلَىٰ بُسْتَانٍ** — أَلَا here is a particle of suggestion/invitation (حَرْف عَرْض), meaning "shall we not...?" or "why don't we...?" The verb after it remains in the indicative (مَرْفُوع).

### 10. The Five Nouns (الأَسْمَاء الخَمْسَة)

- **كَلِّمْ أَخَاكَ سُلَيْمَانَ** — أَخَاكَ is one of the five special nouns (أَخ = brother). In the accusative (mafʿūl bih), it takes the long alif: أَخَا + كَ (your brother). These nouns are declined with long vowels (و/ا/ي) instead of ḍammah/fatḥah/kasrah.

### 11. Iḍāfah Chains (الإِضَافَة)

The text has many iḍāfah constructions:

- **يَوْمُ الثُّلَاثَاءِ الْمَاضِي** — "the day of Tuesday, the past" (يَوْم is muḍāf, الثُّلَاثَاء is muḍāf ilayh; الْمَاضِي is an adjective of الثُّلَاثَاء)
- **ضَوَاحِي الْمَدِيْنَةِ** — "the suburbs of the city"
- **أَمْرَ الطَّبْخِ** — "the matter of cooking"
- **وَقْتُ الظُّهْرِ** — "the time of noon"
- **السَّاعَةِ الْحَادِيَةَ عَشْرَةَ** — "the eleventh hour" (ordinal number construction)

### 12. The فَ Connectors

The text makes heavy use of the **فَاء** (fa-) connector in different functions:

- **فَاء السَّبَبِيَّة** (causal): فَفَرِحْنَا ("so we were happy"), فَأَكَلْنَا ("so we ate")
- **فَاء التَّعْقِيب** (sequential): فَذَهَبْتُ ("then I went"), فَاشْتَرَيْتُ ("then I bought")
- **فَاء الاسْتِئْنَاف** (resumptive): فَاسْتَقَرَّ رَأْيُنَا ("so our opinion settled")

### 13. كُنَّا + Past Tense = Pluperfect

- **كُنَّا أَخَذْنَا مَعَنَا** — "We *had* taken with us" (pluperfect / past perfect)
- **كُنَّا عَلِمْنَا أَنَّ** — "We *had* learned that..."

This construction (كَانَ + past tense verb) creates a pluperfect meaning, indicating an action completed before the main narrative time.

### 14. Ordinal Numbers

- **السَّاعَةِ الْحَادِيَةَ عَشْرَةَ** — "the eleventh hour." The ordinal for 11 is الْحَادِيَة عَشْرَة (feminine, agreeing with سَاعَة). Both parts are in fatḥah as an indeclinable compound number (مَبْنِيّ عَلَى الفَتْح).

## Story Summary

On a school holiday (Tuesday), Dawud suggests an outing with friends. The narrator agrees and they invite Sulayman, Hashim, Umar, and Khalid. They debate between a city garden and a suburban area — the suburb wins because they want to cook outdoors. They rent a vehicle, bring ingredients (rice, meat, spices, ghee, vegetables, pots), and buy bread from a local baker. Umar and Hashim cook while others gather firewood, grind spices, and buy bread. They eat at 11 AM, rest and chat until Dhuhr prayer, visit historical sites afterward, and return home happily at Asr time.
