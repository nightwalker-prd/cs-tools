# Story 30 (Part 2): Who Will Place the Stone?

## مَنْ يَضَعُ الْحَجَرَ؟ (٢)

---

## Text and Translation

### Paragraph 1

> وَمَكَثَتْ قُرَيْشٌ عَلَىٰ ذٰلِكَ أَرْبَعَ لَيَالٍ أَوْ خَمْسًا ثُمَّ إِنَّهُمُ اجْتَمَعُوا فِي الْمَسْجِدِ وَتَشَاوَرُوا.

Quraysh remained in that state for four or five nights, then they gathered in the mosque and consulted one another.

### Paragraph 2

> تَشَاوَرُوا وَقَالُوا: مَنْ يَضَعُ الْحَجَرَ الأَسْوَدَ فِي مَحَلِّهِ؟ كُلُّ قَبِيلَةٍ حَرِيصَةٌ عَلَىٰ أَنْ تَنَالَ هٰذَا الشَّرَفَ، وَالْحَجَرُ وَاحِدٌ، وَالْقَبَائِلُ كَثِيرَةٌ.

They consulted and said: "Who will place the Black Stone in its place?" Every tribe was eager to attain this honor, yet the stone was one and the tribes were many.

### Paragraph 3

> إِذًا لَا بُدَّ مِنَ الْحَرْبِ وَالْحَرْبُ مَشْؤُومَةٌ جِدًّا.

Then war was inevitable, and war is very ill-omened.

### Paragraph 4

> قَالَ بَعْضُ النَّاسِ: لَا بَأْسَ بِالْحَرْبِ فَالْمَوْتُ شَيْءٌ هَيِّنٌ لِلْعَرَبِ فِي سَبِيلِ الْحَقِّ وَالشَّرَفِ.

Some people said: "There is no harm in war, for death is an easy thing for the Arabs in the path of truth and honor."

### Paragraph 5

> قَالَ الْعُقَلَاءُ: نَعَمْ! لَا بَأْسَ بِالْحَرْبِ وَلٰكِنْ لَا حَاجَةَ إِلَى الْحَرْبِ فِي هٰذَا الْوَقْتِ.

The wise ones said: "Yes! There is no harm in war, but there is no need for war at this time."

### Paragraph 6

> وَلٰكِنْ مَا هُوَ الطَّرِيقُ؟ وَكَيْفَ يُوضَعُ الْحَجَرُ الأَسْوَدُ فِي مَحَلِّهِ بِغَيْرِ قِتَالٍ؟

But what is the way? And how can the Black Stone be placed in its position without fighting?

### Paragraph 7

> تَشَاوَرُوا وَتَشَاوَرُوا وَتَشَاوَرُوا كَثِيرًا وَوَجَدُوا الطَّرِيقَ.

They consulted and consulted and consulted at great length, and they found the way.

### Paragraph 8

> قَالَ شَيْخٌ وَكَانَ أَكْبَرَهُمْ سِنًّا: أَوَّلُ مَنْ يَدْخُلُ مِنْ بَابِ هٰذَا الْمَسْجِدِ يَقْضِي بَيْنَكُمْ، فَقَبِلُوا وَرَضُوا بِذٰلِكَ.

An elder — and he was the oldest among them in age — said: "The first one to enter through the door of this mosque shall judge between you." So they accepted and were pleased with that.

### Paragraph 9

> تَعْرِفُونَ مَنْ كَانَ أَوَّلَ دَاخِلٍ؟ كَانَ أَوَّلَ دَاخِلٍ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ،

Do you know who was the first to enter? The first to enter was the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.

### Paragraph 10

> فَلَمَّا رَأَوْهُ قَالُوا: هٰذَا الأَمِينُ رَضِينَا، هٰذَا مُحَمَّدٌ!

When they saw him, they said: "This is the Trustworthy One — we are pleased! This is Muhammad!"

### Paragraph 11

> فَلَمَّا وَصَلَ إِلَيْهِمْ وَأَخْبَرُوهُ الْخَبَرَ طَلَبَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ ثَوْبًا فَأُتِيَ بِهِ، فَأَخَذَ الْحَجَرَ الأَسْوَدَ فَوَضَعَهُ فِيهِ بِيَدِهِ ثُمَّ قَالَ: لِتَأْخُذْ كُلُّ قَبِيلَةٍ بِنَاحِيَةٍ مِنَ الثَّوْبِ ثُمَّ ارْفَعُوهُ جَمِيعًا، فَفَعَلُوا

When he reached them and they told him the news, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ asked for a cloth, and it was brought to him. He took the Black Stone and placed it in it with his hand, then said: "Let each tribe take hold of a side of the cloth, then lift it all together." So they did.

### Paragraph 12

> حَتَّىٰ إِذَا بَلَغُوا مَوْضِعَهُ وَضَعَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ الْحَجَرَ الأَسْوَدَ فِي مَحَلِّهِ بِيَدِهِ، وَهٰكَذَا دَفَعَ رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ هٰذَا الشَّرَّ وَمَنَعَ الْحَرْبَ.

Until when they reached its position, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ placed the Black Stone in its place with his own hand. And thus the Messenger of Allah ﷺ averted this evil and prevented war.

## Grammatical Analysis (التحليل النحوي)

### 2. Passive Voice (المبني للمجهول)

- **يُوضَعُ** — "is placed" (passive of يَضَعُ). Used in: كَيْفَ يُوضَعُ الْحَجَرُ الأَسْوَدُ ("How can the Black Stone be placed?"). الْحَجَرُ is the نائب الفاعل (deputy subject) in the nominative.
- **أُتِيَ بِهِ** — "it was brought to him" (passive of أَتَى). The preposition بِ introduces the original object.

### 3. Nominal Sentences (الجملة الاسمية)

- **كُلُّ قَبِيلَةٍ حَرِيصَةٌ** — "Every tribe [is] eager." مبتدأ + خبر (subject + predicate). كُلُّ is مُضاف and قَبِيلَةٍ is مُضاف إليه (مجرور).
- **الْحَجَرُ وَاحِدٌ** — "The stone [is] one." Simple nominal sentence.
- **الْقَبَائِلُ كَثِيرَةٌ** — "The tribes [are] many." Simple nominal sentence.
- **الْمَوْتُ شَيْءٌ هَيِّنٌ** — "Death [is] an easy thing." هَيِّنٌ is صفة (adjective) for شَيْءٌ.
- **هٰذَا الأَمِينُ** — "This [is] the Trustworthy One." اسم إشارة as مبتدأ.

### 4. إنَّ and Its Sisters

- **إِنَّهُمُ اجْتَمَعُوا** — إنَّ + attached pronoun (هُمْ) as its اسم (noun), followed by the جملة فعلية as the خبر (predicate): "Indeed they gathered."

### 5. Conditional & Temporal Structures

- **فَلَمَّا رَأَوْهُ قَالُوا** — "When they saw him, they said." لَمَّا is a temporal particle meaning "when" (for completed past actions). It requires a past tense verb.
- **حَتَّىٰ إِذَا بَلَغُوا** — "Until when they reached." حَتَّى إِذَا introduces a temporal clause indicating the endpoint of an action.

### 6. The Iḍāfa Construction (الإضافة)

Multiple examples of the possessive/genitive construction:

| Iḍāfa | Translation | Analysis |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| بَابِ هٰذَا الْمَسْجِدِ | the door of this mosque | Three-part iḍāfa |
| بَعْضُ النَّاسِ | some of the people | Partitive iḍāfa |
| رَسُولُ اللهِ | the Messenger of Allah | Title iḍāfa |
| أَكْبَرَهُمْ سِنًّا | the oldest of them in age | Superlative + تمييز |
| مَحَلِّهِ | its place | Noun + pronoun suffix |
| مَوْضِعَهُ | its position | Noun + pronoun suffix |

### 7. لَا النافية للجنس (لَا of Absolute Negation)

- **لَا بُدَّ مِنَ الْحَرْبِ** — "There is no escape from war." بُدَّ is the اسم لا (built on fatḥa), مِنَ الحَرْبِ is the خبر.
- **لَا بَأْسَ بِالْحَرْبِ** — "There is no harm in war." بَأْسَ is the اسم لا (built on fatḥa).
- **لَا حَاجَةَ إِلَى الْحَرْبِ** — "There is no need for war." حَاجَةَ is the اسم لا (built on fatḥa).

### 8. Lām of Command (لام الأمر)

- **لِتَأْخُذْ كُلُّ قَبِيلَةٍ** — "Let each tribe take." The لام is a jussive particle (لام الأمر) giving an indirect command to a third person. The verb تَأْخُذْ is مجزوم (with sukūn). This is used because you cannot directly command a third person with the regular imperative.

### 9. Imperative Verbs (فعل الأمر)

- **ارْفَعُوهُ** — "Lift it!" (2nd person masculine plural imperative of رَفَعَ). The واو is the subject pronoun, and the هُ is the object pronoun suffix.

### 10. Tamyīz (التمييز — Specification)

- **أَكْبَرَهُمْ سِنًّا** — "The oldest of them **in age**." سِنًّا is a tamyīz (accusative of specification), clarifying what "oldest" refers to. It is in the منصوب (accusative) case.
- **أَرْبَعَ لَيَالٍ أَوْ خَمْسًا** — The number أَرْبَعَ is مُضاف to لَيَالٍ (genitive plural), following the rule that numbers 3–10 take a plural مُضاف إليه. Note the gender polarity: أَرْبَعَ (masculine form) is used with لَيَالٍ (feminine noun).

### 11. Ḥāl (الحال — Circumstantial Accusative)

- **جَمِيعًا** — "all together." This is a حال (circumstantial accusative) describing the state of the subject when lifting the cloth. It is in the منصوب case.

### 12. Key Rhetorical Features

- **Repetition for emphasis**: تَشَاوَرُوا وَتَشَاوَرُوا وَتَشَاوَرُوا كَثِيرًا — The triple repetition of "they consulted" conveys the length and intensity of the deliberation.
- **Rhetorical question**: تَعْرِفُونَ مَنْ كَانَ أَوَّلَ دَاخِلٍ؟ — "Do you know who was the first to enter?" This engages the reader directly.
- **Juxtaposition**: وَالْحَجَرُ وَاحِدٌ وَالْقَبَائِلُ كَثِيرَةٌ — "The stone is one and the tribes are many" — a concise contrast that captures the entire dilemma.

## Historical Context

This story narrates the famous incident of the placement of the Black Stone (الحَجَر الأسوَد) during the rebuilding of the Ka'ba by the Quraysh, approximately five years before the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ received revelation. The tribes disputed over who would have the honor of placing the stone back in its corner. The Prophet's solution — placing the stone on a cloth so that representatives of each tribe could lift it together, and then placing it himself — demonstrated his wisdom and diplomacy, and is one of the reasons he was already known as "al-Amīn" (the Trustworthy One) before prophethood.
