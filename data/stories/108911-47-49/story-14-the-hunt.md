# Story 14: الصَّيْدُ — The Hunt

**Source:** Pages 47–49

---

## Full Text with Translation

### Paragraph 1

> خَرَجْتُ يَوْمَ عُطْلَةٍ مَعَ صَيَّادِينَ عِنْدَهُمْ بَنَادِقُ وَسَكَاكِينُ، خَرَجْنَا مُبَكِّرِينَ فِي الصَّبَاحِ لَمْ تَطْلُعِ الشَّمْسُ، وَكَانَ مَعِيْ كَثِيرٌ مِنْ أَتْرَابِيْ وَأَصْدِقَائِي، وَأَخَذْنَا غَدَاءَنَا مَعَنَا لِنَتَغَدَّىٰ إِذَا غَلَبَنَا الْجُوْعُ، وَكُنَّا نُرِيدُ أَنْ نَرْجِعَ فِي الْمَسَاءِ.

I went out on a holiday with hunters who had rifles and knives. We set out early in the morning before the sun had risen, and with me were many of my peers and friends. We took our lunch with us to eat when hunger overcame us, and we intended to return in the evening.

### Paragraph 2

> وَلَمْ نَزَلْ نَمْشِيْ فِي الْحَرِّ وَالشَّمْسِ حَتَّىٰ تَعِبْنَا، وَغَلَبَنَا الْجُوْعُ وَالظَّمَأُ، وَكَانَ الْغَدَاءُ مَعَ خَلِيلٍ، وَقَدْ ضَلَّ الطَّرِيقَ، وَمَا وَجَدْنَا طَعَامًا وَلَا مَاءً.

We kept walking in the heat and sun until we grew tired, and hunger and thirst overcame us. The lunch was with Khalil, and he had lost his way, and we found neither food nor water.

### Paragraph 3

> وَانْتَصَفَ النَّهَارُ وَجَلَسْنَا فِيْ ظِلِّ شَجَرَةٍ نَنْتَظِرُ خَلِيلًا، وَبَرَزَ خَلِيلٌ مِنْ بُعْدٍ فَنَادَيْنَاهُ بِاسْمِهِ وَحَمِدْنَا اللهَ، وَتَغَدَّيْنَا وَاسْتَرَحْنَا قَلِيلًا، ثُمَّ خَرَجْنَا.

Midday came and we sat in the shade of a tree waiting for Khalil. Khalil appeared from a distance, so we called him by name and praised Allah. We ate lunch and rested a little, then we set out.

### Paragraph 4

> وَدَخَلْنَا فِي الْغَابَةِ وَوَجَدْنَا آثَارَ بَقَرِ الْوَحْشِ فَتَفَرَّقْنَا وَجَلَسْنَا بِالْمِرْصَادِ وَخَرَجَتْ بَقَرَةٌ مِنْ بَيْنِ الأَشْجَارِ، وَكَانَ السَّيِّدُ إِسْمَاعِيلُ مُسْتَعِدًّا فَصَوَّبَ إِلَيْهَا بُنْدُقِيَّتَهُ، وَأَطْلَقَ الرَّصَاصَةَ وَأَصَابَ الْبَقَرَةَ فِي صَدْرِهَا، فَسَقَطَتْ جَرِيحًا تَضْرِبُ بِرِجْلَيْهَا.

We entered the forest and found traces of wild cattle, so we spread out and sat in ambush. A cow came out from among the trees, and Mr. Ismail was ready, so he aimed his rifle at it and fired the bullet. He hit the cow in its chest, and it fell wounded, kicking with its legs.

### Paragraph 5

> وَسَمِعَ الإِخْوَانُ صَوْتَ الْبُنْدُقِيَّةِ فَجَاؤُوا، وَذَبَحَهَا يَاقُوتُ بِسِكِّينٍ كَبِيرٍ حَادٍّ وَسَمَّى اللهَ وَكَبَّرَ، وَكُنَّا نَتَكَلَّمُ وَكُنَّا مُطْمَئِنِّينَ إِذْ خَرَجَتْ بَقَرَةٌ أُخْرَىٰ، فَأَطْلَقَ عَلَيْهَا هَاشِمٌ بُنْدُقِيَّتَهُ بِسُرْعَةٍ، وَمَا قَدَرَ أَنْ يُصَوِّبَ الْبُنْدُقِيَّةَ، فَأَخْطَأَتِ الرَّصَاصَةُ وَمَا صَادَتِ الْبَقَرَةَ وَتَأَسَّفَ هَاشِمٌ وَتَأَسَّفَتِ الْجَمَاعَةُ.

The brothers heard the sound of the rifle and came. Yaqut slaughtered it with a large, sharp knife, saying "Bismillah" and "Allahu Akbar." We were talking and feeling at ease when another cow came out. Hashim quickly fired his rifle at it, but he couldn't aim the rifle properly, so the bullet missed and didn't hit the cow. Hashim was sorry and the group was sorry.

### Paragraph 6

> وَصِدْنَا حَمَامَتَيْنِ بِرَصَاصَةٍ وَبَطَّتَيْنِ بِرَصَاصَتَيْنِ، وَكَانَ عِنْدِي سِكِّينٌ صَغِيرٌ حَادٌّ فَذَبَحْتُ الْبَطَّتَيْنِ، وَسَمَّيْتُ اللهَ وَكَبَّرْتُ.

We hunted two pigeons with one bullet and two ducks with two bullets. I had a small, sharp knife, so I slaughtered the two ducks, saying "Bismillah" and "Allahu Akbar."

### Paragraph 7

> وَقُلْتُ لِلسَّيِّدِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ: أَنَا أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَصِيدَ أَيْضًا، فَأَعْطَانِي بُنْدُقِيَّتَهُ وَوَضَعَ فِيهَا رَصَاصَةً، وَكُنْتُ أَعْرِفُ كَيْفَ أُصَوِّبُ الْبُنْدُقِيَّةَ وَكَيْفَ أُطْلِقُهَا، لِأَنِّي أَطْلَقْتُ الْبُنْدُقِيَّةَ أَرْبَعَ مَرَّاتٍ أَوْ خَمْسَ مَرَّاتٍ، فَذَهَبْتُ وَجَلَسْتُ بِالْمِرْصَادِ، وَجَاءَ حَمَامٌ وَوَقَعَ عَلَىٰ شَجَرَةٍ قَرِيبَةٍ، وَصَوَّبْتُ بُنْدُقِيَّتِي نَحْوَ الْحَمَامِ، وَأَطْلَقْتُ الْبُنْدُقِيَّةَ، فَأَصَبْتُ حَمَامَتَيْنِ وَفَرِحْتُ جِدًّا لَمَّا أَصَبْتُ الْحَمَامَتَيْنِ وَكَبَّرْتُ مِنَ الْفَرَحِ.

I said to Mr. Ismail: "I also want to hunt." So he gave me his rifle and loaded a bullet in it. I knew how to aim the rifle and how to fire it, because I had fired a rifle four or five times. So I went and sat in ambush, and pigeons came and landed on a nearby tree. I aimed my rifle toward the pigeons and fired. I hit two pigeons and was very happy when I hit them, and I said "Allahu Akbar" out of joy.

### Paragraph 8

> وَجَاءَ الإِخْوَانُ وَقَالُوا: مَرْحَىٰ مَرْحَىٰ، وَقَالُوا: مَا شَاءَ اللهُ إِنَّ خَالِدًا صَيَّادٌ.

The brothers came and said: "Bravo! Bravo!" And they said: "Masha'Allah, indeed Khalid is a hunter."

### Paragraph 9

> وَمَا رَضِيتُ أَنْ يَذْبَحَهُمَا أَحَدٌ، فَذَهَبْتُ وَسَمَّيْتُ اللهَ وَذَبَحْتُ الْحَمَامَتَيْنِ بِسِكِّينِي الصَّغِيرِ الْحَادِّ، وَرَجَعْنَا إِلَى الْقَرْيَةِ فِي الْمَسَاءِ بِصَيْدٍ كَثِيرٍ، وَقُطِّعَتِ الْبَقَرَةُ قِطَعًا قِطَعًا، وَأَهْدَيْنَا لَحْمَهَا إِلَىٰ جَمِيعِ الأَصْدِقَاءِ وَالأَقَارِبِ وَأَهْلِ الْقَرْيَةِ، فَأَكَلُوا وَشَبِعُوا وَشَكَرُوا الصَّيَّادِينَ.

I did not want anyone else to slaughter them, so I went and said "Bismillah" and slaughtered the two pigeons with my small, sharp knife. We returned to the village in the evening with a large catch. The cow was cut into pieces, and we gifted its meat to all friends, relatives, and the people of the village. They ate, were satisfied, and thanked the hunters.

## Grammatical Analysis (تحليل نحوي)

### 1. Verb Tenses and Narrative Style

The story is narrated in the **past tense (الماضي)**, consistent with Arabic storytelling. The narrator (Khalid) uses:

- **First person singular:** خَرَجْتُ، قُلْتُ، فَرِحْتُ، رَضِيتُ، ذَهَبْتُ
- **First person plural:** خَرَجْنَا، أَخَذْنَا، وَجَدْنَا، تَفَرَّقْنَا، رَجَعْنَا
- **Third person (for other characters):** صَوَّبَ، أَطْلَقَ، ذَبَحَهَا، تَأَسَّفَ

### 2. لَمْ نَزَلْ + مضارع (Continuous Past)

> وَلَمْ نَزَلْ نَمْشِيْ فِي الْحَرِّ

**لَمْ نَزَلْ** (we did not cease) + **نَمْشِي** (walking) = "We kept on walking." This is the negated form of **زَالَ/يَزَالُ** used with **لَمْ** to express a continuous action in the past. The muḍāri' verb after it functions as a ḥāl (state) or khabar (predicate).

### 3. لَمْ + مجزوم (Negated Past with Jussive)

> لَمْ تَطْلُعِ الشَّمْسُ

**لَمْ** negates the past and puts the muḍāri' verb into the **jussive mood (مجزوم)**. The original form تَطْلُعُ becomes تَطْلُعْ (with sukūn, but here with kasra due to the following hamzat al-waṣl in pronunciation).

### 4. لِنَتَغَدَّى — Purpose Clause (لام التعليل)

> وَأَخَذْنَا غَدَاءَنَا مَعَنَا لِنَتَغَدَّىٰ إِذَا غَلَبَنَا الْجُوْعُ

The **لام** here is **لام التعليل** (lām of purpose), causing the muḍāri' to take the **manṣūb (subjunctive)** form. نَتَغَدَّى is originally نَتَغَدَّيُ → the yā' is dropped in the subjunctive. Meaning: "so that we could eat lunch."

### 5. إِذَا — Conditional Particle

> لِنَتَغَدَّىٰ **إِذَا** غَلَبَنَا الْجُوْعُ

**إِذَا** introduces a **conditional clause for expected future events**. Here: "when/if hunger overcame us." The verb after إِذَا is ماضٍ (past tense) but indicates a future meaning.

### 6. Ḥāl (الحال) — Adverbial State

Several examples of the ḥāl construction appear:

| Arabic | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| مُبَكِّرِينَ | ḥāl of the subject of خَرَجْنَا — "we went out **early**" |
| جَرِيحًا | ḥāl of بَقَرَة — "it fell **wounded**" |
| تَضْرِبُ بِرِجْلَيْهَا | ḥāl jumliyya (sentential) — "**kicking** with its legs" |
| مُسْتَعِدًّا | ḥāl of السَّيِّدُ إِسْمَاعِيلُ — "Ismail was **ready**" |
| مُطْمَئِنِّينَ | ḥāl of the subject of كُنَّا — "we were **at ease**" |

### 7. إِذْ — Sudden/Unexpected Event

> وَكُنَّا نَتَكَلَّمُ وَكُنَّا مُطْمَئِنِّينَ **إِذْ** خَرَجَتْ بَقَرَةٌ أُخْرَىٰ

**إِذْ** here means "when suddenly" — it introduces an unexpected event that interrupts the established situation. This is a common narrative device in Arabic storytelling.

### 10. مَا + ماضٍ for Negation

> وَ**مَا** وَجَدْنَا طَعَامًا وَلَا مَاءً
> وَ**مَا** قَدَرَ أَنْ يُصَوِّبَ
> وَ**مَا** صَادَتِ الْبَقَرَةَ
> وَ**مَا** رَضِيتُ أَنْ يَذْبَحَهُمَا أَحَدٌ

**مَا** is used here to negate the **past tense verb** (مَا النَّافِيَة). This is an alternative to using لَمْ + مجزوم. Both negate the past, but مَا + ماضٍ is more common in narrative/literary Arabic.

### 11. أَنْ + مضارع منصوب (Subjunctive)

> نُرِيدُ **أَنْ نَرْجِعَ** — "we wanted **to return**"
> أُرِيدُ **أَنْ أَصِيدَ** — "I want **to hunt**"
> مَا قَدَرَ **أَنْ يُصَوِّبَ** — "he couldn't **aim**"
> مَا رَضِيتُ **أَنْ يَذْبَحَهُمَا** — "I didn't accept **that someone slaughter them**"

**أَنْ** puts the following muḍāri' verb into the **naṣb (subjunctive)** mood, indicated by fatḥa on the final root letter.

### 12. Passive Voice (المبني للمجهول)

> وَ**قُطِّعَتِ** الْبَقَرَةُ قِطَعًا قِطَعًا

**قُطِّعَتْ** is the passive of **قَطَّعَ** (Form II). The pattern is ضُمّة on the first letter, كسرة on the second-to-last. الْبَقَرَةُ is the **نائب الفاعل** (deputy agent, i.e. grammatical subject of the passive). The repetition **قِطَعًا قِطَعًا** ("pieces, pieces") is used for emphasis, meaning "into many pieces."

### 13. فَـ (Sequential Connector)

The story uses **فَـ** extensively to chain events in rapid sequence:

> فَصَوَّبَ... فَسَقَطَتْ... فَجَاؤُوا... فَأَطْلَقَ... فَأَخْطَأَتْ... فَذَبَحْتُ... فَأَعْطَانِي... فَأَصَبْتُ... فَأَكَلُوا

Unlike **وَ** (and), which simply joins, **فَ** implies **immediate succession or consequence** — "and so / and then / whereupon."

### 14. إِنَّ + Accusative (إِنَّ واسمها وخبرها)

> إِنَّ **خَالِدًا** **صَيَّادٌ**

- **إِنَّ**: emphatic particle ("indeed")
- **خَالِدًا**: ism of إِنَّ (accusative, marked by fatḥa + tanwīn)
- **صَيَّادٌ**: khabar of إِنَّ (nominative, marked by ḍamma + tanwīn)
- Translation: "Indeed, Khalid is a hunter."

## Summary

This is a first-person hunting narrative told by a boy named Khalid. He goes on a hunting trip with experienced hunters carrying rifles and knives. After a long, tiring walk in the heat, they hunt wild cattle, pigeons, and ducks. Khalid proves himself by successfully shooting two pigeons with a borrowed rifle. The group returns to the village in the evening, distributes the meat to friends, relatives, and villagers, and receives their gratitude.

The story is rich in intermediate-level grammar: dual forms, broken plurals, multiple verb forms (I–V, X), ḥāl constructions, conditional particles, passive voice, and the distinctive Arabic narrative style using فَـ for sequential events.
