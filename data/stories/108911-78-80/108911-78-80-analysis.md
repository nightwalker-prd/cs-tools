# Lesson 28 — البريد (The Mail) — Part 2

**Source:** Pages 78–80

---

## Full Text and Translation

### خالد:
> وَكَيْفَ يَكُوْنُ السَّاعِيْ يَا طَارِقُ؟ أَنَا مَا رَأَيْتُهُ قَطُّ؟

**Khalid:** And what does the postman look like, O Tariq? I have never seen him.

---

### طارق:
> أَمَا رَأَيْتَ رَجُلاً يَرْتَدِيْ حُلَّةً لَوْنُهَا رَمَادِيٌّ فِيْهَا أَزْرَارٌ نُحَاسِيَّةٌ وَعَلَىٰ رَأْسِهِ عِمَامَةٌ يَحْمِلُ حَقِيْبَةً مِنْ جِلْدٍ؟

**Tariq:** Haven't you seen a man wearing a grey suit with brass buttons on it, and on his head a turban, carrying a leather bag?

---

### خالد:
> نَعَمْ! قَدْ رَأَيْتُهُ وَرَأَيْتُ حَقِيْبَةً وَهِيَ مَلآنَةٌ بِالأَوْرَاقِ، وَأَزْرَارُهُ النُّحَاسِيَّةُ تَلْمَعُ وَعِمَامَتُهُ تَظْهَرُ مِنْ بَعِيْدٍ.

**Khalid:** Yes! I have seen him and I saw a bag — it was full of papers. His brass buttons gleam and his turban is visible from afar.

---

### طارق:
> ذٰلِكَ هُوَ سَاعِيْ البَرِيْدِ يَا خَالِدُ! وَالنَّاسُ يَنْتَظِرُوْنَهُ جِدًّا وَيَشْتَاقُوْنَ إِلَيْهِ خُصُوْصاً فِي القُرَىٰ، وَأَنْتَ تَنْتَظِرُهُ أَيْضاً إِذَا كَتَبْتَ كِتَاباً وَانْتَظَرْتَ جَوَابَهُ وَإِذَا طَلَبْتَ كِتَاباً مِنْ تَاجِرِ كُتُبٍ.

**Tariq:** That is the postman, O Khalid! People wait for him eagerly and long for him, especially in the villages. And you too wait for him when you have written a letter and are awaiting its reply, and when you have ordered a book from a book seller.

---

### خالد:
> وَرَأَيْتُ يَا طَارِقُ رَجُلاً آخَرَ يَرْتَدِيْ مِثْلَ حُلَّةِ السَّاعِيْ وَلَهُ أَزْرَارٌ نُحَاسِيَّةٌ وَعَلَىٰ رَأْسِهِ عِمَامَةٌ أَيْضاً وَلٰكِنْ لَيْسَ عِنْدَهُ حَقِيْبَةٌ مِنْ جِلْدٍ، وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ دَرَّاجَةٍ حَمْرَاءَ أَرَاهُ يَذْهَبُ بِسُرْعَةٍ كَأَنَّهُ مُسْتَعْجِلٌ.

**Khalid:** And I saw, O Tariq, another man wearing a suit like the postman's, with brass buttons, and on his head a turban as well — but he does not have a leather bag. He is on a red bicycle; I see him going quickly as if he is in a hurry.

---

### طارق:
> هُوَ أَيْضاً سَاعِيْ البَرِيْدِ وَلٰكِنَّهُ لا يُوَزِّعُ الكُتُبَ بَلْ يُوَزِّعُ البَرْقِيَّاتِ وَيَذْهَبُ عَلَىٰ دَرَّاجَةٍ حَمْرَاءَ مِنْ مَكَانٍ إِلَىٰ مَكَانٍ لِيَصِلَ البَرْقِيَّاتِ سَرِيْعاً، فَإِنَّ النَّاسَ لا يُرْسِلُوْنَ البَرْقِيَّةَ إِلَّا لِتَصِلَ سَرِيْعَةً، وَالنَّاسُ يَعْرِفُوْنَهُ بِدَرَّاجَتِهِ الحَمْرَاءِ.

**Tariq:** He is also a postman, but he does not deliver letters — rather, he delivers telegrams. He goes on a red bicycle from place to place to deliver the telegrams quickly, because people only send a telegram for it to arrive fast. And people recognize him by his red bicycle.

---

### خالد:
> وَلٰكِنْ كَيْفَ يَقْدِرُ رَجُلٌ وَاحِدٌ أَنْ يُوَزِّعَ الكُتُبَ فِيْ جَمِيْعِ أَنْحَاءِ المَدِيْنَةِ؟

**Khalid:** But how can one man distribute the letters throughout all parts of the city?

---

### طارق:
> لا! فِيْ مَكْتَبِ البَرِيْدِ رِجَالٌ كَثِيْرٌ وَلِكُلِّ نَاحِيَةٍ مِنْ أَنْحَاءِ المَدِيْنَةِ وَلِكُلِّ حَيٍّ مِنْ أَحْيَاءِ البَلَدِ سَاعٍ.

**Tariq:** No! In the post office there are many men, and for each area of the city and for each neighborhood of the town, there is a postman.

---

### خالد:
> وَكَيْفَ يُنْفِقُ مَكْتَبُ البَرِيْدِ الأَمْوَالَ الكَثِيْرَةَ وَكَيْفَ يَخْدِمُ مَجَّاناً؟ وَرِجَالُ البَرِيْدِ يَرْتَدُوْنَ حُلَلاً وَيَحْمِلُوْنَ حَقَائِبَ وَيَرْكَبُوْنَ دَرَّاجَاتٍ، فَمِنْ أَيْنَ تَأْتِيْ هٰذِهِ الأَمْوَالُ؟

**Khalid:** And how does the post office spend so much money, and how does it serve for free? The postmen wear suits and carry bags and ride bicycles — so where does this money come from?

---

### طارق:
> إِنَّ مَكْتَبَ البَرِيْدِ يَأْخُذُ أُجْرَةً مِنْ كُلِّ مَنْ يُرْسِلُ كِتَاباً بِالبَرِيْدِ.

**Tariq:** The post office takes a fee from everyone who sends a letter by mail.

---

### خالد:
> وَمَا هٰذِهِ الأُجْرَةُ وَمَتَىٰ تَدْفَعُهَا يَا أَخِيْ؟

**Khalid:** And what is this fee, and when do you pay it, O my brother?

---

### طارق:
> قَدْ اشْتَرَيْتُ هٰذَا الطَّابَعَ مِنْ مَكْتَبِ البَرِيْدِ وَهٰذِهِ هِيَ أُجْرَةُ البَرِيْدِ.

**Tariq:** I have bought this stamp from the post office, and this is the postal fee.

---

### خالد:
> أَشْكُرُكَ عَلَىٰ هٰذَا الحَدِيْثِ المُفِيْدِ، وَسَأَكْتُبُ إِلَىٰ أَخِيْ وَصَدِيْقِيْ جَمِيْل، وَسَأُرْسِلُ الكِتَابَ بِالبَرِيْدِ، وَإِذَا كَتَبْتُ الكِتَابَ جِئْتُكَ بِهِ فَتَرَاهُ وَتُصْلِحُهُ.

**Khalid:** I thank you for this useful conversation. I will write to my brother and my friend Jameel, and I will send the letter by mail. And when I have written the letter, I will bring it to you so you can see it and correct it.

---

### طارق:
> حُبًّا وَكَرَامَةً، يَسُرُّنِيْ أَنْ أُسَاعِدَكَ.

**Tariq:** With pleasure and honor — it delights me to help you.

---

## Grammatical Analysis (التحليل النحوي)

### 1. Negation with ما + Past Tense (النفي بـ"ما")

> **أَنَا مَا رَأَيْتُهُ قَطُّ**
> "I have never seen him"

- **ما** negates the past tense verb **رأيتُ** ("I saw").
- **قطّ** reinforces the negation, meaning "ever" → "never (at all)." It is exclusively used with past tense negation.

### 2. Interrogative Particle أَمَا (أَمَا الاستفهاميّة)

> **أَمَا رَأَيْتَ رَجُلاً...**
> "Haven't you seen a man..."

- **أَمَا** = **أ** (interrogative hamza) + **مَا** (negation), forming a rhetorical question expecting a "yes" answer.
- Equivalent to English "Haven't you...?" or "Surely you have seen..."

### 3. حال (Circumstantial Clauses)

Several حال constructions appear:

> **يَرْتَدِي حُلَّةً** — verbal حال describing the man (رجلاً)
> "wearing a suit"

> **وَهِيَ مَلآنَةٌ بِالأَوْرَاقِ** — nominal حال (واو الحال + جملة اسميّة)
> "while it was full of papers"

> **أَرَاهُ يَذْهَبُ بِسُرْعَةٍ** — verbal حال after the verb of perception أرى
> "I see him going quickly"

### 4. إضافة (Construct Chains / Iḍāfa)

The text is rich with iḍāfa constructions:

### 5. Verb Forms and Derived Patterns

The text showcases several augmented verb forms:

| Form | Example | Root | Meaning Added |
|------|---------|------|---------------|
| **Form II** (فَعَّلَ) | يُوَزِّعُ (distributes) | و ز ع | Intensive/causative — to distribute widely |
| **Form III** (فَاعَلَ) | يُسَاعِدُ (helps) | س ع د | Reciprocal/interaction — to assist |
| **Form IV** (أَفْعَلَ) | يُنْفِقُ (spends), يُرْسِلُ (sends), يُصْلِحُ (corrects) | Various | Causative/transitive |
| **Form VIII** (اِفْتَعَلَ) | يَرْتَدِي (wears), يَنْتَظِرُ (waits), يَشْتَاقُ (longs for), اِشْتَرَى (bought) | Various | Reflexive/middle voice |

### 6. إذا الشرطيّة (Conditional إذا)

> **إِذَا كَتَبْتَ كِتَاباً وَانْتَظَرْتَ جَوَابَهُ**
> "When/if you have written a letter and awaited its reply"

> **وَإِذَا كَتَبْتُ الكِتَابَ جِئْتُكَ بِهِ فَتَرَاهُ وَتُصْلِحُهُ**
> "And when I have written the letter, I will bring it to you so you can see it and correct it"

- **إذا** introduces a temporal/conditional clause.
- The condition uses the past tense (كتبتَ / كتبتُ) with future meaning ("when you write / when I write").
- The result clause follows with past tense or فـ + مضارع.

### 7. الاستثناء بـ"إلّا" (Exception with إلّا)

> **لا يُرْسِلُوْنَ البَرْقِيَّةَ إِلَّا لِتَصِلَ سَرِيْعَةً**
> "They only send a telegram for it to arrive quickly"

- **لا...إلّا** = "only" (restriction/حصر).
- **لِتَصِلَ** = لام التعليل + مضارع منصوب (subjunctive after lām of purpose).

### 8. كأنّ (Simile Particle)

> **كَأَنَّهُ مُسْتَعْجِلٌ**
> "As if he is in a hurry"

- **كأنّ** is one of the sisters of إنّ (أخوات إنّ). It takes a noun in the accusative (here: the attached pronoun ـه) and a predicate (مستعجل).
- **مُسْتَعْجِل** is an اسم فاعل from Form X (اِسْتَفْعَلَ), meaning "one who is hurrying/in haste."

### 9. لِكُلّ Construction (Universal Distribution)

> **وَلِكُلِّ نَاحِيَةٍ مِنْ أَنْحَاءِ المَدِيْنَةِ وَلِكُلِّ حَيٍّ مِنْ أَحْيَاءِ البَلَدِ سَاعٍ**
> "For each area of the city and for each neighborhood of the town, there is a postman"

- **لِكُلّ** = لام الجرّ + كُلّ (in iḍāfa).
- **سَاعٍ** is a defective noun (اسم منقوص) — the final ي drops when it is indefinite and in the nominative or genitive case (ساعٍ instead of السَّاعِي).

### 10. سَـ (Future Particle)

> **سَأَكْتُبُ** — "I will write"
> **سَأُرْسِلُ** — "I will send"

- **سَـ** is the shortened future particle (equivalent to سَوْفَ), indicating near-future intention.
- Attached directly to the مضارع (imperfect) verb.

### 11. فـ (Connective فاء / فاء السببيّة)

> **فَمِنْ أَيْنَ تَأْتِيْ هٰذِهِ الأَمْوَالُ؟**
> "So where does this money come from?"

- **فـ** here is the فاء of result/consequence — it draws a logical conclusion from what preceded.

> **فَتَرَاهُ وَتُصْلِحُهُ**
> "so that you see it and correct it"

- **فـ** here is فاء السببيّة (فاء of purpose/result) after a conditional clause.

## Key Themes

1. **The postal system** — how mail is delivered, the role of the postman, and the distinction between letter carriers and telegram couriers.
2. **Describing people by appearance** — clothing (suit, turban), equipment (bag, bicycle), and behavior (hurrying).
3. **Economics of public services** — how the post office funds itself through stamps/fees.
4. **Village vs. city life** — the eagerness of villagers for the postman highlights the importance of mail in rural areas.
5. **Friendship and mutual help** — the dialogue ends with Khalid asking Tariq to review his letter, and Tariq agreeing with warmth (حُبًّا وَكَرَامَةً).
