# (١٣) الأمانة — Trustworthiness

**Source:** Pages 45–46

---

## Text & Translation

### Paragraph 1

> اسْتَأْجَرَ رَجُلٌ قَوْمًا ، فَاشْتَغَلُوْا وَعَمِلُوْا ، وَلَمَّا فَرَغُوْا مِنْ شُغْلِهِمْ جَاؤُوا إِلَيْهِ فَأَعْطَاهُمْ أُجْرَهُمْ ، وَكَانَ فِيهِمْ رَجُلٌ اشْتَغَلَ مَعَهُمْ ، وَلَكِنَّهُ لَمْ يَأْخُذْ أُجْرَهُ وَتَرَكَهُ وَذَهَبَ .

A man hired some people, and they worked and labored. When they finished their work, they came to him and he gave them their wages. Among them was a man who had worked with them, but he did not take his wages — he left them and went away.

### Paragraph 2

> وَكَانَ الرَّجُلُ كَرِيمًا أَمِينًا ، فَلَمْ يَأْكُلْ أُجْرَتَهُ ، وَلَمْ يَنْتَفِعْ بِهَا ، وَخَافَ اللهَ وَوَضَعَهَا فِي التِّجَارَةِ ، وَثَمَّرَهَا وَأَثْمَرَتِ الأُجْرَةُ كَثِيرًا ، وَكَثُرَتْ مِنْهَا الأَمْوَالُ .

The man (the employer) was generous and trustworthy. He did not consume his wages, nor did he benefit from them himself. He feared Allah and invested them in trade. He grew them, and the wages yielded great profit, and wealth multiplied from them.

### Paragraph 3

> وَبَعْدَ حِينٍ جَاءَهُ الأَجِيرُ وَهُوَ خَائِفٌ أَنْ لَا يَعْرِفَهُ الرَّجُلُ ، فَقَدْ طَالَتِ المُدَّةُ ، وَمَضَى زَمَنٌ كَثِيرٌ ، وَمَاذَا يَفْعَلُ المِسْكِينُ إِنْ لَمْ يَعْرِفْهُ الرَّجُلُ أَوْ نَسِيَ قِصَّتَهُ .

After some time, the hired worker came to him, afraid that the man would not recognize him — for the period had been long and much time had passed. What would the poor man do if the man did not recognize him or had forgotten his story?

### Paragraph 4

> جَاءَ الأَجِيرُ وَهُوَ لَا يَطْمَعُ إِلَّا فِي أُجْرَتِهِ القَلِيلَةِ دَرَاهِمَ مَعْدُودَةٍ ، وَإِذَا جَحَدَهَا الرَّجُلُ وَلَمْ يَدْفَعْهَا رَجَعَ خَائِبًا .

The hired worker came expecting nothing more than his small wages — a few counted dirhams. And if the man denied them and did not pay them, he would return disappointed.

### Paragraph 5

> وَلَكِنَّهُ جَاءَهُ لِأَنَّهُ يَحْتَاجُ إِلَى هَذِهِ الدَّرَاهِمِ، فَقَالَ: يَا عَبْدَ اللهِ! أَدِّ إِلَيَّ أُجْرِي ، فَمَا جَحَدَ الرَّجُلُ وَمَا أَنْكَرَ ، بَلْ قَالَ: كُلُّ مَا تَرَى مِنَ الإِبِلِ وَالبَقَرِ وَالغَنَمِ وَالرَّقِيقِ مِنْ أُجْرِكَ .

But he came to him because he needed these dirhams. He said: "O servant of Allah! Give me my wages." The man did not deny it nor refuse. Rather, he said: "All that you see of camels, cattle, sheep, and slaves — it is all from your wages."

### Paragraph 6

> دُهِشَ الرَّجُلُ وَتَحَيَّرَ وَظَنَّ أَنَّهُ يَسْتَهْزِئُ بِهِ ، فَقَالَ: يَا عَبْدَ اللهِ لَا تَسْتَهْزِئْ بِي .

The man was astonished and bewildered. He thought he was mocking him, so he said: "O servant of Allah, do not mock me!"

### Paragraph 7

> قَالَ الرَّجُلُ: لَا أَسْتَهْزِئُ بِكَ ، فَكُلُّ مَا تَرَى مِنَ الإِبِلِ وَالبَقَرِ وَالغَنَمِ وَالرَّقِيقِ لَكَ ، فَإِنِّي قَدْ وَضَعْتُ أُجْرَتَكَ فِي التِّجَارَةِ ، وَثَمَّرْتُهَا وَأَثْمَرَتْ هَذِهِ الإِبِلَ وَالبَقَرَ وَالغَنَمَ وَالرَّقِيقَ .

The man said: "I am not mocking you. All that you see of camels, cattle, sheep, and slaves is yours. For indeed, I invested your wages in trade and grew them, and they yielded these camels, cattle, sheep, and slaves."

### Paragraph 8

> فَأَخَذَ الأَجِيرُ الإِبِلَ وَالبَقَرَ وَالغَنَمَ وَالرَّقِيقَ وَلَمْ يَتْرُكْ مِنْهَا شَيْئًا .

So the hired worker took the camels, the cattle, the sheep, and the slaves, and did not leave any of them behind.

### Paragraph 9

> وَقَدْ رَضِيَ اللهُ عَنْهُ عَلَى هَذِهِ الأَمَانَةِ وَالوَفَاءِ وَالكَرَمِ .

And Allah was pleased with him for this trustworthiness, faithfulness, and generosity.

### Paragraph 10

> وَقَدْ وَقَعَ هَذَا الرَّجُلُ الأَمِينُ مَرَّةً فِي غَارٍ ، وَانْطَبَقَتْ عَلَيْهِ صَخْرَةٌ ، فَلَمَّا يَئِسَ مِنَ الحَيَاةِ دَعَا اللهَ بِهَذَا العَمَلِ الصَّالِحِ ، وَقَالَ: اللَّهُمَّ إِنْ كُنْتَ فَعَلْتُ ذَلِكَ ابْتِغَاءَ وَجْهِكَ ، فَاكْشِفْ عَنَّا هَذِهِ الصَّخْرَةَ ، فَأَجَابَ اللهُ دَعْوَتَهُ وَأَعَانَهُ .

This trustworthy man once found himself in a cave, and a rock sealed shut upon him. When he despaired of life, he called upon Allah by this righteous deed and said: "O Allah, if I did that seeking Your pleasure, then remove this rock from us." So Allah answered his supplication and aided him.

## Grammatical Analysis

### 1. Verb Form Usage (أوزان الأفعال)

This text is rich in derived verb forms, showcasing how Arabic builds meaning from roots:

| Form | Example | Function |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Form I** (فَعَلَ) | تَرَكَ، خَافَ، وَضَعَ | Base meanings: left, feared, placed |
| **Form II** (فَعَّلَ) | ثَمَّرَ | Causative: "made fruitful" (caused profit) |
| **Form IV** (أَفْعَلَ) | أَعْطَى، أَثْمَرَ، أَنْكَرَ، أَجَابَ، أَعَانَ | Causative/transitive: gave, bore fruit, denied, answered, aided |
| **Form V** (تَفَعَّلَ) | تَحَيَّرَ | Reflexive of II: "became bewildered" |
| **Form VII** (اِنْفَعَلَ) | اِنْطَبَقَ | Passive/reflexive: "closed shut (on its own)" |
| **Form VIII** (اِفْتَعَلَ) | اِشْتَغَلَ، اِنْتَفَعَ، اِحْتَاجَ | Reflexive: worked, benefited, needed |
| **Form X** (اِسْتَفْعَلَ) | اِسْتَأْجَرَ، اِسْتَهْزَأَ | Seeking/requesting: sought to hire, sought to mock |

**Note the Form II / Form IV pair:** ثَمَّرَ (II) = "he grew/invested it" (transitive, human agent) vs. أَثْمَرَ (IV) = "it bore fruit" (intransitive, the money as subject). This is a classic Arabic pattern where II is the deliberate human action and IV is the resultant state.

### 2. Negation Patterns

The text demonstrates three negation strategies:

- **لَمْ + مجزوم (jussive):** لَمْ يَأْخُذْ، لَمْ يَأْكُلْ، لَمْ يَنْتَفِعْ — negating completed past actions
- **لَا + مضارع (imperfect):** لَا تَسْتَهْزِئْ — prohibition (نهي), with jussive mood
- **مَا + ماضٍ (past):** فَمَا جَحَدَ وَمَا أَنْكَرَ — negating past actions (more emphatic/literary than لَمْ)

### 3. كَانَ and Its Predicate (خبر كان)

- **كَانَ الرَّجُلُ كَرِيمًا أَمِينًا** — كان takes a nominative subject (الرَّجُلُ) and accusative predicate (كَرِيمًا أَمِينًا). Two accusative adjectives serve as compound predicate.
- **كَانَ فِيهِمْ رَجُلٌ** — Here the prepositional phrase فِيهِمْ is the predicate (خبر مقدم) and رَجُلٌ is the delayed subject (اسم مؤخر). This inverted structure is common when introducing new/indefinite subjects.

### 4. Conditional Structures (الشرط)

- **إِنْ لَمْ يَعْرِفْهُ** — إِنْ + لَمْ + jussive: real conditional about a possible future
- **إِنْ كُنْتَ فَعَلْتُ ذَلِكَ … فَاكْشِفْ** — إِنْ + كان: conditional with a past-tense protasis and an imperative apodosis (جواب الشرط). The فَـ before the imperative is obligatory here.
- **وَإِذَا جَحَدَهَا … رَجَعَ خَائِبًا** — إِذَا + past: hypothetical conditional

### 5. الحال (Circumstantial/Haal Clauses)

The text uses حال structures extensively:

- **وَهُوَ خَائِفٌ** — nominal haal clause: "while he was afraid"
- **وَهُوَ لَا يَطْمَعُ إِلَّا فِي أُجْرَتِهِ** — nominal haal with negation + restriction: "while he hoped for nothing but his wages"
- **خَائِبًا** — single-word accusative haal: "disappointed" (describing the state of the subject when returning)

### 6. المفعول لأجله (Adverbial Accusative of Purpose)

- **ابْتِغَاءَ وَجْهِكَ** — accusative of purpose: "seeking Your pleasure." This is a مفعول لأجله — the reason/motivation for the action, in the accusative case.

### 7. فَـ as Narrative Sequencer

The فَـ particle appears throughout as a discourse connector with causal/sequential force:

- **فَاشْتَغَلُوا** — "and so they worked" (result of hiring)
- **فَأَعْطَاهُمْ** — "and so he gave them" (result of coming)
- **فَأَخَذَ** — "and so he took" (result of the explanation)
- **فَأَجَابَ اللهُ** — "and so Allah answered" (result of the supplication)

This contrasts with وَ which is purely conjunctive ("and") without implying sequence or consequence.

### 8. إِنَّ and Its Sisters

- **لَكِنَّهُ لَمْ يَأْخُذْ** — لَكِنَّ + attached pronoun (اسم لكنّ) + verbal sentence (خبر لكنّ)
- **فَإِنِّي قَدْ وَضَعْتُ** — إنَّ for emphasis: "indeed I have invested"
- **لِأَنَّهُ يَحْتَاجُ** — لِأَنَّ introducing a causal clause

### 9. Vocabulary of Livestock (Cultural Note)

The four categories of wealth — الإِبِل (camels), البَقَر (cattle), الغَنَم (sheep/goats), الرَّقِيق (slaves) — represent the traditional measures of wealth in early Arabian society. Their repetition four times in the text emphasizes the enormity of what the small wages grew into, creating rhetorical impact through accumulation (تَكْرار).

### 10. The Hadith Connection

This story is based on the famous hadith of the three men trapped in a cave (حديث الثلاثة الذين آواهم الغار), reported in Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim. Each man called upon Allah through a righteous deed (عمل صالح) to remove the rock. This passage tells the story of the man whose good deed was his trustworthiness (أمانة) in preserving and growing the wages of his hired worker.
