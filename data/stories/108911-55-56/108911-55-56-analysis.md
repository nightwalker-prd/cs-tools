# (١٧) فَضِيْلَةُ الشُّغْلِ — The Virtue of Work

**Source:** Pages 55–56, Story #17

---

## Full Arabic Text

> إِنَّ رَجُلاً مِنَ الأَنْصَارِ أَتَى النَّبِيَّ ﷺ يَسْأَلُهُ ، فَقَالَ: أَمَا فِيْ بَيْتِكَ شَيْءٌ؟
>
> قَالَ: بَلَىْ حِلْسٌ نَلْبَسُ بَعْضَهُ ، وَنَبْسُطُ بَعْضَهُ وَقَعْبٌ نَشْرَبُ فِيْهِ مِنَ الْمَاءِ.
>
> قَالَ: ائْتِنِيْ بِهِمَا.
>
> فَأَخَذَهُمَا رَسُوْلُ اللهِ ﷺ بِيَدِهِ وَقَالَ: مَنْ يَشْتَرِيْ هٰذَيْنِ؟
>
> قَالَ رَجُلٌ: أَنَا آخُذُهُمَا بِدِرْهَمٍ.
>
> قَالَ: مَنْ يَزِيْدُ عَلَىْ دِرْهَمٍ؟ مَنْ يَزِيْدُ عَلَىْ دِرْهَمٍ؟
>
> قَالَ رَجُلٌ: أَنَا آخُذُهُمَا بِدِرْهَمَيْنِ.
>
> فَأَعْطَاهُمَا إِيَّاهُ وَأَخَذَ الدِّرْهَمَيْنِ فَأَعْطَاهُمَا الأَنْصَارِيَّ وَقَالَ:
>
> اشْتَرِ بِأَحَدِهِمَا طَعَامًا فَانْبِذْهُ إِلَىْ أَهْلِكَ ، وَاشْتَرِ بِالآخَرِ قَدُوْمًا فَائْتِنِيْ بِهِ.
>
> فَأَتَاهُ بِهِ فَشَدَّ فِيْهِ رَسُوْلُ اللهِ ﷺ عُوْدًا بِيَدِهِ.
>
> ثُمَّ قَالَ لَهُ: اذْهَبْ فَاحْتَطِبْ وَبِعْ وَلَا أَرِيَنَّكَ خَمْسَةَ عَشَرَ يَوْمًا.
>
> فَذَهَبَ الرَّجُلُ يَحْتَطِبُ وَيَبِيْعُ ، فَجَاءَ وَقَدْ أَصَابَ عَشَرَةَ دَرَاهِمَ ، فَاشْتَرَىْ بِبَعْضِهَا ثَوْبًا وَبِبَعْضِهَا طَعَامًا.
>
> فَقَالَ رَسُوْلُ اللهِ ﷺ: هٰذَا خَيْرٌ لَكَ مِنْ أَنْ تَجِيْءَ الْمَسْأَلَةُ نُكْتَةً فِيْ وَجْهِكَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ.

---

## English Translation

Indeed, a man from the Ansar (the Medinan helpers) came to the Prophet ﷺ asking him (for charity). So he [the Prophet] said: "Do you not have anything in your house?"

He said: "Yes — a coarse cloth, part of which we wear and part of which we spread (on the ground), and a wooden bowl from which we drink water."

He [the Prophet] said: "Bring them both to me."

So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ took them both in his hand and said: "Who will buy these two?"

A man said: "I will take them for one dirham."

He said: "Who will offer more than one dirham? Who will offer more than one dirham?"

A man said: "I will take them for two dirhams."

So he gave them (the two items) to him (the buyer) and took the two dirhams, then gave them to the Ansari man and said:

"Buy food with one of them and send it to your family, and buy an axe with the other and bring it to me."

So he brought it to him, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ fastened a wooden handle into it with his own hand.

Then he said to him: "Go, gather firewood and sell it, and do not let me see you for fifteen days."

So the man went gathering firewood and selling it. He came back having earned ten dirhams, and with some of it he bought clothing and with some of it he bought food.

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "This is better for you than begging coming as a mark of disgrace on your face on the Day of Judgment."

## Grammatical Analysis (تحليل نحوي)

### 1. إِنَّ رَجُلاً مِنَ الأَنْصَارِ أَتَى النَّبِيَّ يَسْأَلُهُ
- **إِنَّ** — particle of emphasis (حرف توكيد ونصب), puts its subject (اسم إنّ) into the accusative.
- **رَجُلاً** — اسم إنّ, accusative indefinite (منصوب بالفتحة). The tanwīn on an indefinite noun.
- **مِنَ الأَنْصَارِ** — prepositional phrase (جار ومجرور) acting as an adjective (صفة) for رَجُلاً.
- **أَتَى** — past tense verb (فعل ماضٍ), Form I from أ ت ي. The subject is a hidden pronoun (هو) referring back to رَجُلاً.
- **النَّبِيَّ** — direct object (مفعول به), accusative with fatḥa. Definite noun.
- **يَسْأَلُهُ** — present tense verb (فعل مضارع مرفوع), functioning as a **حال** (circumstantial clause) — "while asking him." The attached pronoun ـهُ is the direct object.

### 2. أَمَا فِيْ بَيْتِكَ شَيْءٌ؟
- **أَمَا** — interrogative particle (أ) + negation (ما) = "Is there not...?"
- **فِيْ بَيْتِكَ** — prepositional phrase (جار ومجرور), the noun بَيْت is in the genitive (مجرور بالكسرة), with the attached pronoun ـكَ (your).
- **شَيْءٌ** — nominative subject (مبتدأ مؤخّر), the prepositional phrase is the predicate (خبر مقدّم). This is an inverted nominal sentence.

### 3. بَلَىْ حِلْسٌ نَلْبَسُ بَعْضَهُ
- **بَلَى** — affirmative response to a negative question ("Yes, indeed").
- **حِلْسٌ** — nominative (مبتدأ), an indefinite noun starting a new sentence.
- **نَلْبَسُ بَعْضَهُ** — verbal sentence acting as the predicate (خبر) of حِلْس. بَعْضَهُ is the direct object (مفعول به) of نَلْبَسُ.

### 4. ائْتِنِيْ بِهِمَا
- **ائْتِنِيْ** — imperative (فعل أمر) from أَتَى (Form I). The ـنِيْ is the attached pronoun (me), object of the verb.
- **بِهِمَا** — prepositional phrase: بِ + هِمَا (dual pronoun = "with them both"). The dual pronoun refers to the حِلْس and the قَعْب.

### 5. مَنْ يَشْتَرِيْ هٰذَيْنِ؟
- **مَنْ** — interrogative pronoun (اسم استفهام), "who?"
- **يَشْتَرِيْ** — present tense, Form VIII (افتعل) from ش ر ي. Mudāri' marfū', with the ḍamma estimated on the yā' (الضمة المقدرة على الياء).
- **هٰذَيْنِ** — demonstrative pronoun, dual form, accusative/genitive (مثنى منصوب/مجرور بالياء). Functions as the direct object (مفعول به).

### 6. أَنَا آخُذُهُمَا بِدِرْهَمٍ / بِدِرْهَمَيْنِ
- **أَنَا** — detached pronoun, first person singular (ضمير منفصل), acting as the subject (مبتدأ).
- **آخُذُهُمَا** — verbal sentence as the predicate (خبر). The dual pronoun ـهُمَا is the direct object.
- **بِدِرْهَمٍ** — price expressed with the preposition بِ (بَاء الثَّمَن / the bā' of price). دِرْهَمٍ is singular genitive.
- **بِدِرْهَمَيْنِ** — same construction but dual: genitive dual marked with يْنِ (مجرور بالياء لأنه مثنى).

### 7. فَأَعْطَاهُمَا إِيَّاهُ
- **فَ** — conjunction (فاء عاطفة), connecting sequential events.
- **أَعْطَى** — past tense, Form IV (أَفْعَلَ) from ع ط ي. A **ditransitive** verb (يتعدى إلى مفعولين).
- **ـهُمَا** — first object (مفعول به أول) = "them both" (the items).
- **إِيَّاهُ** — detached accusative pronoun (ضمير منفصل في محل نصب), second object (مفعول به ثانٍ) = "to him" (the buyer). Used because two attached pronouns cannot be stacked on the same verb.

### 8. اشْتَرِ بِأَحَدِهِمَا طَعَامًا فَانْبِذْهُ إِلَىْ أَهْلِكَ
- **اشْتَرِ** — imperative, Form VIII from ش ر ي. The final yā' is dropped in the imperative (حذف حرف العلة).
- **بِأَحَدِهِمَا** — "with one of them two." أَحَد is in the genitive after بِ. The dual pronoun ـهِمَا specifies "of them both."
- **طَعَامًا** — direct object (مفعول به), accusative indefinite.
- **فَانْبِذْهُ** — imperative, Form VII (انفعل) from ن ب ذ. "Throw it / send it." The فَ here is فاء الترتيب (sequential فَ).

### 9. فَشَدَّ فِيْهِ رَسُوْلُ اللهِ عُوْدًا بِيَدِهِ
- **شَدَّ** — past tense, doubled verb (فعل ماضٍ مضعّف) from ش د د, "he fastened."
- **فِيْهِ** — "in it" (in the axe head).
- **رَسُوْلُ اللهِ** — subject (فاعل), nominative. اللهِ is muḍāf ilayhi (genitive).
- **عُوْدًا** — direct object (مفعول به), "a stick/handle."
- **بِيَدِهِ** — "with his hand" — emphasizing the Prophet did it himself.

### 10. لَا أَرِيَنَّكَ خَمْسَةَ عَشَرَ يَوْمًا
- **لَا أَرِيَنَّكَ** — emphatic prohibition/command: لَا + مضارع + نون التوكيد الثقيلة. Literally "let me not see you" = "don't let me see you (i.e., don't come back begging)."
- **خَمْسَةَ عَشَرَ** — compound number (عدد مركّب), indeclinable, built on fatḥ (مبني على الفتح). Accusative as ظرف زمان (adverb of time).
- **يَوْمًا** — tamyīz (تمييز) of the number, accusative singular. Numbers 11–99 take a singular accusative tamyīz.

### 11. فَذَهَبَ الرَّجُلُ يَحْتَطِبُ وَيَبِيْعُ
- **يَحْتَطِبُ** — present tense, Form VIII (افتعل) from ح ط ب, "he gathers firewood." Functions as حال (circumstantial description).
- **يَبِيْعُ** — present tense, Form I from ب ي ع, "he sells." Conjoined with وَ to the previous حال.

### 12. فَجَاءَ وَقَدْ أَصَابَ عَشَرَةَ دَرَاهِمَ
- **وَقَدْ أَصَابَ** — واو الحال + قَدْ + past tense. The entire clause is a حال (circumstantial clause) = "while he had already earned..."
- **عَشَرَةَ دَرَاهِمَ** — "ten dirhams." Numbers 3–10 take a broken plural in the genitive. دَرَاهِمَ is ممنوع من الصرف (diptote — broken plural pattern فَعَالِل), hence genitive is marked with fatḥa instead of kasra.

### 13. هٰذَا خَيْرٌ لَكَ مِنْ أَنْ تَجِيْءَ الْمَسْأَلَةُ نُكْتَةً فِيْ وَجْهِكَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ
- **هٰذَا خَيْرٌ** — nominal sentence: هٰذَا = مبتدأ, خَيْرٌ = خبر. Comparative (اسم تفضيل) meaning "better."
- **لَكَ** — "for you," prepositional phrase linked to خَيْر.
- **مِنْ أَنْ تَجِيْءَ** — مِنْ + أَنْ المصدرية + subjunctive verb. The أَنْ clause forms a مصدر مؤوّل ("than that begging comes...").
- **الْمَسْأَلَةُ** — subject (فاعل) of تَجِيْءَ, nominative. Here it means "begging/asking for charity."
- **نُكْتَةً** — حال (circumstantial accusative), "as a mark/blemish."
- **يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ** — ظرف زمان (adverb of time), accusative. الْقِيَامَةِ is muḍāf ilayhi.

## Key Grammatical Patterns Summary

| Pattern | Example | Explanation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **إِنَّ + accusative** | إِنَّ رَجُلاً | Emphasis particle putting its noun in accusative |
| **Dual pronouns** | بِهِمَا، هٰذَيْنِ، آخُذُهُمَا | Dual forms throughout referring to two items |
| **بَاء الثَّمَن** (price بِ) | بِدِرْهَمٍ، بِدِرْهَمَيْنِ | Preposition بِ expressing price |
| **Ditransitive verb** | أَعْطَاهُمَا إِيَّاهُ | Two objects: one attached, one detached |
| **حال (circumstantial)** | يَسْأَلُهُ، يَحْتَطِبُ، نُكْتَةً | Present tense or accusative noun describing state |
| **Compound numbers** | خَمْسَةَ عَشَرَ يَوْمًا | 11–99: singular accusative تمييز |
| **واو الحال + قَدْ** | وَقَدْ أَصَابَ | Past-tense circumstantial clause |
| **Diptote plural** | دَرَاهِمَ (not دَرَاهِمٍ) | Broken plural ممنوع من الصرف — genitive with fatḥa |
| **أَنْ المصدرية** | مِنْ أَنْ تَجِيْءَ | أَنْ + subjunctive forming a مصدر مؤوّل |
| **Form VIII verbs** | اشْتَرَى، احْتَطَبَ | افْتَعَلَ pattern — very common in this text |

## Thematic Notes

This hadith (reported by Abu Dawud and others) illustrates a core Islamic economic principle: **earning one's livelihood through honest labor is superior to begging**. The Prophet ﷺ did not simply give the man charity. Instead he:

1. **Assessed** his situation (asked what he owned)
2. **Created capital** (auctioned the man's belongings to raise seed money)
3. **Invested** wisely (split funds between immediate needs and a tool for production)
4. **Personally equipped** him (fixed the axe handle himself)
5. **Set a goal** (fifteen days of work)
6. **Affirmed the result** (praised honest earnings over begging)

This is essentially a **prophetic model for economic empowerment** — not a handout, but a hand up.
