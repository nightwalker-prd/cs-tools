# Story 29: Who Will Place the Stone?

# مَنْ يَضَعُ الْحَجَرَ؟

**Source:** Pages 81–83

---

## Arabic Text & English Translation

### Section 1 (Page 81)

**Arabic:**

> إِنَّكُمْ لَتَعْرِفُونَ هذَا الْبِنَاءَ، وَمَنْ فِي الدُّنْيَا لَا يَعْرِفُ هذَا الْبِنَاءَ؟ إِنَّكُمْ تَتَوَجَّهُونَ إِلَيْهِ فِي الصَّلَاةِ، وَيُسَافِرُ إِلَيْهِ الْمُسْلِمُونَ مِنْ كُلِّ جَانِبٍ وَيَطُوفُونَ فِي الْحَجِّ.

**Translation:**

Indeed you know this building — and who in the world does not know this building? You face towards it in prayer, and Muslims travel to it from every direction and perform tawaf (circumambulation) during Hajj.

---

> الْكَعْبَةُ أَوَّلُ بَيْتٍ وُضِعَ لِلنَّاسِ لِعِبَادَةِ اللهِ، بَنَاهَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ خَلِيلُ اللهِ فِي مَكَّةَ وَفِيهَا حَجَرٌ أَسْوَدُ يُقَبِّلُهُ النَّاسُ فِي الْحَجِّ وَكَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يُقَبِّلُهُ.

**Translation:**

The Ka'bah is the first house established for mankind for the worship of Allah. Ibrahim, the Friend of Allah (Khalil Allah), built it in Makkah. In it is a Black Stone which people kiss during Hajj, and the Prophet ﷺ used to kiss it.

---

### Section 2 (Page 82)

**Arabic:**

> وَبَعْدَ زَمَنٍ طَوِيلٍ أَرَادَ أَوْلَادُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَهُمْ قُرَيْشٌ أَنْ يَبْنُوا بِنَاءَ الْكَعْبَةِ مِنْ جَدِيدٍ فَإِنَّهُ كَانَ بِنَاءً قَدِيمًا قَدْ سَقَطَ سَقْفُهُ، وَضَعُفَتْ جُدْرَانُهُ، فَجَمَعَتْ قُرَيْشٌ الْحِجَارَةَ وَالْخَشَبَ لِبِنَائِهَا وَبَنَتْ قُرَيْشٌ بِنَاءَ الْكَعْبَةِ مِنْ جَدِيدٍ.

**Translation:**

After a long time, the descendants of Ibrahim — who are Quraysh — wanted to rebuild the Ka'bah anew, for it was an old structure whose roof had fallen and whose walls had weakened. So Quraysh gathered stones and wood for its construction, and Quraysh built the Ka'bah anew.

---

> وَلَمَّا تَمَّ بِنَاءُ الْكَعْبَةِ أَرَادَتْ قُرَيْشٌ أَنْ تَضَعَ الْحَجَرَ الْأَسْوَدَ فِي مَحَلِّهِ فَاخْتَصَمَتْ قُرَيْشٌ فِي وَضْعِ الْحَجَرِ الْأَسْوَدِ فِي مَحَلِّهِ، كُلُّ قَبِيلَةٍ تُرِيدُ أَنْ تَرْفَعَهُ إِلَى مَوْضِعِهِ لِأَنَّهُ شَرَفٌ عَظِيمٌ، كُلُّ قَبِيلَةٍ حَرِيصَةٌ عَلَى أَنْ تَنَالَ هذَا الشَّرَفَ.

**Translation:**

When the construction of the Ka'bah was complete, Quraysh wanted to place the Black Stone in its position. But Quraysh quarrelled over the placing of the Black Stone in its position — every tribe wanted to lift it to its place because it was a great honour. Every tribe was eager to attain this honour.

---

> كُلُّ قَبِيلَةٍ حَرِيصَةٌ عَلَى أَنْ تَنَالَ هذَا الشَّرَفَ وَلَكِنَّ ذَلِكَ لَا يُمْكِنُ لِأَنَّ الْحَجَرَ وَاحِدٌ وَالْقَبَائِلَ كَثِيرَةٌ.

**Translation:**

Every tribe was eager to attain this honour, but that was not possible because the stone was one and the tribes were many.

---

> وَاخْتَلَفَتْ قُرَيْشٌ كَثِيرًا وَتَنَازَعَتْ وَكَانَ الْعَرَبُ يُقَاتِلُونَ لِأَدْنَى شَيْءٍ وَلِغَيْرِ شَيْءٍ، يَتَقَدَّمُ فَرَسٌ فَيُقَاتِلُونَ، وَيَسْبِقُ أَحَدٌ فَيَسْقِي فَرَسَهُ أَوْ بَعِيرَهُ فَيَقْتُلُونَ وَيُقْتَلُونَ، وَلَا يَزَالُونَ يُقَاتِلُونَ أَرْبَعِينَ سَنَةً وَخَمْسِينَ سَنَةً، فَلِمَاذَا لَا يُقَاتِلُونَ عَلَى هذَا الشَّرَفِ؟ وَإِنَّهُ لَشَرَفٌ عَظِيمٌ.

**Translation:**

Quraysh disagreed greatly and quarrelled. The Arabs used to fight over the slightest thing — and over nothing at all. A horse would advance, and they would fight. Someone would get ahead and water his horse or his camel, and they would kill and be killed. They would continue fighting for forty and fifty years. So why would they not fight over this honour? And indeed it was a great honour.

---

> وَقَرَّبَتْ قَبِيلَةٌ مِنْ قُرَيْشٍ جَفْنَةً مَمْلُوءَةً دَمًا ثُمَّ تَحَالَفَتْ مَعَ قَبِيلَةٍ أُخْرَى عَلَى الْمَوْتِ وَأَدْخَلُوا أَيْدِيَهُمْ فِي ذَلِكَ الدَّمِ وَقَالُوا: لَا نَتْرُكُ هذَا الشَّرَفَ أَوْ نَمُوتَ.

**Translation:**

A tribe from Quraysh brought forth a large bowl filled with blood, then made a pact with another tribe — a pact unto death. They dipped their hands into that blood and said: "We will not abandon this honour, or we will die."

---

### Section 3 (Page 83)

**Arabic:**

> وَكَانَ هذَا شَرًّا كَبِيرًا وَخَطَرًا عَظِيمًا وَالْمَوْتُ شَيْءٌ هَيِّنٌ لِلْعَرَبِ فِي سَبِيلِ الْحَقِّ وَالشَّرَفِ.

**Translation:**

This was a great evil and an enormous danger. Death was a trivial thing for the Arabs in the pursuit of truth and honour.

---

> إِذًا لَا بُدَّ مِنَ الْحَرْبِ، وَالْحَرْبُ مَشْؤُومَةٌ جِدًّا.

**Translation:**

So war was inevitable — and war is very ominous indeed.

## Grammatical Analysis (التحليل النحوي)

### 1. إنَّ and Its Sisters (إنَّ وأخواتها)

The text uses **إِنَّ** (indeed/verily) extensively — a particle that enters upon the nominal sentence, putting its subject (اسم إنّ) in the accusative:

| Instance | اسم إنّ | خبر إنّ |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **إِنَّكُمْ** لَتَعْرِفُونَ | ـكُمْ (attached pronoun, accusative) | لَتَعْرِفُونَ (verbal sentence as predicate) |
| **إِنَّكُمْ** تَتَوَجَّهُونَ | ـكُمْ | تَتَوَجَّهُونَ |
| **فَإِنَّهُ** كَانَ بِنَاءً | ـهُ | كَانَ بِنَاءً قَدِيمًا |
| **لِأَنَّهُ** شَرَفٌ عَظِيمٌ | ـهُ | شَرَفٌ عَظِيمٌ |
| **وَإِنَّهُ** لَشَرَفٌ عَظِيمٌ | ـهُ | لَشَرَفٌ عَظِيمٌ |
| **لِأَنَّ** الْحَجَرَ وَاحِدٌ | الْحَجَرَ (accusative) | وَاحِدٌ |
| **وَلَكِنَّ** ذَلِكَ لَا يُمْكِنُ | ذَلِكَ | لَا يُمْكِنُ |

Note the **لام التوكيد** (emphatic lām) in **لَتَعْرِفُونَ** and **لَشَرَفٌ**, reinforcing the emphasis of إنّ.

### 2. Verb Forms and Patterns (الأوزان)

| Verb | Form | Pattern | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|---------|
| يَعْرِفُ | I | يَفْعِلُ | to know |
| تَتَوَجَّهُونَ | V (تَفَعَّلَ) | يَتَفَعَّلُ | to face towards (reflexive of وَجَّهَ) |
| يُسَافِرُ | III (فَاعَلَ) | يُفَاعِلُ | to travel |
| يَطُوفُونَ | I (hollow) | يَفْعُلُ | to circumambulate |
| يُقَبِّلُ | II (فَعَّلَ) | يُفَعِّلُ | to kiss (intensive) |
| اخْتَصَمَتْ | VIII (افْتَعَلَ) | يَفْتَعِلُ | to quarrel with each other (reciprocal) |
| اخْتَلَفَتْ | VIII (افْتَعَلَ) | يَفْتَعِلُ | to disagree with each other |
| تَنَازَعَتْ | VI (تَفَاعَلَ) | يَتَفَاعَلُ | to dispute with each other (mutual) |
| يُقَاتِلُونَ | III (فَاعَلَ) | يُفَاعِلُ | to fight each other |
| تَحَالَفَتْ | VI (تَفَاعَلَ) | يَتَفَاعَلُ | to form an alliance (mutual) |
| يُقْتَلُونَ | I passive | يُفْعَلُونَ | to be killed |

**Key observation:** Forms VI (تَفَاعَلَ) and VIII (افْتَعَلَ) dominate the conflict sections, reflecting their **reciprocal/mutual** meaning — the tribes are acting *against each other*.

### 3. Passive Voice (المبني للمجهول)

- **وُضِعَ** لِلنَّاسِ — "was established for people" (Form I passive of وَضَعَ)
- **يُقْتَلُونَ** — "they are killed" (Form I passive of قَتَلَ)

### 4. لَمَّا (Temporal Conjunction)

> **وَلَمَّا** تَمَّ بِنَاءُ الْكَعْبَةِ أَرَادَتْ قُرَيْشٌ...

**لَمَّا** = "when" (past temporal clause). It requires a past-tense verb in both its clause and its answer (جواب لمّا). Structure: **لمّا + فعل ماضٍ ... فعل ماضٍ**.

### 5. أَنْ + Subjunctive (أنْ المصدرية)

The text repeatedly uses **أَنْ** followed by a verb in the **subjunctive mood** (منصوب):

| Phrase | Subjunctive Marker |
|--------|--------------------|
| أَنْ **يَبْنُوا** | حذف النون (deletion of ن from يبنون) |
| أَنْ **تَضَعَ** | فتحة on final letter |
| أَنْ **تَرْفَعَهُ** | فتحة on final letter |
| أَنْ **تَنَالَ** | فتحة on final letter |

### 6. كُلّ (Quantifier) — Muḍāf Structure

**كُلّ** appears in iḍāfah (possessive construction) throughout:

- **كُلِّ جَانِبٍ** — "every direction" (كلّ as muḍāf + indefinite muḍāf ilayh)
- **كُلُّ قَبِيلَةٍ** — "every tribe" (repeated 3 times for rhetorical emphasis)

### 7. Broken Plurals (جمع التكسير)

| Singular | Plural | Pattern |
|----------|--------|---------|
| وَلَد | أَوْلَاد | أَفْعَال |
| جِدَار | جُدْرَان | فُعْلَان |
| حَجَر | حِجَارَة | فِعَالَة |
| قَبِيلَة | قَبَائِل | فَعَائِل |
| يَد | أَيْدِي | أَفْعُل (irregular) |

### 8. Ism al-Mafʿūl (اسم المفعول — Passive Participle)

- **مَمْلُوءَة** — "filled" (from مَلَأَ, Form I, pattern مَفْعُولَة)
- **مَشْؤُومَة** — "ominous/ill-fated" (from شَأَمَ, pattern مَفْعُولَة)

### 9. لَا يَزَالُونَ (Continuity Verb)

> **وَلَا يَزَالُونَ** يُقَاتِلُونَ أَرْبَعِينَ سَنَةً

**لا يزال** is one of the sisters of كان (أخوات كان) — specifically a verb of continuity. It puts its subject in the nominative and its predicate in the accusative. Here the predicate is the verbal sentence **يُقَاتِلُونَ**.

### 10. Rhetorical Questions (الاستفهام الإنكاري)

Two rhetorical questions drive the narrative:

1. **وَمَنْ فِي الدُّنْيَا لَا يَعْرِفُ هذَا الْبِنَاءَ؟** — "Who in the world does not know this building?" (Expected answer: no one. The question is used for emphasis.)

2. **فَلِمَاذَا لَا يُقَاتِلُونَ عَلَى هذَا الشَّرَفِ؟** — "So why would they not fight over this honour?" (Rhetorical — implying they certainly *would* fight.)

### 11. Adverbial Accusatives (المفعول المطلق / الحال / التمييز)

- **كَثِيرًا** — adverb of manner (حال or cognate accusative): "greatly"
- **أَرْبَعِينَ سَنَةً** — adverbial accusative of time (ظرف زمان): "for forty years"
- **دَمًا** — tamyīz (specification) after مَمْلُوءَة: "filled *with blood*"
- **جِدًّا** — emphatic adverb: "very much"

### 12. Key Sentence Structures

**Nominal sentences with إنّ:**
> إِنَّكُمْ لَتَعْرِفُونَ هذَا الْبِنَاءَ

**Verbal sentence with فاء السببية (causal فاء):**
> فَجَمَعَتْ قُرَيْشٌ الْحِجَارَةَ — "So Quraysh gathered the stones" (result of the building being old)

**فاء الفصيحة (explanatory فاء):**
> فَاخْتَصَمَتْ قُرَيْشٌ — "And so Quraysh quarrelled" (consequence of wanting to place the stone)

**Oath/pact formula:**
> لَا نَتْرُكُ هذَا الشَّرَفَ أَوْ نَمُوتَ — "We shall not leave this honour, or we shall die" — **أَوْ** here carries the meaning "unless" (i.e., we will not abandon it unless death takes us).

## Thematic Summary

This is Part 1 of the story of the placing of the Black Stone (الحجر الأسود). It sets up the historical conflict: after Quraysh rebuilt the Ka'bah, they could not agree on which tribe would have the honour of placing the Black Stone back in its position. The narrative emphasises the Arabs' fierce sense of honour and their readiness to fight and die for it, building dramatic tension toward Part 2 where the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ resolves the dispute.
