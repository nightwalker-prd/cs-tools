# Story 22: مُسَابَقَةٌ بَيْنَ شَقِيقَيْنِ

## A Competition Between Two Brothers

*Source: Pages 63–64 | A famous narration from the Battle of Badr*

---

## Arabic Text & English Translation

### Title

> **مُسَابَقَةٌ بَيْنَ شَقِيقَيْنِ**
>
> **A Competition Between Two Brothers**

---

### Paragraph 1

> **Arabic:**
> قَالَ سَيِّدُنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمٰنِ بْنُ عَوْفٍ رَضِيَ اللهُ عَنْهُ: كُنْتُ وَاقِفًا يَوْمَ بَدْرٍ وَغُلَامَانِ مِنَ الْأَنْصَارِ مُعَاذُ بْنُ عَفْرَاءَ وَمُعَوِّذُ بْنُ عَفْرَاءَ عَنْ يَمِينِي وَشِمَالِي.
>
> **English:**
> Our master Abd al-Rahman ibn Awf, may Allah be pleased with him, said: "I was standing on the day of Badr, and two young men from the Ansar — Mu'adh ibn Afra' and Mu'awwidh ibn Afra' — were on my right and my left."

---

### Paragraph 2

> **Arabic:**
> وَالْتَفَتَ إِلَيَّ أَحَدُهُمَا، وَقَالَ لِي سِرًّا مِنْ صَاحِبِهِ: «أَيْ عَمِّ! هَلْ تَعْرِفُ أَبَا جَهْلٍ»؟
>
> **English:**
> One of them turned to me and said to me secretly, without his companion knowing: "O uncle! Do you know Abu Jahl?"

---

### Paragraph 3

> **Arabic:**
> فَقُلْتُ: نَعَمْ! وَمَاذَا تُرِيدُ مِنْهُ يَا بْنَ أَخِي؟
>
> **English:**
> I said: "Yes! And what do you want from him, O son of my brother?"

---

### Paragraph 4

> **Arabic:**
> قَالَ: أُخْبِرْتُ أَنَّهُ يَسُبُّ رَسُولَ اللهِ ﷺ، أَرِنِيهِ يَا عَمِّ! فَإِنِّي أَعْطَيْتُ اللهَ عَهْدًا إِنْ رَأَيْتُهُ أَنْ أَقْتُلَهُ أَوْ أَمُوتَ دُونَهُ.
>
> **English:**
> He said: "I have been told that he insults the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. Show him to me, O uncle! For I have given Allah a pledge: if I see him, I will kill him or die trying."

---

### Paragraph 5

> **Arabic:**
> وَقَالَ لِيَ الْآخَرُ سِرًّا مِنْ صَاحِبِهِ: أَرِنِيهِ يَا عَمِّ! فَإِنِّي عَاهَدْتُ اللهَ إِنْ عَايَنْتُهُ أَنْ أَضْرِبَهُ بِسَيْفِي حَتَّى أَقْتُلَهُ.
>
> **English:**
> The other one said to me secretly, without his companion knowing: "Show him to me, O uncle! For I have pledged to Allah: if I see him, I will strike him with my sword until I kill him."

---

### Paragraph 6

> **Arabic:**
> فَبَيْنَا أَنَا كَذٰلِكَ إِذْ بَرَزَ أَبُو جَهْلٍ، فَقُلْتُ: أَلَا تَرَيَانِ؟ هٰذَا أَبُو جَهْلٍ، هٰذَا صَاحِبُكُمْ، فَشَدَّا عَلَيْهِ مِثْلَ الصَّقْرَيْنِ حَتَّى ضَرَبَاهُ.
>
> **English:**
> While I was in that state, Abu Jahl appeared. I said: "Don't you two see? This is Abu Jahl — this is your man!" So they charged at him like two hawks until they struck him down.

## Grammatical Analysis

### 1. Dual Forms (المثنى)

This story is rich in **dual number** (المثنى), which is one of Arabic's distinctive features (singular / dual / plural). Key examples:

| **Noun (genitive)** | الصَّقْرَيْنِ | Two hawks — dual marker ـَيْنِ (gen. after مِثْلَ) |
| **Noun (genitive)** | السَّيْفَيْنِ | Two swords — dual marker ـَيْنِ (gen. after فِي) |

### 3. Conditional Structures (الشرط)

The story contains two conditional pledges using **إِنْ** (if):

**First brother:**
> إِنْ رَأَيْتُهُ أَنْ أَقْتُلَهُ أَوْ أَمُوتَ دُونَهُ
> "If I see him, [I pledge] that I will kill him or die trying."

- **إِنْ** — conditional particle (for uncertain/future conditions)
- **رَأَيْتُهُ** — verb of condition (فعل الشرط), past tense used for future meaning
- **أَنْ أَقْتُلَهُ** — answer of the condition (جواب الشرط), with أَنْ + subjunctive

**Second brother:**
> إِنْ عَايَنْتُهُ أَنْ أَضْرِبَهُ بِسَيْفِي حَتَّى أَقْتُلَهُ
> "If I see him, [I pledge] that I will strike him with my sword until I kill him."

- Same structure: إِنْ + past tense (فعل الشرط) → أَنْ + subjunctive (جواب الشرط)

### 4. Key Sentence Structures

#### a. كَانَ + اسم الفاعل (Past continuous)
> كُنْتُ وَاقِفًا — "I was standing"

- **كُنْتُ** — كان (was) + تُ (1st person)
- **وَاقِفًا** — active participle (اسم الفاعل) of وَقَفَ, functioning as خبر كان (predicate), manṣūb

#### b. فَبَيْنَا ... إِذْ (While... suddenly)
> فَبَيْنَا أَنَا كَذٰلِكَ إِذْ بَرَزَ أَبُو جَهْلٍ

- **بَيْنَا** / **بَيْنَمَا** — "while" (ظرف زمان)
- **إِذْ** — "suddenly, when" (فجائية)
- This is a classic Arabic construction for expressing a sudden interruption during an ongoing state

#### c. Vocative (النداء)
- **أَيْ عَمِّ!** — "O uncle!" (أَيْ = vocative particle for close address; عَمِّ = مُنَادَى مُضَاف, with the ي dropped)
- **يَا بْنَ أَخِي** — "O son of my brother!" (يَا = standard vocative; بْنَ = مُنَادَى مُضَاف, manṣūb)
- **يَا عَمِّ!** — "O uncle!" (يَا = vocative particle)

#### d. إِنَّ with Causal فَـ
> فَإِنِّي أَعْطَيْتُ اللهَ عَهْدًا — "For indeed I have given Allah a pledge"

- **فَـ** — causal/explanatory فاء (فاء التعليل)
- **إِنِّي** — إِنَّ + ي (1st person pronoun as اسم إنّ)
- Gives the reason for the request "show him to me"

#### e. Simile (التشبيه)
> فَشَدَّا عَلَيْهِ **مِثْلَ الصَّقْرَيْنِ** — "They charged at him **like two hawks**"

- **مِثْلَ** — "like" (أداة التشبيه, functioning as حال or مفعول مطلق)
- **الصَّقْرَيْنِ** — "the two hawks" (مُشَبَّه بِهِ, dual genitive)
- A vivid simile conveying speed, precision, and ferocity

### 5. Passive Voice (المبني للمجهول)

> أُخْبِرْتُ أَنَّهُ يَسُبُّ رَسُولَ اللهِ
> "I was informed that he insults the Messenger of Allah"

- **أُخْبِرْتُ** — Form IV passive (أُفْعِلَ) + تُ (1st person)
- The original subject (who told him) is omitted — the focus is on the information itself
- أَنَّهُ يَسُبُّ — noun clause (مصدر مؤوّل) serving as the topic of the informing

## Historical Context

This narration describes an episode from the **Battle of Badr** (2 AH / 624 CE), the first major military encounter between the Muslims and the Quraysh of Makkah. **Abu Jahl** (Amr ibn Hisham) was one of the fiercest opponents of Islam and the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. The narrator, **Abd al-Rahman ibn Awf**, was a senior Companion, while **Mu'adh** and **Mu'awwidh ibn Afra'** were two young Ansari brothers — teenagers at the time — whose zeal and bravery are highlighted in this account. The Prophet's judgment that "both of them killed him" recognized the shared effort of both brothers.
