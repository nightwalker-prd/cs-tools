# (٨) سَفَرُ الْقِطَارِ — The Train Journey

**Source:** Pages 30–32

---

## Full Text with Translation

### Paragraph 1 — Preparing for the Journey

> لَا أَنْسَى سَفَرِي الأَوَّلَ ، عَلِمْتُ أَنِّي مُسَافِرٌ بُكْرَةً مَعَ أُمِّي وَإِخْوَتِي ، فَاسْتَيْقَظْتُ قَبْلَ السَّحَرِ وَبَقِيتُ أَنْتَظِرُ سَاعَةَ السَّفَرِ ، وَاسْتَيْقَظَ أَهْلُ الْبَيْتِ مُبَكِّرِينَ ، وَصَلَّيْنَا الصُّبْحَ ، وَجَاءَ عَمِّي وَبَدَأَتْ فِي الْبَيْتِ حَرَكَةٌ وَأَصْوَاتٌ ، هَذَا يَجْرِي وَذَلِكَ يَلُفُّ الْفِرَاشَ وَهَذَا يُنَادِي وَذَلِكَ يُجِيبُ ، وَالْعَمُّ يَغْضَبُ وَيَسْتَعْجِلُ ، وَالْوَالِدُ قَائِمٌ يَأْمُرُ وَيَنْهَى ، وَيَغْضَبُ وَيُرْشِدُ ، وَالْخَادِمُ يُهَيِّئُ الزَّادَ ، حَتَّى كَانَ وَقْتُ الْخُرُوجِ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ وَقَرُبَ مِيعَادُ الْقِطَارِ.

**Translation:** I will never forget my first journey. I learned that I would be travelling in the morning with my mother and my siblings. So I woke up before dawn and remained waiting for the hour of departure. The household woke up early, and we prayed the Fajr prayer. My uncle came, and activity and voices began in the house — this one running, that one rolling up bedding, this one calling out, that one answering. The uncle was getting angry and hurrying everyone along, and the father was standing giving orders and prohibitions, getting angry and giving directions, while the servant was preparing the provisions — until it was time to leave the house and the train's scheduled time drew near.

---

### Paragraph 2 — Departure to the Station

> جَاءَتْ مَرْكَبَتَانِ فَرَكِبْنَاهُمَا ، وَسَلَّمْتُ عَلَى أَبِي فَوَدَّعَنِي وَدَعَا لِي ، وَوَصَلْنَا إِلَى الْمَحَطَّةِ فَأَخَذَ الْحَمَّالُونَ الْحَوَائِجَ وَالْمَتَاعَ ، وَكَانَتْ أَيَّامُ شِتَاءٍ فَكَانَتِ الْفُرُشُ كَبِيرَةً ، وَذَهَبَ عَمِّي فَاشْتَرَى تَذَاكِرَ الْقِطَارِ.

**Translation:** Two carriages came and we rode in them. I said goodbye to my father, and he bade me farewell and made supplication for me. We arrived at the station, and the porters took the luggage and belongings. It was wintertime, so the bedding was bulky. My uncle went and bought the train tickets.

---

### Paragraph 3 — The Fare and the Ticket

> وَسَأَلْتُ عَمِّي عَنِ النَّوْلِ فَقَالَ: إِنَّ النَّوْلَ ثَلَاثُ رُبِّيَّاتٍ ، وَرُبِّيَّةٌ وَنِصْفٌ لَكَ.

**Translation:** I asked my uncle about the fare, and he said: "The fare is three rupees, and a rupee and a half for you."

---

### Paragraph 4 — Keeping the Ticket

> وَقُلْتُ لِعَمِّي: أَعْطِنِي تَذْكِرَتِي. فَقَالَ عَمِّي: إِنَّكَ تُضَيِّعُ تَذْكِرَتَكَ ، فَقُلْتُ: لَا! سَأُحَافِظُ عَلَى تَذْكِرَتِي إِنْ شَاءَ اللهُ ، فَأَعْطَانِي تَذْكِرَتِي وَوَضَعْتُهَا عِنْدِي.

**Translation:** I said to my uncle: "Give me my ticket." My uncle said: "You will lose your ticket." I said: "No! I will keep my ticket safe, God willing." So he gave me my ticket and I placed it with me.

---

### Paragraph 5 — Inside the Station

> دَخَلْنَا الْمَحَطَّةَ فَرَأَيْنَا رِجَالًا كَثِيرًا وَنِسَاءً وَأَطْفَالًا وَرَأَيْنَا زِحَامًا شَدِيدًا وَسَمِعْنَا أَصْوَاتَ النَّاسِ وَ الأَطْفَالِ وَصَيْحَةَ الْحَمَّالِينَ وَصَفِيرَ الْقَاطِرَةِ.

**Translation:** We entered the station and saw many men, women, and children. We saw severe crowding and heard the voices of people and children, the shouts of the porters, and the whistle of the locomotive.

---

### Paragraph 6 — Waiting for the Train

> وَكَانَ قِطَارُنَا مُتَأَخِّرًا فَذَهَبْنَا إِلَى الْمُنْظَرَةِ وَجَلَسْنَا قَلِيلًا ، ثُمَّ جِئْتُ إِلَى الرَّصِيفِ لِأَرَى هَلْ جَاءَ الْقِطَارُ ثُمَّ رَجَعْتُ إِلَى الْمُنْظَرَةِ.

**Translation:** Our train was delayed, so we went to the waiting room and sat for a while. Then I went to the platform to see if the train had arrived, then I returned to the waiting room.

---

### Paragraph 7 — Boarding the Train

> وَبَعْدَ قَلِيلٍ جَاءَ الْقِطَارُ فَخَرَجْنَا مِنَ الْمُنْظَرَةِ ، وَقَامَ النَّاسُ كُلُّهُمْ عَلَى الرَّصِيفِ وَوَقَفَ الْقِطَارُ ، وَنَزَلَ أُنَاسٌ وَرَكِبَ أُنَاسٌ وَرَكِبْنَا.

**Translation:** After a short while the train arrived, and we came out of the waiting room. All the people stood on the platform, and the train stopped. Some people got off and some people got on, and we boarded.

---

### Paragraph 8 — On the Train

> وَكُنْتُ أُطِلُّ مِنَ الْقِطَارِ وَأَرَى الْمَنَاظِرَ ، وَكَانَ الزِّحَامُ شَدِيدًا فِي الْقِطَارِ ، وَجَاءَ الْبَاعَةُ وَجَعَلَ النَّاسُ يَشْتَرُونَ وَيَأْكُلُونَ ، وَاشْتَرَى بَعْضُ النَّاسِ مِنَ الْبَاعَةِ هَدَايَا لِأَصْدِقَائِهِمْ وَأَقَارِبِهِمْ.

**Translation:** I was looking out from the train and seeing the scenery. The crowding was severe on the train. The vendors came, and the people began buying and eating. Some people bought gifts from the vendors for their friends and relatives.

---

### Paragraph 9 — The Train Departs

> وَبَعْدَ قَلِيلٍ صَفَرَ أَمِينُ الْقِطَارِ وَهَزَّ الْعَلَمَ الأَخْضَرَ فَأَسْرَعَ النَّاسُ وَدَخَلُوا فِي الْقِطَارِ ، وَتَحَرَّكَتِ الْقَاطِرَةُ وَسَارَ الْقِطَارُ.

**Translation:** After a short while, the train conductor blew the whistle and waved the green flag. The people hurried and entered the train, and the locomotive moved and the train set off.

---

### Paragraph 10 — The Ticket Inspector

> وَدَخَلَ نَقَّابٌ فِي عَرَبَتِنَا فَنَقَبَ تَذَاكِرَنَا وَرَدَّهَا إِلَيْنَا.

**Translation:** A ticket inspector entered our carriage, punched our tickets, and returned them to us.

---

### Paragraph 11 — Eating on the Way

> وَفِي الطَّرِيقِ تَغَدَّيْنَا بِالزَّادِ وَأَكَلْنَا وَشَرِبْنَا وَحَمِدْنَا اللهَ.

**Translation:** On the way, we had lunch from our provisions, ate and drank, and praised God.

---

### Paragraph 12 — Stops and Prayer

> وَلَمْ يَزَلْ يَقِفُ الْقِطَارُ عَلَى الْمَحَطَّاتِ وَيَسِيرُ حَتَّى وَصَلَ وَقْتُ الظُّهْرِ فَتَوَضَّأْنَا بِسُرْعَةٍ عَلَى مَحَطَّةٍ وَصَلَّيْنَا صَلَاةَ السَّفَرِ ، صَلَّيْنَا الظُّهْرَ رَكْعَتَيْنِ وَسَلَّمْنَا ، وَصَفَرَ أَمِينُ الْقِطَارِ فَرَكِبْنَا سَرِيعًا.

**Translation:** The train kept stopping at stations and moving on, until the time for Dhuhr (noon prayer) arrived. We made wudu (ablution) quickly at a station and prayed the traveller's prayer — we prayed Dhuhr as two rak'ahs and gave salaam. Then the train conductor blew the whistle, so we boarded quickly.

---

### Paragraph 13 — The Uncle's Remark

> وَقَالَ عَمِّي: لَوْ كَانَ الْقِطَارُ لِلْمُسْلِمِينَ لَكَانَ فِيهِ مَكَانٌ لِلْوُضُوءِ وَالصَّلَاةِ ، نُؤَذِّنُ فِيهِ وَنُصَلِّي جَمَاعَةً.

**Translation:** My uncle said: "If the train belonged to the Muslims, there would be a place in it for ablution and prayer — we would give the adhan in it and pray in congregation."

---

### Paragraph 14 — Arrival

> وَفِي الْعَصْرِ وَصَلَ الْقِطَارُ إِلَى مَحَطَّتِنَا ، وَكُنْتُ أُطِلُّ مِنَ النَّافِذَةِ فَرَأَيْتُ هَاشِمًا وَسَعِيدًا عَلَى الرَّصِيفِ وَعَرَفْتُهُمَا وَسَلَّمْتُ عَلَيْهِمَا وَسَلَّمَا عَلَيَّ.

**Translation:** In the afternoon (Asr time), the train arrived at our station. I was looking out from the window and saw Hashim and Sa'id on the platform. I recognized them and greeted them, and they greeted me.

---

### Paragraph 15 — Home at Last

> وَوَصَلْتُ إِلَى قَرْيَتِي وَقَابَلْتُ أَصْدِقَائِي وَإِخْوَانِي وَجَعَلْتُ أُحَدِّثُهُمْ حَدِيثَ الْبَلَدِ وَأُخْبِرُهُمْ بِعَجَائِبِهِ وَأَحْكِي لَهُمْ مَا رَأَيْتُ فِي السَّفَرِ.

**Translation:** I arrived at my village and met my friends and brothers, and I began telling them stories of the city, informing them of its wonders, and narrating to them what I had seen on the journey.

## Grammatical Analysis

### 1. Verb Forms (أَوْزَانُ الأَفْعَالِ)

This text is rich in derived verb forms:

| Form | Pattern | Example | Meaning | Notes |
|------|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| **I** (فَعَلَ) | فَعَلَ | صَفَرَ ، هَزَّ ، نَقَبَ | basic meaning | Most verbs in the text |
| **II** (فَعَّلَ) | فَعَّلَ | وَدَّعَ ، يُهَيِّئُ ، سَلَّمَ | intensification / causative | وَدَّعَ = "bid farewell" (intensive of وَدَعَ) |
| **IV** (أَفْعَلَ) | أَفْعَلَ | أُطِلُّ ، أَسْرَعَ ، أَعْطَى | transitive / causative | أَسْرَعَ = "to hurry" (make fast) |
| **V** (تَفَعَّلَ) | تَفَعَّلَ | تَوَضَّأَ ، تَغَدَّى ، تَحَرَّكَ | reflexive of Form II | تَوَضَّأَ = "to perform ablution on oneself" |
| **VIII** (اِفْتَعَلَ) | اِفْتَعَلَ | اشْتَرَى | reflexive / middle voice | "to buy for oneself" |
| **X** (اِسْتَفْعَلَ) | اِسْتَفْعَلَ | اسْتَيْقَظَ ، اسْتَعْجَلَ | seeking / requesting | اسْتَيْقَظَ = "to seek wakefulness" = wake up |

### 3. Key Grammatical Structures

#### a) لَا أَنْسَى — Negation of the Imperfect (Present/Future)
- **لَا** + مُضَارِع = negation of present/future tense
- "I do not / will not forget"

#### b) إِنَّ + Accusative (اسم إنّ)
- **إِنَّ النَّوْلَ ثَلَاثُ رُبِّيَّاتٍ** — "Indeed the fare is three rupees"
- **إِنَّكَ تُضَيِّعُ** — "Indeed you will lose..."
- إِنَّ takes its subject (اسم) in the accusative: النَّوْلَ ، ـكَ

#### c) لَوْ كَانَ ... لَكَانَ — Conditional (Unreal Past)
- **لَوْ كَانَ الْقِطَارُ لِلْمُسْلِمِينَ لَكَانَ فِيهِ مَكَانٌ**
- "If the train had belonged to the Muslims, there would have been a place in it..."
- لَوْ = "if" (contrary-to-fact), followed by past tense; لَ introduces the result clause

#### d) لَمْ يَزَلْ — Continuous Action
- **لَمْ يَزَلْ يَقِفُ الْقِطَارُ** — "The train continued to stop..."
- لَمْ يَزَلْ (= مَا زَالَ) is a كان وأخواتها verb meaning "to continue, still be"

#### e) جَعَلَ + Imperfect — Inceptive ("began to")
- **جَعَلَ النَّاسُ يَشْتَرُونَ** — "The people began buying"
- **جَعَلْتُ أُحَدِّثُهُمْ** — "I began telling them"
- جَعَلَ here is an أَفْعَال الشُّرُوع (verb of beginning)

#### f) فَـ (Fa) — Connective / Consequential
Used extensively throughout the text for sequential narration:
- **فَاسْتَيْقَظْتُ** — "so I woke up"
- **فَأَخَذَ الْحَمَّالُونَ** — "so the porters took"
- **فَرَكِبْنَا** — "so we boarded"

This فَـ is the **فاء السببية / فاء العاطفة** indicating cause-and-effect or chronological sequence.

#### g) حَتَّى — "Until"
- **حَتَّى كَانَ وَقْتُ الْخُرُوجِ** — "until it was time to leave"
- **حَتَّى وَصَلَ وَقْتُ الظُّهْرِ** — "until Dhuhr time arrived"
- حَتَّى + past tense = "until (something happened)"

#### h) Dual Form (المُثَنَّى)
- **مَرْكَبَتَانِ** — "two carriages" (nominative dual of مَرْكَبَة)
- **فَرَكِبْنَاهُمَا** — "we rode them both" (هُمَا = dual pronoun)
- **رَكْعَتَيْنِ** — "two rak'ahs" (accusative dual of رَكْعَة)
- **عَرَفْتُهُمَا / سَلَّمْتُ عَلَيْهِمَا / سَلَّمَا** — dual pronouns and dual verb

#### i) حَال (Circumstantial Clause)
- **وَكُنْتُ أُطِلُّ مِنَ النَّافِذَةِ** — "while I was peering out the window"
- **اسْتَيْقَظَ أَهْلُ الْبَيْتِ مُبَكِّرِينَ** — مُبَكِّرِينَ is a حال (state/manner): "waking up early"

#### j) لِـ + Subjunctive — Purpose Clause
- **لِأَرَى هَلْ جَاءَ الْقِطَارُ** — "in order to see whether the train had come"
- لِـ (lām al-taʿlīl) + مضارع منصوب expresses purpose

### 5. Cultural & Islamic Notes

- **صَلَاةُ السَّفَرِ** (Traveller's Prayer): Muslims shorten the four-rak'ah prayers to two rak'ahs when travelling (قَصْر الصَّلَاة). The text demonstrates this: صَلَّيْنَا الظُّهْرَ رَكْعَتَيْنِ.
- **الوُضُوء** (Wudu/Ablution): Ritual washing before prayer. The uncle laments the lack of facilities on the train.
- **إِنْ شَاءَ اللهُ** ("God willing"): Standard Islamic phrase when speaking of future intentions.
- **حَمِدْنَا اللهَ** ("We praised God"): Saying الحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ after eating is a Sunnah practice.
- **رُبِّيَّة** (Rupee): Indicates the story is set in the Indian subcontinent, likely during the British colonial period.
- **الأَذَان** (Adhan): The call to prayer. The uncle wishes the train had a place for congregational prayer with adhan.
