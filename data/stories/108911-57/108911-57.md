# (١٨) تَرْنِيمَةُ الْوَلَدِ فِي الصَّبَاحِ

## The Child's Morning Hymn

*Source: مَدَارِجُ القِرَاءَة (Madarij al-Qira'ah — Stages of Reading), p. 57*

---

## Full Prose Translation

> The sun has risen and the darkness has turned and fled away. So all thanks be to God, the One — great and obligatory thanks. How beautiful the light is! I see through it all things by His name. And the birds sing at dawn, standing upon the branches. How beautiful the radiant light is! In it I strive, working hard. Indeed I always wish never to be idle. God has protected me from every evil in the darkness. Thanks be to Him — He has guarded me. Thanks be to Him, forever and always.

## Grammatical Analysis (تَحْلِيل نَحْوِيّ)

### 1. Verb Forms (أَوْزَان الأَفْعَال)

| Verb | Form | Pattern | Meaning Shift |
|------|------|---------|---------------|
| أَشْرَقَ | IV (أَفْعَلَ) | أَ + شرق | Intransitive → "to emit light, shine" |
| وَلَّى | II (فَعَّلَ) | و ل ي with shadda | Intensified: "to turn away completely, flee" |
| أَجَارَ | IV (أَفْعَلَ) | أَ + جور | Causative: "to give protection/shelter" |
| تَشْدُو | I (فَعَلَ/يَفْعُلُ) | ش د و — defective (nāqiṣ) | Base form: "to sing, warble" |
| أَجِدُّ | I (فَعَلَ/يَفْعِلُ) | ج د د — doubled (muḍaʿʿaf) | "to be earnest, strive" |
| صَانَ | I (فَعَلَ/يَفْعُلُ) | ص و ن — hollow (ajwaf) | "to guard, protect" |
| أَوَدُّ | I (فَعِلَ/يَفْعَلُ) | و د د — doubled + assimilated | "to wish, love" |

### 2. Exclamatory Structure (أُسْلُوب التَّعَجُّب)

**مَا أَحْسَنَ النُّورَ** — "How beautiful the light is!"

This is the standard Arabic exclamatory form:

> **مَا + أَفْعَلَ + المَفْعُول بِهِ**

- **مَا** — exclamatory particle (not interrogative or negative here)
- **أَحْسَنَ** — Form IV verb used as an exclamatory (from حَسُنَ "to be beautiful")
- **النُّورَ** — accusative (مَنْصُوب), the object of wonder

This structure appears twice in the poem (lines 3 and 5), emphasizing the beauty of light.

### 3. حَال (Circumstantial Accusative)

Several words appear in the accusative (مَنْصُوب) as حَال, describing the state of the subject:

| Word | Describes | Meaning |
|------|-----------|---------|
| هَارِبًا | الظَّلَام | "the darkness [fleeing]" — state of darkness as it departs |
| سَحَرًا | الطَّيْر | "at dawn" — temporal adverb (ظَرْف زَمَان) |
| قَائِمَةً | الطَّيْر | "standing" — state of the birds while singing |
| عَامِلًا | ضمير المتكلم (أنا) | "working" — state of the speaker while striving |
| دَائِمًا | أَوَدُّ | "always" — adverbial for the wishing |
| خَامِلًا | ضمير المتكلم (أنا) | "idle" — predicate of كَانَ in أَكُونَ خَامِلًا |

### 4. قَدْ + الفِعْل المَاضِي (Emphasis with Past Tense)

**قَدْ** appears three times, each time emphasizing certainty of a completed action:

- **وَقَدْ وَلَّى الظَّلَامُ** — "and indeed the darkness has fled" (certainty)
- **قَدْ أَجَارَنِي** — "He has [certainly] protected me"
- **قَدْ صَانَنِي** — "He has [certainly] guarded me"

> When قَدْ precedes a past tense verb (فِعْل مَاضٍ), it indicates certainty and completion — similar to English "indeed" or the perfect aspect "has done."

### 5. أَلَّا أَكُونَ (Negative Wish with Subjunctive)

> إِنِّي أَوَدُّ دَائِمًا أَلَّا أَكُونَ خَامِلًا

- **أَلَّا** = أَنْ + لَا (the subjunctive particle + negation)
- **أَكُونَ** — subjunctive (مَنْصُوب) of كَانَ, because أَنْ triggers the subjunctive mood
- **خَامِلًا** — predicate of كَانَ (خَبَر كَانَ), accusative

Structure: إنّ + ي (subject) + أَوَدُّ (verb) + أَنْ لَا + أَكُونَ (subjunctive) + خَامِلًا (predicate)

### 6. إضَافَة (Construct Chains)

| Chain | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| تَرْنِيمَةُ الوَلَدِ | the hymn of the child |
| كُلِّ شَرٍّ | every evil |
| عَلَى الدَّوَامِ | upon permanence → forever |

### 7. فَاء السَّبَبِيَّة (Causal فَ)

> **فَالشُّكْرُ للهِ الأَحَدِ**

The **فَ** here is causal — "so / therefore." Because the sun has risen and darkness has fled, *therefore* thanks are due to God.

### 8. Pronoun Suffixes (ضَمَائِر مُتَّصِلَة)

| Form | Breakdown | Meaning |
|------|-----------|---------|
| فِيهِ | فِي + هِ | in it (referring to النُّور) |
| بِاسْمِهِ | بِ + اسْم + هِ | by His name (referring to الله) |
| أَجَارَنِي | أَجَارَ + نِي | He protected me |
| صَانَنِي | صَانَ + نِي | He guarded me |
| لَهُ | لِ + هُ | to/for Him |
| إِنِّي | إِنَّ + ي | indeed I |

## Thematic Notes

This is a children's morning poem expressing gratitude to God for the new day. Its themes include:

1. **Light vs. Darkness** — The sun's rising symbolizes divine blessing; darkness represents danger and evil
2. **Gratitude (شُكْر)** — The word شُكْر/شُكْرًا appears four times, forming the poem's backbone
3. **Diligence vs. Idleness** — The child wishes to be عَامِل (active) and not خَامِل (idle) — note the rhyming contrast
4. **Divine Protection** — God is credited with guarding (صَانَ) and sheltering (أَجَارَ) the child through the night
5. **Nature as praise** — Birds singing at dawn (تَشْدُو سَحَرًا) mirrors the child's own morning hymn
