# (٢٣) جَزَاءُ الوَالِدَيْنِ
# (23) The Reward of the Parents

**Source:** Pages 65–67

---

## Arabic Text & English Translation

### Paragraph 1 — Early Childhood

**Arabic:**

> وُلِدتُّ صَغِيراً ضَعِيفاً لَا أَقْدِرُ عَلَى عَمَلٍ، لَا آكُلُ بِنَفْسِي وَلَا أَشْرَبُ بِنَفْسِي، وَلَا أَتَكَلَّمُ وَلَا أَفْهَمُ، فَحَنَّتْ عَلَيَّ أُمِّي وَأَرْضَعَتْنِي وَنَسِيَتْ نَفْسَهَا لِنَفْسِي، وَهَجَرَتْ رَاحَتَهَا لِرَاحَتِي، فَكَمْ سَهِرَتِ اللَّيَالِيَ، وَكَمْ تَعِبَتْ فِي النَّهَارِ، وَكُنْتُ لَهَا شُغْلاً وَحَدِيثاً، وَإِذَا مَرِضْتُ طَارَ عَنْهَا النَّوْمُ، وَمَا ذَاقَتْ طَعَاماً وَلَا شَرَاباً، وَإِذَا سَكَتُّ اهْتَمَّتْ وَقَالَتْ: مَا بَالُكَ يَا بُنَيَّ! مَاذَا أَسْكَتَكَ؟ لِمَاذَا لَا تَتَكَلَّمُ، أَتَشْكُو وَجَعاً أَوْ أَغْضَبَكَ أَحَدٌ؟ وَإِذَا بَكَيْتُ جَاءَتْ تَجْرِي، وَفِي اللَّيْلِ تَتَكَلَّمُ مَعِي وَتُضَاحِكُنِي.

**English:**

I was born small and weak, unable to do anything. I could not eat by myself, nor drink by myself, nor speak, nor understand. So my mother showed compassion toward me and nursed me, and she forgot herself for my sake, and she abandoned her comfort for my comfort. How many nights she stayed up, and how much she toiled during the day! I was her sole occupation and concern. And when I fell ill, sleep fled from her, and she tasted neither food nor drink. And when I fell silent, she grew worried and said: "What is the matter with you, my son! What has silenced you? Why don't you speak — are you in pain, or has someone upset you?" And when I cried, she came running. And at night she would talk with me and make me laugh.

### Paragraph 2 — Stories from Mother

**Arabic:**

> وَلَمَّا دَخَلْتُ فِي السَّنَةِ الخَامِسَةِ كَانَتْ تَتَحَدَّثُ مَعِي فِي اللَّيْلِ، وَقَدْ عَرَفْتُ اللهَ وَرَسُولَهُ فِي حَدِيثِهَا، وَسَمِعْتُ قِصَصاً كَثِيرَةً، سَمِعْتُ مِنْهَا وَأَنَا عَلَى فِرَاشِي قِصَّةَ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، وَكَيْفَ أُلْقِيَ فِي النَّارِ، فَصَارَتْ بَرْداً وَسَلَاماً، وَكَيْفَ نَشَأَ مُوسَى فِي قَصْرِ فِرْعَوْنَ، وَسَمِعْتُ قِصَّةَ رَسُولِ اللهِ ﷺ وَقِصَّةَ حَلِيمَةَ السَّعْدِيَّةِ وَقِصَصاً جَمِيلَةً، وَحَفِظْتُ آيَةَ الكُرْسِيِّ وَالآيَاتِ الأَخِيرَةَ مِنْ سُورَةِ البَقَرَةِ وَأَدْعِيَةً كَثِيرَةً، فَكُنْتُ عَالَمَ الأَطْفَالِ.

**English:**

And when I entered my fifth year, she would talk with me at night. Through her conversations I came to know Allah and His Messenger. I heard many stories. From her, while lying on my bed, I heard the story of Ibrahim — how he was cast into the fire, and it became coolness and peace. And how Musa grew up in the palace of Pharaoh. And I heard the story of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and the story of Halimah al-Sa'diyyah, and many beautiful stories. I memorized Ayat al-Kursi, the final verses of Surat al-Baqarah, and many supplications — so I was the scholar of the children.

### Paragraph 3 — A Father's Love

**Arabic:**

> وَكَانَ أَبِي يُحِبُّنِي كَثِيراً، وَكُنْتُ أَصْغَرَ إِخْوَتِي أَبِيتُ مَعَهُ وَآكُلُ مَعَهُ، وَإِذَا جَاءَ مِنْ سَفَرٍ اشْتَرَى لِي هَدِيَّةً جَمِيلَةً، وَكَانَ النَّاسُ يُحِبُّونَنِي وَيُقَرِّبُونَنِي إِلَيْهِمْ لِمَكَانِي مِنْ أَبِي، وَقَدْ قَرَأْتُ عَلَى أَبِي أَيْضاً، فَهُوَ لِي أَبٌ وَمُعَلِّمٌ.

**English:**

My father loved me greatly. I was the youngest of my siblings — I would spend the night with him and eat with him. And whenever he returned from a journey, he would buy me a beautiful gift. People loved me and drew me close to them because of my position with my father. I also studied under my father, so he was both a father and a teacher to me.

### Paragraph 4 — How Can I Repay Them?

**Arabic:**

> وَكَانَ يُوصِي أُمِّي أَنْ تَكْسُوَنِي يَوْمَ العِيدِ لِبَاساً جَدِيداً، وَإِذَا مَرِضْتُ أَوْ سَقَطْتُ مِنْ مَكَانٍ أَوْ أَصَابَنِي ضَرَرٌ أَوْ أَلَمٌ وَجَاءَهُ الخَبَرُ طَارَ نَوْمُهُ، وَسَهِرَ اللَّيْلَ هَمّاً وَحُزْناً، كَيْفَ أُجَازِي هَذِهِ النِّعَمَ؟ هَلْ يُمْكِنُ أَنْ أُجَازِيَهُمَا بِمَالٍ؟ كَلَّا! فَأَنَا وَمَالِي لِوَالِدَيَّ، نَعَمْ! أَنَا أَخْدِمُهُمَا بِالمَالِ وَالبَدَنِ، بَلْ أَصِلُ أَصْدِقَاءَهُمَا وَأَقَارِبَهُمَا بِالبِرِّ وَالمَعْرُوفِ، وَلَكِنِّي سَأَدْعُو لَهُمَا، وَأَقُولُ دَائِماً فِي دُعَائِي: «رَبِّ ارْحَمْهُمَا كَمَا رَبَّيَانِي صَغِيراً».

**English:**

He would advise my mother to clothe me in new garments on the day of Eid. And if I fell ill, or fell from a place, or harm or pain befell me, and the news reached him — sleep would flee from him, and he would stay up all night in worry and grief. How can I repay these blessings? Is it possible to repay them with wealth? Never! For I and my wealth belong to my parents. Yes! I serve them with my wealth and my body. Rather, I maintain ties with their friends and relatives through righteousness and kindness. But I will also pray for them, and I always say in my supplication: **"My Lord, have mercy upon them as they raised me when I was small."** *(Quran 17:24)*

### Paragraph 5 — Aspiration for the Day of Judgment

**Arabic:**

> وَسَأَجْتَهِدُ إِنْ شَاءَ اللهُ أَنْ يَغْتَبِطَ بِي يَوْمَ القِيَامَةِ أَمَامَ النَّاسِ وَأَمَامَ الآبَاءِ وَالأُمَّهَاتِ وَيَغْبِطُهُمَا أَصْحَابُ الأَوْلَادِ وَيَقُولُونَ: يَا لَيْتَ لَنَا مِنَ الأَوْلَادِ مِثْلَ مَا أُوتِيَ فُلَانٌ، إِنَّهُ لَسَعِيدٌ.

**English:**

And I will strive, God willing, that they will be proud of me on the Day of Judgment before all the people, before the fathers and the mothers, and that other parents will envy them and say: "If only we had among our children one like what so-and-so was given — he is truly fortunate!"

### Paragraph 6 — Crowning and Intercession

**Arabic:**

> وَسَأَجْتَهِدُ أَنْ أَعْمَلَ عَمَلاً يُنَادِي بِي يَوْمَ القِيَامَةِ عَلَى رُؤُوسِ الأَشْهَادِ فَيَقُولُ النَّاسُ: مَنْ هُوَ؟ فَيُقَالُ: ابْنُ فُلَانٍ وَفُلَانَةٍ، فَيَغْتَبِطُ وَالِدَايَ وَيَنْعَمُ بَالِي.
>
> وَقَدْ سَمِعْتُ أَنَّ الوَلَدَ إِذَا حَفِظَ القُرْآنَ يُتَوَّجُ وَالِدَاهُ يَوْمَ القِيَامَةِ، فَسَأَجْتَهِدُ فِي حِفْظِ القُرْآنِ لِيُتَوَّجَ وَالِدَايَ يَوْمَ القِيَامَةِ.
>
> وَقَدْ سَمِعْتُ أَنَّ الشَّهِيدَ يَشْفَعُ لِسَبْعِينَ مِنْ أَهْلِ بَيْتِهِ، وَلَعَلَّ اللهَ يَرْزُقُنِي الشَّهَادَةَ، فَأَشْفَعُ لِوَالِدَيَّ قَبْلَ النَّاسِ، وَبِذَلِكَ أُجَازِي بَعْضَ نِعَمِهِمَا.

**English:**

And I will strive to do a deed that will be proclaimed in my name on the Day of Judgment before all witnesses, and the people will ask: "Who is he?" And it will be said: "The son of so-and-so," and my parents will be overjoyed, and my heart will be at peace.

I have heard that when a child memorizes the Quran, his parents will be crowned on the Day of Judgment. So I will strive to memorize the Quran so that my parents may be crowned on the Day of Judgment.

And I have heard that the martyr intercedes for seventy of the people of his household. Perhaps Allah will grant me martyrdom, so that I may intercede for my parents before all others — and by that, I will have repaid some of their blessings.

## Grammatical Structures & Analysis

### 1. Passive Voice (المبني للمجهول)

| Arabic | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| **وُلِدتُّ** صَغِيراً | Past passive of وَلَدَ (Form I). The subject becomes نائب الفاعل (deputy agent). صَغِيراً is حال (circumstantial accusative). |
| **أُلْقِيَ** فِي النَّارِ | Past passive of أَلْقَى (Form IV). The implied pronoun (هو = Ibrahim) is the نائب الفاعل. |
| **يُتَوَّجُ** وَالِدَاهُ | Present passive of تَوَّجَ (Form II) — "his parents are crowned." والداه is the نائب الفاعل in the dual form (مرفوع بالألف). |
| **أُوتِيَ** فُلَانٌ | Past passive of آتَى (Form IV) — "so-and-so was given." |
| **فَيُقَالُ** | Past passive of قَالَ — "and it is said." Impersonal passive. |

### 2. Dual Form (المثنى)

| Arabic | Case | Analysis |
|--------|------|----------|
| الوَالِدَيْنِ | مجرور | Dual of والد, genitive after إضافة (جَزَاءُ الوَالِدَيْنِ) |
| لِوَالِدَيَّ | مجرور | Dual + 1st person possessive: لِوَالِدَيْ + يْ → لِوَالِدَيَّ |
| وَالِدَايَ | مرفوع | Dual nominative (فَيَغْتَبِطُ وَالِدَايَ) — raised with الألف |
| وَالِدَاهُ | مرفوع | Dual nominative + 3rd person pronoun (يُتَوَّجُ وَالِدَاهُ) |
| رَبَّيَانِي | — | Dual past verb (هُمَا رَبَّيَا + نِي) — "they both raised me" |

### 3. Conditional Sentences (الجمل الشرطية)

The text uses **إِذَا** (temporal/conditional particle for likely events) extensively:

| Condition (فعل الشرط) | Result (جواب الشرط) |
|-----------------------|---------------------|
| وَإِذَا **مَرِضْتُ** | **طَارَ** عَنْهَا النَّوْمُ |
| وَإِذَا **سَكَتُّ** | **اهْتَمَّتْ** وَقَالَتْ |
| وَإِذَا **بَكَيْتُ** | **جَاءَتْ** تَجْرِي |
| وَإِذَا **جَاءَ** مِنْ سَفَرٍ | **اشْتَرَى** لِي هَدِيَّةً |
| وَإِذَا **مَرِضْتُ** أَوْ **سَقَطْتُ** | **طَارَ** نَوْمُهُ |

Also **إِنْ** (conditional for uncertain events):
- **إِنْ شَاءَ اللهُ** — "if God wills"

And **لَمَّا** (temporal, "when"):
- **وَلَمَّا دَخَلْتُ** فِي السَّنَةِ الخَامِسَةِ — "and when I entered the fifth year"

### 4. Circumstantial Accusative (الحال)

| Arabic | Translation | Analysis |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| صَغِيراً ضَعِيفاً | small and weak | حال from the subject of وُلِدتُّ |
| هَمّاً وَحُزْناً | in worry and grief | حال (or تمييز) describing سَهِرَ — manner of staying awake |
| صَغِيراً | when I was small | حال in رَبَّيَانِي صَغِيراً (Quran 17:24) |
| وَأَنَا عَلَى فِرَاشِي | while I was on my bed | جملة اسمية في محل نصب حال |

### 5. Exclamatory كَمْ (كم الخبرية)

| Arabic | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| فَكَمْ **سَهِرَتِ** اللَّيَالِيَ | How many nights she stayed up! |
| وَكَمْ **تَعِبَتْ** فِي النَّهَارِ | And how much she toiled during the day! |

This is **كم الخبرية** (exclamatory كم), not interrogative. It expresses a large, unspecified quantity. The noun after it (الليالي) is in the accusative as a تمييز (specification).

### 7. Lām of Purpose (لام التعليل)

| Arabic | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| لِنَفْسِي | for my sake — لام الجر (preposition of benefit) |
| لِرَاحَتِي | for my comfort |
| لِمَكَانِي مِنْ أَبِي | because of my position with my father — لام التعليل (causative) |
| لِيُتَوَّجَ وَالِدَايَ | so that my parents may be crowned — لام التعليل + فعل مضارع منصوب |

### 8. The Optative يَا لَيْتَ

> يَا لَيْتَ لَنَا مِنَ الأَوْلَادِ مِثْلَ مَا أُوتِيَ فُلَانٌ

- **يَا لَيْتَ** — "if only, would that" — particle of wishing (تمنٍّ)
- **لَنَا** — لِ + نَا — prepositional phrase, خبر مقدم (fronted predicate)
- **مِثْلَ مَا أُوتِيَ** — "the like of what was given" — comparative + مَا المصدرية + passive verb

### 9. Quranic Reference

> رَبِّ ارْحَمْهُمَا كَمَا رَبَّيَانِي صَغِيراً

This is from **Surah al-Isra' (17:24)**. Grammatically:
- **رَبِّ** — vocative (منادى مضاف), the ياء of يَا رَبِّي is dropped
- **ارْحَمْهُمَا** — imperative (فعل أمر) + dual object pronoun (هُمَا)
- **كَمَا** — كَ (like) + مَا المصدرية (what = "just as")
- **رَبَّيَانِي** — past tense dual (هُمَا) + object pronoun (نِي) — "they both raised me"
- **صَغِيراً** — حال (state when the raising occurred — "when I was small")

### 10. Metaphorical Expressions (المجاز)

| Arabic | Literal | Figurative |
|--------|---------|------------|
| طَارَ عَنْهَا النَّوْمُ | "sleep flew from her" | She could not sleep at all |
| طَارَ نَوْمُهُ | "his sleep flew" | He could not sleep |
| كُنْتُ لَهَا شُغْلاً | "I was an occupation for her" | I was her sole concern |
| عَلَى رُؤُوسِ الأَشْهَادِ | "upon the heads of witnesses" | Publicly, before all witnesses |

## Rhetorical Devices (البلاغة)

1. **Rhetorical Question (استفهام إنكاري):**
   - هَلْ يُمْكِنُ أَنْ أُجَازِيَهُمَا بِمَالٍ؟ — "Can I repay them with wealth?" (answer: no)

2. **Self-answering (أسلوب الإضراب):**
   - كَلَّا! ... نَعَمْ! — The speaker negates one idea and affirms another

3. **Gradation (التدرج):**
   - The text moves from infancy → childhood → aspiration → Day of Judgment, building emotional intensity

4. **Parallelism (المقابلة):**
   - نَسِيَتْ نَفْسَهَا لِنَفْسِي / هَجَرَتْ رَاحَتَهَا لِرَاحَتِي — parallel structures contrasting mother's sacrifice

5. **Repetition for emphasis (التكرار):**
   - وَسَأَجْتَهِدُ is repeated three times in the final paragraphs, emphasizing determination
