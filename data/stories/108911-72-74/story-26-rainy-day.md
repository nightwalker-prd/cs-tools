# Story 26: يَوْمٌ مَطِيرٌ — A Rainy Day

**Source:** Pages 72–74

---

## Arabic Text and English Translation

### Paragraph 1

> نَزَلَ الْمَطَرُ فِي اللَّيْلِ وَسَالَتِ الطُّرُقُ وَالشَّوَارِعُ وَنَشَأَ وَحْلٌ كَثِيرٌ زَلَقَ بِهِ النَّاسُ وَتَوَسَّخَتِ الثِّيَابُ، وَإِذَا سَارَتْ سَيَّارَةٌ تَطَايَرَ الْمَاءُ.

The rain fell at night and the roads and streets flowed (with water). Much mud formed in which people slipped, and clothes got dirty. Whenever a car drove by, the water splashed.

### Paragraph 2

> انْقَطَعَ الْمَطَرُ فِي الصَّبَاحِ وَأَمِنَ النَّاسُ، خَرَجُوا يَمْشُونَ عَلَى الشَّوَارِعِ وَقَدْ تَوَسَّخَتْ ثِيَابُهُمْ بِالْوَحْلِ، وَزَلَقَ بَعْضُ النَّاسِ عَلَى الشَّارِعِ وَسَقَطَ فِي الْوَحْلِ وَضَحِكَ النَّاسُ وَضَحِكَ الرَّجُلُ وَتَوَسَّخَتْ ثِيَابُهُ جِدًّا.

The rain stopped in the morning and people felt safe. They went out walking on the streets, their clothes already dirty with mud. Some people slipped on the street and fell in the mud. People laughed, and the man (who fell) laughed too, and his clothes got very dirty.

### Paragraph 3

> وَكَانَ النَّاسُ يَمْشُونَ مُطْمَئِنِّينَ إِذْ جَاءَ الْمَطَرُ عَلَى غَفْلَةٍ فَابْتَلَّتِ الثِّيَابُ، وَكَانَ بَعْضُ الْعُقَلَاءِ قَدْ أَخَذُوا مَعَهُمُ الْمَطَرِيَّاتِ فَنَشَرُوهَا، وَكُنْتُ تَرَكْتُ مَطَرِيَّتِي فِي الْبَيْتِ وَظَنَنْتُ أَنَّ الْمَطَرَ قَدِ انْقَطَعَ فَتَأَسَّفْتُ جِدًّا وَجَرَيْتُ وَرَجَعْتُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ وَقَدِ ابْتَلَّتْ ثِيَابِي.

The people were walking at ease when the rain came suddenly, and clothes got wet. Some wise people had taken their umbrellas with them and opened them. I had left my umbrella at home, thinking the rain had stopped. So I was very sorry and I ran and returned home with my clothes soaked.

### Paragraph 4

> وَلَمْ تَطْلُعِ الشَّمْسُ طُولَ النَّهَارِ وَلَمْ يَزَلْ فِي السَّمَاءِ غَيْمٌ وَلَمْ يَخْرُجْ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ إِلَّا ذُو حَاجَةٍ، وَسَئِمْتُ الْجُلُوسَ فِي الْبَيْتِ فَخَرَجْتُ فِي الْعَصْرِ وَأَخَذْتُ الْمَطَرِيَّةَ مَعِي وَذَهَبْتُ إِلَى صَدِيقِي مَسْعُودٍ، فَوَجَدْتُهُ يُطَالِعُ كِتَابًا.

The sun did not come out all day, and there were still clouds in the sky, and no one left the house except those with a need. I got bored of sitting at home, so I went out in the afternoon and took my umbrella with me and went to my friend Mas'ud. I found him reading a book.

### Paragraph 5 — Dialogue

> قُلْتُ لَهُ: أَلَا تَخْرُجُ يَا أَخِي نَتَنَزَّهَ وَنَمْشِيَ قَلِيلًا، أَمَا سَئِمْتَ الْجُلُوسَ؟

I said to him: "Won't you come out, my brother, so we can take a walk and stroll a little? Haven't you gotten tired of sitting?"

> قَالَ مَسْعُودٌ: أَلَا تَرَى إِلَى السَّمَاءِ وَإِلَى الْأَرْضِ؟ أَلَا تَرَى الْوَحْلَ؟ هَذَا لَيْسَ يَوْمَ الْخُرُوجِ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ، فَتَفَضَّلْ وَتَعَشَّ مَعِي.

Mas'ud said: "Don't you see the sky and the ground? Don't you see the mud? This is not a day for going out. So please, come in and have dinner with me."

> قُلْتُ: أَمَّا الْجُلُوسُ فَنَعَمْ! وَأَمَّا الْعَشَاءُ فَلَا، فَإِنِّي أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَرْجِعَ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ سَرِيعًا فَلَسْنَا نَدْرِي مَتَى تُمْطِرُ السَّمَاءُ.

I said: "As for sitting, yes! But as for dinner, no — for I want to return home quickly, since we don't know when it will rain again."

### Paragraph 6

> وَجَلَسْتُ مَعَ صَدِيقِي مَسْعُودٍ قَلِيلًا، ثُمَّ سَئِمْتُ الْجُلُوسَ وَالْغَيْمَ، فَاسْتَأْذَنْتُهُ وَخَرَجْتُ إِلَى الشَّارِعِ وَجَاءَتْ سَيَّارَةٌ فَمَرَّتْ بِي وَتَطَايَرَ الْمَاءُ وَالْوَحْلُ وَتَوَسَّخَتْ ثِيَابِي، وَمَشَيْتُ قَلِيلًا وَرَجَعْتُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ، وَمَا دَخَلْتُ الْبَيْتَ وَمَا وَضَعْتُ الْمَطَرِيَّةَ حَتَّى جَاءَ الْمَطَرُ، فَحَمِدْتُ اللَّهَ تَعَالَى أَنْ وَصَلْتُ إِلَى الْبَيْتِ.

I sat with my friend Mas'ud for a while, then I got bored of sitting and the clouds. So I asked his leave and went out to the street. A car came and passed by me — the water and mud splashed and my clothes got dirty. I walked a little and returned home. I had barely entered the house and put down the umbrella when the rain came, so I praised Allah the Most High that I had reached home.

### Paragraph 7

> وَتَعَشَّيْتُ وَصَلَّيْتُ الْعِشَاءَ وَنِمْتُ وَمَا عَلِمْتُ مَتَى انْقَطَعَ الْمَطَرُ.

I had dinner and prayed the evening prayer and slept, and I did not know when the rain stopped.

### Paragraph 8

> وَكَانَتِ الْأَمْطَارُ فِي هَذِهِ السَّنَةِ كَثِيرَةً وَقَدْ فَاضَتْ أَنْهَارٌ وَجَاءَ السَّيْلُ وَتَهَدَّمَتْ بُيُوتٌ كَثِيرَةٌ.

The rains were plentiful that year. Rivers had overflowed, floods came, and many houses were destroyed.

## Grammatical Analysis

### 1. إِذَا + Past Tense (Conditional: "whenever")

> وَإِذَا سَارَتْ سَيَّارَةٌ تَطَايَرَ الْمَاءُ

**Structure:** إِذَا + فعل ماضٍ (condition) + فعل ماضٍ (result)

Here إِذَا with two past-tense verbs expresses a **repeated/habitual condition** in the past: "Whenever a car drove by, the water would splash." This is distinct from إِنْ (hypothetical) — إِذَا implies the event actually occurred repeatedly.

### 2. إِذْ (Sudden/unexpected occurrence: "when suddenly")

> وَكَانَ النَّاسُ يَمْشُونَ مُطْمَئِنِّينَ إِذْ جَاءَ الْمَطَرُ عَلَى غَفْلَةٍ

**Structure:** كَانَ + مضارع (background) + إِذْ + ماضٍ (sudden event)

إِذْ here introduces an unexpected interruption to an ongoing state. The combination with عَلَى غَفْلَةٍ ("unawares") reinforces the surprise element.

### 3. لَمْ + مضارع مجزوم (Negation of the past)

> وَلَمْ تَطْلُعِ الشَّمْسُ طُولَ النَّهَارِ

**Structure:** لَمْ + فعل مضارع مجزوم

لَمْ negates the past using the jussive (مجزوم) form of the present tense verb. تَطْلُعْ is مجزوم with sukun (the kasra on the عين is for the meeting of two sukuns: تَطْلُعِ الشَّمْسُ).

### 4. لَمْ يَزَلْ (Still/continues — incomplete negation)

> وَلَمْ يَزَلْ فِي السَّمَاءِ غَيْمٌ

**Meaning:** "There were still clouds in the sky."

لَمْ يَزَلْ is one of the أفعال ناقصة (incomplete verbs like كان). It means "to still be / to continue being." The subject غَيْمٌ is مرفوع and the predicate is the prepositional phrase فِي السَّمَاءِ (fronted for emphasis).

### 5. إِلَّا (Exception/restriction — حَصْر)

> وَلَمْ يَخْرُجْ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ إِلَّا ذُو حَاجَةٍ

**Structure:** لَمْ + فعل + إِلَّا + فاعل

This is negation + exception = restriction (حَصْر). "No one left the house except those with a need." ذُو is from the الأسماء الخمسة (five nouns), مرفوع with واو as the فاعل.

### 6. أَلَا (Interrogative invitation: "Won't you...?")

> أَلَا تَخْرُجُ يَا أَخِي نَتَنَزَّهَ وَنَمْشِيَ قَلِيلًا

**Structure:** أَلَا + مضارع مرفوع

أَلَا (أ + لَا) is a gentle invitation or encouragement. The verb after it stays مرفوع (not مجزوم) because لا here is not the جازمة particle — it's لا النافية in a question that expects a positive answer. نَتَنَزَّهَ is مجزوم as جواب الطلب (response to a request/command).

### 7. أَمَّا ... فَـ (Topicalization: "As for... then...")

> أَمَّا الْجُلُوسُ فَنَعَمْ! وَأَمَّا الْعَشَاءُ فَلَا

**Structure:** أَمَّا + مبتدأ + فَـ + خبر

أَمَّا is a conditional particle of detail/specification (حرف تفصيل). The فاء after it is **obligatory** (واجبة). This construction elegantly splits a response into two contrasting parts: "As for sitting — yes! But as for dinner — no!"

### 8. قَدْ + Past Tense (Emphasis/completion)

> وَكَانَ بَعْضُ الْعُقَلَاءِ قَدْ أَخَذُوا مَعَهُمُ الْمَطَرِيَّاتِ

**Structure:** كَانَ + subject + قَدْ + فعل ماضٍ

قَدْ before a past-tense verb emphasizes that the action was **already completed** (past perfect sense). Combined with كَانَ, it creates a clear pluperfect: "had already taken."

### 9. وَقَدْ as حال (Circumstantial clause)

> خَرَجُوا يَمْشُونَ عَلَى الشَّوَارِعِ وَقَدْ تَوَسَّخَتْ ثِيَابُهُمْ بِالْوَحْلِ

**Structure:** main verb + وَقَدْ + فعل ماضٍ (حال)

The واو here is واو الحال (the waw of circumstance). The entire clause وَقَدْ تَوَسَّخَتْ ثِيَابُهُمْ is a جملة حالية describing the state of the subject during the main action: "while their clothes had [already] gotten dirty with mud."

### 10. مَا ... حَتَّى (Barely... when / No sooner... than)

> وَمَا دَخَلْتُ الْبَيْتَ وَمَا وَضَعْتُ الْمَطَرِيَّةَ حَتَّى جَاءَ الْمَطَرُ

**Structure:** مَا + فعل ماضٍ + حَتَّى + فعل ماضٍ

مَا here negates in a rhetorical/temporal sense: "I had barely entered... barely put down... when the rain came." The حَتَّى introduces the sudden follow-up event. This is an idiomatic construction expressing how quickly one event followed another.

### 11. لَيْسَ as Negation (Nominal sentence negation)

> هَذَا لَيْسَ يَوْمَ الْخُرُوجِ مِنَ الْبَيْتِ

**Structure:** مبتدأ + لَيْسَ + خبر لَيْسَ (منصوب)

لَيْسَ is a فعل ناقص (defective verb) that negates nominal sentences. هَذَا is the اسم لَيْسَ (or مبتدأ in some analyses), and يَوْمَ is the خبر لَيْسَ (منصوب with فتحة).

### 12. Form V Pattern (تَفَعَّلَ) — Reflexive/resultative

This text is rich in Form V verbs, all showing the **reflexive or resultative** meaning:

| Verb | Meaning | Reflexive sense |
|------|---------|----------------|
| تَوَسَّخَ | to get dirty | result of وَسَّخَ (to make dirty) |
| تَأَسَّفَ | to feel sorry | internal feeling from أَسَف |
| تَنَزَّهَ | to take a stroll | reflexive enjoyment |
| تَفَضَّلَ | please (come in) | reflexive of فَضَّلَ (to favor) |
| تَعَشَّى | to have dinner | reflexive of عَشَّى (to give dinner) |
| تَهَدَّمَ | to collapse | result of هَدَّمَ (to demolish) |

### 13. Form VII (اِنْفَعَلَ) — Passive/reflexive intransitive

> اِنْقَطَعَ الْمَطَرُ

From قَطَعَ (to cut). Form VII gives the intransitive/passive meaning: "to be cut off, to cease." The rain didn't cut anything — it itself stopped.

### 14. Form X (اِسْتَفْعَلَ) — Seeking/requesting

> فَاسْتَأْذَنْتُهُ

From أَذِنَ (to permit). Form X adds the meaning of **seeking**: "I sought his permission" = "I asked his leave."

### 15. Broken Plurals (جمع التكسير)

The text showcases several common broken plural patterns:

| Singular | Pattern | Plural | Pattern |
|----------|---------|--------|---------|
| طَرِيق | فَعِيل | طُرُق | فُعُل |
| شَارِع | فَاعِل | شَوَارِع | فَوَاعِل |
| ثَوْب | فَعْل | ثِيَاب | فِعَال |
| بَيْت | فَعْل | بُيُوت | فُعُول |
| نَهْر | فَعْل | أَنْهَار | أَفْعَال |
| عَاقِل | فَاعِل | عُقَلَاء | فُعَلَاء |

### 16. كَانَ + يَفْعَلُ (Past continuous / habitual past)

> وَكَانَ النَّاسُ يَمْشُونَ مُطْمَئِنِّينَ

**Structure:** كَانَ + اسم كان (مرفوع) + خبر كان (جملة فعلية)

كَانَ + present tense verb expresses continuous or habitual action in the past: "The people were walking." مُطْمَئِنِّينَ is a حال (circumstantial accusative) describing the state of الناس — in the منصوب plural form with ياء.

## Rhetorical and Stylistic Notes

1. **Repetition for emphasis:** The verb تَوَسَّخَ (to get dirty) appears **5 times**, reinforcing the messy, muddy reality of the day. Similarly, سَئِمَ (to get bored) appears **3 times**, conveying the narrator's restlessness.

2. **Humor:** The scene of a man slipping in mud, everyone laughing, and the man himself laughing — then noting his clothes got "very dirty" (جِدًّا) — is a gentle comic moment.

3. **Dramatic irony:** The narrator leaves his umbrella at home thinking the rain has stopped, only for it to rain again. Later, he barely reaches home before the next downpour — a recurring pattern of near-misses with the rain.

4. **أَمَّا...فَـ dialogue structure:** The narrator's split response ("As for sitting, yes! As for dinner, no!") is a classic Arabic rhetorical device showing balanced, decisive speech.

5. **Closure and scale shift:** The story ends by zooming out from the narrator's personal day to the broader consequences — rivers overflowing, floods, and houses destroyed — elevating the narrative from anecdote to something more serious.
