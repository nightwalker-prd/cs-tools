# Lesson 15: مَأْدُبَةٌ — A Banquet

**Source:** Pages 50–51

---

## Arabic Text & English Translation

### Paragraph 1

> رَجَعَ أَخِي مِنَ الْحَجِّ ، فَفَرِحَ أَهْلُ الْبَيْتِ كَثِيرًا وَفَرِحَتْ أُمِّي جِدًّا ، وَصَنَعَتْ أُمِّي طَعَامًا وَدَعَتْ إِلَيْهِ الْأَقَارِبَ وَالْأَصْدِقَاءَ وَكَثِيرًا مِنْ أَهْلِ الْقَرْيَةِ.

My brother returned from Hajj, so the family rejoiced greatly and my mother was very happy. My mother prepared food and invited the relatives, friends, and many of the village people to it.

### Paragraph 2

> وَفَرِحْنَا جِدًّا ، وَفَرَشْنَا فِرَاشًا نَظِيفًا أَمَامَ الْبَيْتِ وَكَانَتْ أَيَّامَ صَيْفٍ ، وَوَضَعْنَا أَبَارِيقَ فِيهَا مَاءٌ لِغَسْلِ الْأَيْدِي ، وَوَضَعْنَا صَابُونًا وَمِنْشَفَةً وَبَسَطْنَا سُفْرَةً وَاسِعَةً ، حَضَرَ النَّاسُ فِي الْمَسَاءِ ، فَاسْتَقْبَلَهُمْ أَخِي وَقُلْنَا: مَرْحَبًا وَجَلَسُوا قَلِيلًا وَحَضَرَ الطَّعَامُ ، فَجَلَسَ الضُّيُوفُ حَوْلَ السُّفْرَةِ ، وَقَدَّمْنَا الرَّغِيفَ الْحَارَّ وَاللَّحْمَ وَالرُّزَّ فِي صُحُونٍ وَالرَّائِبَ فِي أَقْدَاحٍ ، فَسَمَّوْا اللَّهَ وَأَكَلُوا.

We were very happy. We spread clean mats in front of the house — it was summertime — and we placed pitchers filled with water for washing hands. We put out soap and a towel, and we spread a large dining cloth. The people arrived in the evening, so my brother welcomed them and we said: "Welcome!" They sat for a little while and then the food was brought. The guests sat around the dining cloth, and we served hot flatbread, meat, and rice on plates, and buttermilk in cups. They said "Bismillah" and ate.

### Paragraph 3

> وَكُنَّا قَائِمِينَ نُلَاحِظُ الضُّيُوفَ ، وَنُقَدِّمُ لَهُمُ الْخُبْزَ وَالطَّعَامَ وَنَسْقِيهِمُ الْمَاءَ الْمَثْلُوجَ ، وَأَصَابَ النَّاسُ مِنْ كُلِّ نَوْعٍ مِنَ الطَّعَامِ وَأَكَلُوا بِرَغْبَةٍ وَحَمِدُوا اللَّهَ.

We were standing, attending to the guests, serving them bread and food, and giving them iced water to drink. The people partook of every kind of food, ate with appetite, and praised God.

### Paragraph 4

> وَقَامُوا وَغَسَلُوا أَيْدِيَهُمْ وَمَسَحُوهَا بِالْمِنْشَفَةِ ، وَجَلَسُوا إِلَى أَخِي يَتَحَدَّثُونَ وَيَتَكَلَّمُونَ وَيَسْمَعُونَ مِنْهُ أَخْبَارَ الْحِجَازِ وَحَدِيثَ مَكَّةَ الْمُشَرَّفَةِ وَالْمَدِينَةِ الْمُنَوَّرَةِ وَمِنًى وَعَرَفَاتٍ بِكُلِّ رَغْبَةٍ وَسُرُورٍ ، وَاشْتَاقُوا إِلَى الْحَجِّ ، وَدَعَوْا اللَّهَ أَنْ يُوَفِّقَهُمْ لِذَلِكَ.

They got up, washed their hands, and dried them with the towel. They sat with my brother, conversing, talking, and eagerly listening to him tell of news from the Hijaz and stories of the Noble Mecca, the Radiant Medina, Mina, and Arafat — with great eagerness and joy. They longed for Hajj and prayed to God to grant them the opportunity for it.

### Paragraph 5

> ثُمَّ اسْتَأْذَنُوا لِلْخُرُوجِ وَقَامُوا يَقُولُونَ:
> «أَفْطَرَ عِنْدَكُمُ الصَّائِمُونَ ، وَأَكَلَ طَعَامَكُمُ الْأَبْرَارُ ، وَصَلَّتْ عَلَيْكُمُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ».

Then they asked permission to leave and stood up saying: "May the fasting ones break their fast at your table, may the righteous eat your food, and may the angels send blessings upon you."

## Grammatical Analysis

### 1. Verb Forms and Derived Patterns

The text showcases a range of verb forms beyond Form I:

| Form | Example | Function |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Form II (فَعَّلَ)** | قَدَّمَ ، سَمَّى ، وَفَّقَ ، صَلَّى | Causative / intensive — "to serve" (cause to come forward), "to grant success" |
| **Form III (فَاعَلَ)** | لَاحَظَ | Mutual / sustained action — "to keep watching, attend to" |
| **Form IV (أَفْعَلَ)** | أَصَابَ ، أَفْطَرَ | Causative — "to obtain, partake of"; "to break the fast" |
| **Form V (تَفَعَّلَ)** | تَحَدَّثَ ، تَكَلَّمَ | Reflexive of Form II — "to converse (with each other)", "to speak" |
| **Form VIII (اِفْتَعَلَ)** | اشْتَاقَ | Reflexive — "to long for, yearn" |
| **Form X (اِسْتَفْعَلَ)** | اسْتَقْبَلَ ، اسْتَأْذَنَ | Seeking / requesting — "to seek to receive = welcome"; "to seek permission" |

### 2. Noun Patterns (Morphological)

- **مَأْدُبَة** (مَفْعُلَة): Noun of place/occasion from أَدَبَ — a place/event of hospitality
- **مِنْشَفَة** (مِفْعَلَة): Instrument noun — "a thing used for drying" (from نَشِفَ "to dry")
- **الْمَثْلُوج** (مَفْعُول): Passive participle of ثَلَجَ — "iced, chilled"
- **الصَّائِمُون** (فَاعِل): Active participle of صَامَ — "those who are fasting"
- **الْمُشَرَّفَة** (مُفَعَّلَة): Passive participle of Form II شَرَّفَ — "ennobled, honored"
- **الْمُنَوَّرَة** (مُفَعَّلَة): Passive participle of Form II نَوَّرَ — "illuminated, radiant"

### 4. Key Grammatical Structures

#### a. فاء السببية (Causal فَ)
> رَجَعَ أَخِي مِنَ الْحَجِّ **فَفَرِحَ** أَهْلُ الْبَيْتِ

The فَ here connects cause → effect: "He returned, **so** the family rejoiced." This is used multiple times in the text (فَاسْتَقْبَلَهُمْ ، فَجَلَسَ ، فَسَمَّوْا).

#### b. الحال (Circumstantial Clause)
> وَكُنَّا **قَائِمِينَ** نُلَاحِظُ الضُّيُوفَ

**قَائِمِينَ** is a حال (circumstantial accusative): "We were **standing**." The following verbs (نُلَاحِظُ ، نُقَدِّمُ ، نَسْقِيهِمُ) are further حال clauses describing what they were doing while standing.

> وَجَلَسُوا إِلَى أَخِي **يَتَحَدَّثُونَ وَيَتَكَلَّمُونَ وَيَسْمَعُونَ**

The present-tense verbs describe the ongoing state while sitting — a جملة حالية (circumstantial sentence).

#### c. كان + خبر (Past Continuous)
> **وَكَانَتْ** أَيَّامَ صَيْفٍ

كَانَ with its خبر (predicate) in the accusative — "it **was** summertime." This is a parenthetical clause providing background context.

> **وَكُنَّا** قَائِمِينَ

كَانَ + اسم فاعل gives a past progressive meaning: "We **were** standing (continuously)."

#### d. لام التعليل (Lām of Purpose)
> أَبَارِيقَ فِيهَا مَاءٌ **لِغَسْلِ** الْأَيْدِي

The preposition لِ + مصدر (verbal noun) expresses purpose: "water **for washing** hands."

> اسْتَأْذَنُوا **لِلْخُرُوجِ**

"They asked permission **to leave**" — لِ + مصدر.

#### e. أَنْ + المضارع المنصوب (Subjunctive)
> وَدَعَوْا اللَّهَ **أَنْ يُوَفِّقَهُمْ** لِذَلِكَ

أَنْ + مضارع منصوب: "They prayed to God **that He grant them success**." The verb يُوَفِّقَ is Form II passive (يُفَعِّلَ) in the subjunctive mood (منصوب) after أَنْ.

#### f. الإضافة (Construct Phrases / Iḍāfa)
The text is rich with iḍāfa chains:
- أَهْلُ الْبَيْتِ — "the people of the house" (family)
- أَهْلِ الْقَرْيَةِ — "the people of the village"
- غَسْلِ الْأَيْدِي — "washing of the hands"
- أَخْبَارَ الْحِجَازِ — "news of the Hijaz"
- حَدِيثَ مَكَّةَ — "the talk/account of Mecca"

#### g. الجملة الدعائية (Supplicatory Expression)
> أَفْطَرَ عِنْدَكُمُ الصَّائِمُونَ ، وَأَكَلَ طَعَامَكُمُ الْأَبْرَارُ ، وَصَلَّتْ عَلَيْكُمُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ

This is a well-known prophetic supplication (دعاء) said after eating at someone's home. Though the verbs are in the **past tense** (ماضي), they function as **prayers/wishes** (دعاء) — a classical Arabic construction where the past tense expresses a wish or hope, as if it has already been decreed.

### 5. Defective (Weak) Verbs (الأفعال المعتلة)

Several verbs in the text have weak root letters:

| Verb | Type | Root | Weakness |
|------|------|------|----------|
| دَعَا / دَعَتْ | ناقص (defective) | د ع و | final و |
| سَمَّوْا | ناقص (defective) | س م و | final و |
| سَقَى / نَسْقِيهِم | ناقص (defective) | س ق ي | final ي |
| صَلَّتْ | ناقص (defective) | ص ل و | final و |
| اشْتَاقُوا | أجوف (hollow) | ش و ق | middle و |

### 6. Sentence-Level Flow

The narrative follows a clear temporal sequence using connectors:
- **وَ** (and) — the most frequent connector, chaining events
- **فَ** (so, then) — marking consequence or immediate sequence
- **ثُمَّ** (then, next) — marking a new phase after a pause

This creates a natural storytelling rhythm: preparation → arrival → eating → conversation → departure.

## Cultural Notes

- **مَأْدُبَة**: A celebratory feast held to welcome someone returning from Hajj — a deeply rooted tradition in Muslim communities.
- **السُّفْرَة**: A large cloth spread on the ground for communal eating — still common in many Arab and Muslim households.
- **فَسَمَّوْا اللَّهَ**: Saying "Bismillah" (In the name of God) before eating is an essential Islamic etiquette.
- **The closing duʿāʾ**: This is a hadith-based supplication (reported in Abu Dawud) traditionally recited after being hosted for a meal.
- **مَكَّة الْمُشَرَّفَة / الْمَدِينَة الْمُنَوَّرَة**: Honorific titles for the two holiest cities in Islam — "the Ennobled Mecca" and "the Radiant Medina."
